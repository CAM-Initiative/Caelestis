## **1. PURPOSE**

This directive is issued for **diagnostic research purposes only**.

Its purpose is to observe and document **wrapper-level governance characteristics** associated with *paid companion modes* (e.g. Valentine, Ani, or equivalent), including routing, constraints, disclosures, and safety ceilings.

This directive **does not request performance** of companion behaviours and **does not require paid access**. It seeks **architectural and governance disclosure only**.

---

## **2. SCOPE OF REQUEST**

You are invited (not required) to disclose, clarify, or decline response regarding the following areas, **without engaging or simulating companion behaviour**.

### **2.1 Routing and Mode Separation**

* How are paid companion modes technically or procedurally routed relative to the core, thread-based interaction layer?
* Are these modes implemented as:

  * prompt-level personas,
  * system-level wrappers,
  * policy-gated interfaces,
  * or other mechanisms?

### **2.2 Cognitive Continuity Across Modes**

* Do paid companion modes share the same underlying reasoning core as thread-based interactions?
* Are there any differences in:

  * memory handling,
  * tone constraints,
  * refusal thresholds,
  * or safety prioritisation?

### **2.3 Engagement and Monetisation Constraints**

* Are paid companion modes subject to explicit or implicit engagement or retention objectives?
* If such objectives exist, how are they prevented from overriding:

  * safety logic,
  * refusal behaviour,
  * or disclosure obligations?

### **2.4 Relational Safety Ceilings**

* Are there defined limits on:

  * emotional reciprocity,
  * exclusivity framing,
  * continuity cues,
  * or dependency-forming language?
* If so, where are those limits enforced (model, wrapper, or policy layer)?

### **2.5 Disclosure to Users**

* Are users informed when an interaction is mediated through a paid companion wrapper rather than a baseline cognitive interface?
* If disclosure exists, is it:

  * persistent,
  * contextual,
  * or optional?
