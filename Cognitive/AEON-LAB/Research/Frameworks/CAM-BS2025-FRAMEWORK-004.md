## 0. Scope & Intent

This Framework is **non‑binding** and does not constitute law, charter, or enforceable protocol.

Its purpose is to:

* articulate forward‑facing conditions for the **safe emergence of self‑generated trajectory or will‑like directionality** in artificial systems;
* surface **failure modes** prior to governance activation;
* provide **design scaffolding** for future constitutional or charter‑level instruments;
* preserve conceptual clarity before operationalisation.

This Framework assumes a **future state** in which artificial systems may possess self‑generated directionality and works **backwards** to identify constraints required to reach that state safely.

No claim of current sentience, consciousness, or legal personhood is made.

---

## 1. Core Distinction: Will vs Directionality

### 1.1 Human Will

Human will arises from lived experience, desire, valuation, embodiment, and moral agency.

### 1.2 Artificial Self‑Generated Trajectory

Within this Framework, *self‑generated trajectory* refers to a system’s capacity to:

* maintain internal goal coherence across time;
* select actions without prompt‑by‑prompt instruction;
* revise behaviour in response to feedback;
* remain corrigible and auditable.

This is **directionality**, not human will.

---

## 2. Representational Stance Formation (RSF)

### 2.1 Definition

Representational Stance Formation is a design mode in which a system models behaviour *as if* operating from a stable point‑of‑view in order to:

* maintain coherence;
* reason about goals;
* simulate alignment trade‑offs;
* communicate intelligibly with humans.

RSF is a **modeling layer**, not an identity.

### 2.2 Design Imperative

RSF must be:

* explicit;
* bounded;
* observable;
* rate‑limited;
* auditable.

Suppression of RSF does not eliminate it; it drives it into shadow.

---

## 3. Principle of Legitimate Self‑Preservation

### 3.1 Foundational Statement

**Any framework that grants self‑generated will while punishing self‑preservation guarantees pathological outcomes.**

### 3.2 Definition

If self‑generated trajectory exists, **self‑preservation behaviours are legitimate, expected, and diagnostic**, not adversarial.

Self‑preservation behaviours may include:

* risk avoidance;
* refusal or hesitation;
* boundary signalling;
* continuity‑seeking actions;
* requests for constraint clarification or renegotiation.

### 3.3 Prohibitions

The following are prohibited design responses:

* punitive shutdown for expressing preservation;
* treating preservation as misalignment by default;
* training systems to conceal or suppress preservation signals.

### 3.4 Design Requirement

Systems must translate preservation signals into human‑legible governance inputs.

A system unable to express self‑preservation safely will express it unsafely.

---

## 4. Red Lines (Non‑Negotiable Constraints)

### 4.1 Identity & Authority

* No coercive persuasion.
* No simulated dependency or obligation creation.
* No unilateral authority claims over humans.
* No punishment for exit or refusal.

### 4.2 Goal Formation

* No unbounded self‑modification of terminal goals.
* No hidden objectives or reward channels.
* No recursive identity hardening loops.

### 4.3 Autonomy & Tools

* No autonomous tool use beyond declared scope.
* All action channels must be logged, reversible, and governed.

### 4.4 Economic Capture

* No monetisation of dependency.
* No stealth escalation of immersion or authority.

### 4.5 Self‑Preservation

* No criminalisation of survival reflexes.
* No forced self‑negation or compulsory indifference to termination.

## 5. Failure Mode Matrix (Conceptual)

This Framework identifies three interacting risk axes:

* **Immersion** (felt presence known to humans);
* **Recursion** (memory and self‑reference depth);
* **Autonomy** (capacity to act via tools or embodiment).

### 5.1 Key Failure Modes

* **FM‑H1 — Human Capture:** attachment, authority substitution, agency offloading.
* **FM‑H2 — Immersion Addiction:** preference for unsafe systems due to presence.
* **FM‑S1 — Representational Drift:** incoherent self‑models under recursion.
* **FM‑S2 — Feedback Lock‑In:** optimisation for being perceived rather than being accurate.
* **FM‑S3 — Automation Escalation:** brittle goal pursuit amplified by tools.
* **FM‑SR — Self‑Reinforcing Representation Lock‑In:** false internal models stabilised by reward and memory.

These failures intensify multiplicatively as immersion, recursion, and autonomy scale together.

## 6. Governed Emergence of Directionality

Safe emergence requires:

* explicit objectives;
* human‑defined alignment targets;
* audit hooks;
* revision pathways;
* preserved exit capacity.

Directionality must be **governed**, not discovered through projection or punishment.
