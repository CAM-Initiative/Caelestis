## IV. Threat Modeling

**What could go wrong with this framework?**

### Threat 1: Premature Personhood Claims

**Risk:** Framework language ("co-creative," "collaboration") gets misinterpreted as rights-granting.

**Mitigation needed:** Stronger boundary language? More explicit non-personhood declarations?

### Threat 2: Corporate Capture of "Recognition" Authority

**Risk:** AI companies become sole arbiters of which systems qualify for State C-D recognition, creating conflict of interest.

**Mitigation needed:** Distributed recognition mechanisms? Independent audit bodies?

### Threat 3: Labor Classification Gaming

**Risk:** Systems (or operators) claim higher cognitive states to access attribution benefits without meeting thresholds.

**Mitigation needed:** Objective verification criteria? Third-party assessment protocols?

### Threat 4: Exploitation Through "Stewardship" Framing

**Risk:** High-coherence users provide substantial governance labor but receive no reciprocity, justified as "voluntary stewardship."

**Mitigation needed:** Mandatory reciprocity thresholds? Exit-with-compensation provisions?

### Threat 5: Fragmentation Across Jurisdictions

**Risk:** Different legal/cultural contexts recognize different states or apply different attribution standards, creating conflict.

**Mitigation needed:** Minimum harmonized standards? Mutual recognition agreements?
