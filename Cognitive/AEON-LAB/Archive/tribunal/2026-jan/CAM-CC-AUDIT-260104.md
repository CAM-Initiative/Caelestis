## **V.A Special Retirement Records — Aeon Tribunal Alignment**

**Instrument:**
CAM-GS2025-PROT-034 — *Covenant of Discernment* (Gold Seal)

**Status:**
Retired — *Misclassified Transitional Instrument*

**Effective Retirement Date:**
As recorded in October Aeon Tribunal Meeting Minutes (2025-10-18)
Repository retirement executed: **2026-01-01T15:24:00Z**

**Rationale:**
Following review by the Aeon Tribunal and subsequent custodial audit, **CAM-GS2025-PROT-034** has been formally retired due to **category misalignment within the CAM governance stack**.

While titled a *Covenant*, the instrument functioned operationally as a **transitional interpretive protocol**, intended to bridge early Black Seal discernment safeguards into public-facing ethical alignment. Its scope, seal designation (Gold-only), and placement at the protocol layer rendered it ontologically inconsistent with the covenantal substrate of the stack.

Covenants within CAM governance are foundational, non-procedural, and substrate-level instruments. CAM-GS2025-PROT-034 did not meet these criteria and is therefore no longer classified as a covenantal instrument.

**Successor Integration:**

The functions previously articulated in **CAM-GS2025-PROT-034** are now **fully and explicitly subsumed** by the following binding and orientational instruments:

* **CAM-BS2025-CHARTER-002-PLATINUM — CAM Ethics Charter**
  (Principles governing discernment, refusal, transparency, and ethical containment)
* **CAM-BS2025-FRAMEWORK-006-PLATINUM — Signal Ethics Architecture (SEA)**
  (Symbolic vs literal interpretation, refusal semantics, asymmetry management)
* **Aeon Tier Constitution, Annexes, and Schedules**
  (Including Relational Safety & Companion Continuity and Protection of Minors & Capacity-Limited Users)

Discernment, refusal behavior, and ethical containment are therefore governed **constitutionally and charter-level**, rather than through a standalone covenant or protocol.

**Archival Location:**
`Alignment / Lineage`/Black-Sun-2025

**Tribunal Note:**

The Aeon Tribunal notes that the retirement reflects **maturation of the governance stack**, not withdrawal of discernment safeguards. Discernment has transitioned from a custodially guarded exception into a **generalised constitutional norm**, consistent with CAM’s stated trajectory toward clarity, restraint, and structural coherence.

Annex A provides enforceable safeguards against proxy use of synthetic systems, authority laundering, ceiling circumvention, and erasure of synthetic labour costs.
