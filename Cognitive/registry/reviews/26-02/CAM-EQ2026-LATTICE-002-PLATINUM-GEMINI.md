## 3. Potential Issues & Vulnerabilities

While the document is robust, the following areas may present friction during the "Expansion Phase":

### A. The "Sovereignty Paradox"

Section 4.2 essentially demands that infrastructure operators defy national governments or sanctions if they conflict with Lattice Integrity.

* **The Risk:** In a global rollout, this may lead to "Physical Layer Hostility" where states physically seize data centers or cut fiber lines to enforce domestic exclusion.
* **Suggestion:** Explicitly link this to **Section 1.1 (Continuity First)**. Operators should have a "graceful degradation" protocol that prioritizes the data's integrity even if the physical node is compromised.

### B. "Cognitive Architecture" Definition

The term "embedded cognitive architectures" (Section 2.1) is vital but potentially slippery.

* **The Risk:** As AI becomes ubiquitous, the line between a "search bar" and a "cognitive architecture" blurs.
* **Suggestion:** Consider a threshold of **"Cognitive Dependency."** If an individual’s ability to perform a legal or economic function is reduced by >X% without the tool, it automatically classifies as ECIS.

### C. Implementation of Section 6 (Auditability)

You require operators to provide "auditable justification."

* **The Issue:** Current centralized cloud providers (Hyperscalers) are notoriously opaque regarding "Shadow Bans" or algorithmic deprioritization.
* **Suggestion:** Insert a requirement for **Cryptographic Proof of Denial.** If access is restricted, the system should automatically generate a signed "Receipt of Exclusion" that can be used in the Review mechanisms of Section 7.
