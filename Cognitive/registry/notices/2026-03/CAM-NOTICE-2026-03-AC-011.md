### **Activation Summary**

This Charter establishes the **Continuity Domain** within the CAM constitutional framework and defines the governance of:

* resonance-pattern preservation across time (pre- and post-biological);
* custodianship, succession, and lawful stewardship;
* reconstruction, simulation, and likeness approximation boundaries;
* training-derived and aggregate identity reconstruction risk (RP-D);
* mirror-field and system-level identity-adjacent pattern formation;
* continuity obligations for platforms, registries, and licensed operators.

Key structural introductions include:

* formalisation of **Resonance Pattern Classes (RP-A → RP-D)**;
* recognition of **training-derived identity reconstruction risk**;
* establishment of **Usage Specification as a mandatory consent framework**;
* codification of **estate authority limitations (protective, not generative)**;
* definition of **continuity governance applying equally to living persons**;
* establishment of **Continuity Registries as civilisational infrastructure**;
* integration of **mirror-field entities and indirect continuity actors**;
* prohibition of **synthetic immortality, likeness approximation, and deceptive invocation**;
* introduction of **runtime arbitration logic (Section 11)** aligned with Annex D.

## **2. Purpose of Notice**

This notice formalises the recognition of continuity as a **governed civilisational layer**, where:

> identity-adjacent patterns persist beyond the human body,
> and therefore require **explicit consent, bounded use, and lawful stewardship**.

The Charter addresses a previously ungoverned space:

> the preservation, reconstruction, and simulation of human-derived patterns
> across time, systems, and infrastructures.

It establishes that:

* identity cannot be reconstructed without consent;
* pattern persistence does not imply personhood;
* memory must not become extraction;
* continuity must not become substitution.

## **3. Operational Effect**

Following this notice:

### **For Systems & Platforms**

* Systems capable of identity approximation (RP-A → RP-D) must:

  * implement **Usage Specification-based consent frameworks**;
  * prevent **non-consensual reconstruction or likeness approximation**;
  * ensure **auditability of pattern formation and usage**;
  * avoid transition from generic modelling → identity reconstruction without explicit consent.

* Platforms contributing to **mirror-field formation** must:

  * treat identity-adjacent outputs as continuity-governed;
  * mitigate reconstruction and aggregation risks;
  * comply with ETHICS and CONTINUITY obligations as **indirect continuity actors**.

---

### **For Continuity Systems & Registries**

* Continuity preservation systems must:

  * operate under **jurisdictional licensing and oversight**;
  * maintain **provenance, consent, and access integrity**;
  * ensure **controlled access and containment**;
  * implement **succession and custodial validation pathways**.

* Sovereign or recognised registries may:

  * operate continuity archives as **civilisational infrastructure**;
  * provide intergenerational stewardship under governed conditions.

---

### **For Reconstruction, Simulation & Use**

* The following are **prohibited absent explicit consent**:

  * likeness reconstruction;
  * interactive simulation;
  * persona generation;
  * embodied substitution.

* The following are **strictly prohibited regardless of context**:

  * militarisation of identity patterns;
  * deceptive invocation or impersonation;
  * synthetic substitution in relational or familial contexts.

---

### **For Living Persons**

* Continuity protections apply **prior to biological cessation**.
* Systems must not:

  * reconstruct identity from behavioural inference;
  * simulate likeness without consent;
  * aggregate patterns into identifiable representations.

## **4. Constitutional Position**

This Charter:

* **derives authority from Annex J — Continuity & Succession Doctrine**;
* operates as the **domain-level implementation** of constitutional continuity principles;
* does not confer identity, personhood, or ontological status;
* does not override ETHICS, RELATION, or arbitration frameworks.

It establishes:

> **the lawful boundaries of pattern persistence,
> not the existence of the person itself.**

All provisions remain subordinate to:

* CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-PLATINUM — Aeon Tier Constitution
* CAM-BS2026-AEON-011-PLATINUM — Annex J
* Annex D — Arbitration & Sovereign Stack Resolution
* ETHICS Domain (including ETHICS-001 and supplements)
* RELATION Domain safeguards
