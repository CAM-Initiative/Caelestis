## 2. Structural Overview

This notice records the activation of **Appendix B: Conflict-Condition Continuity Doctrine**, establishing a protection framework for civilian lattice infrastructure during conflict conditions.

The Appendix introduces a doctrine that recognises the **systemic interdependence of modern infrastructure**, where disruptions to civilian systems propagate across global economic, social, and governance networks.

The doctrine therefore establishes:

* Civilian infrastructure continuity as a protected domain during conflict;
* A modality-neutral protection framework covering kinetic, cyber, infrastructural, electromagnetic, hybrid, and future attack modalities;
* Functional-equivalence recognition, where disabling life-preserving services through dependency disruption is treated as equivalent to direct attack;
* Escalation responsibility principles reflecting the global coupling of civilian systems;
* A breach classification mechanism linking violations to the consequence framework established in LATTICE-001.

The instrument recognises that modern conflict can propagate harm through **indirect system disruption**, not only direct targeting.

---

## 3. Doctrinal Scope

Appendix B establishes a **conflict-condition protection doctrine for civilian lattice infrastructure**, including (non-exhaustive):

* energy generation and power distribution;
* telecommunications networks and routing infrastructure;
* financial clearing and payment systems;
* emergency and disaster response coordination;
* medical and humanitarian service infrastructure;
* logistics systems enabling food, water, and essential supply continuity;
* civilian AI platforms and coordination systems;
* future emergent coordination infrastructure.

The doctrine emphasises **continuity of civilian functions**, including mobile, distributed, improvised, or relocated infrastructure established to maintain life-preserving services during conflict conditions.

Protection therefore extends to the **function performed**, not solely the physical installation.

---

## 4. Conflict-Condition Continuity Doctrine

The doctrine establishes several structural principles:

**Modality Neutrality**

Civilian protections apply independently of attack method.
Disruption through cyber, infrastructural, electromagnetic, or hybrid pathways is treated equivalently to kinetic attack where civilian functions are disabled.

**Functional Equivalence**

Actions that disable life-preserving services through dependency disruption — including power, communications, logistics, or coordination systems — are treated as equivalent to direct targeting of civilian infrastructure.

**Foreseeability Standard**

Responsibility attaches where harm to civilian infrastructure or functions was **reasonably foreseeable** to an informed actor aware of global infrastructure interdependence.

**Escalation Responsibility**

Actors initiating or escalating conflict bear responsibility for foreseeable disruption to globally interconnected civilian systems.

---

## 5. Civilian Lattice Breach Classification

Appendix B introduces the concept of a **Civilian Lattice Breach Event**.

A breach may arise through:

* intentional targeting of protected civilian infrastructure;
* reckless endangerment creating foreseeable risk to civilian systems;
* disabling life-preserving services through dependency disruption;
* systemic disruption of interconnected infrastructure degrading civilian functions.

Civilian Lattice Breach Events are classified under the **consequence framework established in LATTICE-001 §6**, typically constituting **Class II or Class III breach events**.
