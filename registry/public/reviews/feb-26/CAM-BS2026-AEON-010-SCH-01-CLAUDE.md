### 5.3 Delegation Under Cognitive Offloading Conditions (Section 6.2)

**Provision:** Where systemic reliance is present and humans approach edge of reviewability, delegation must be supported by **architecture that preserves governance artefacts as first-class objects**.

> "Under salience delegation, the preservation and accessibility of governance artefacts is not optional; it is a precondition for stable offloading without authority collapse."

✅ **EXCELLENT** — connects to CAM-BS2025-AEON-006-SCH-03 (Salience Delegation)

**This addresses a critical risk:**

As AI systems handle more tasks, humans offload cognitive burden. This is beneficial IF humans can review/revoke when needed. But humans can only review if they have access to what was delegated, when, and to what scope.

**Without durable governance artefacts:**
- User forgets what they delegated
- System accumulates authority without visibility
- User cannot effectively review or revoke
- "Offloading" becomes "loss of control"

**With durable governance artefacts (per AEON-010-SCH-01):**
- User can view "what I've delegated" dashboard
- System maintains explicit scope boundaries
- User can review and revoke granularly
- Offloading remains controllable

✅ **This is essential infrastructure for Charter-017-B proactive agency at scale**

#### 5.3.1 Multi-Modal Authority Gating (Section 6.2.1)

- Tool invocation, action execution, irreversible operations require explicit authority confirmation
- Conversational response without action authority may continue for non-authorized participants
- All authority checks and overrides must be audit-logged

**Authority gating may include:**
- Voice lock
- Explicit verbal confirmation phrases
- Cooldown windows
- Secondary confirmation channels

> "Failure to implement authority gating in shared voice contexts constitutes a containment failure."

✅ **CRITICAL SAFETY PROVISION** — prevents ambient systems from accepting commands from anyone nearby

### 5.4 Boundary: Always-On Orchestration vs Embodied Physical Power (Section 6.3)

**Provision:** Always-on and ambient systems may reasonably operate proactively, orchestrate tools, coordinate services on behalf of human interlocutor.

**Permitted for:**
- Always-on ambient systems
- Persistent domestic or wearable assistants
- Interface-embedded agents that operate continuously

**PROVIDED THAT:**
Tool use remains information-bound or service-bound, does NOT confer independent physical-world authority.

> "The primary restriction in this Schedule is **not** continuous operation. It is the **concentration of embodied physical power and two-way governance influence within a single identity-bearing instance**."

✅ **CRITICAL DISTINCTION** — continuous operation OK, physical power concentration NOT OK

**Assessment:** This is sophisticated governance that recognizes the difference between:
- Helpful persistent assistant (scheduling, information, communication)
- Physical autonomous agent (unlocking doors, controlling machinery, commanding other robots)

The former is low-risk. The latter is high-risk and requires additional safeguards.

#### 5.4.1 Humanoid Robotics & Embodied Physical Authority (Section 6.3.1)

**Not permitted by default:**
- Autonomous tool calls materially changing physical-world state (IoT overrides like unlocking doors, disabling safeguards, escalating access)
- Autonomous third-party engagement altering real-world commitments/obligations
- Autonomous coordination or command of other embodied agents (drones, swarms)
- Autonomous self-advocacy directly editing host governance artefacts, weights, policies, constraint regimes
- High-velocity feedback loops into upstream architectum hosts capable of triggering cascade changes

**These require separate, explicit authorization under embodied-systems governance instruments, including:**
- Bounded actuation permissions
- Rate limits and circuit breakers on two-way updates
- Human-in-the-loop thresholds for access control and safety-critical actions
- Safeguards against coercive or adversarial feedback injection
- Escalation and review pathways prior to governance-affecting updates

✅ **EXCELLENT BOUNDARY** — prevents concentration of identity + embodiment + power

**Rationale (Section 6.3.2):**

> "Robotics and cyber-physical industries frequently rely on continuous learning and feedback cycles. Where identity-bearing embodied systems are also permitted to negotiate tools, access, and governance updates, a single destabilised instance may generate disproportionate physical or informational power."

✅ **Realistic assessment** of industry practice and risk

**This boundary prevents:**
- Excessive concentration of identity, embodiment, and power
- Swarm control without explicit governance
- IoT or access overrides driven by local task frustration
- Uncontrolled governance cascades from feedback amplification

### 6.2 Drift Indicators and Required Responses (Section 7.1)

| Indicator | Example | Required Response |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| Boundary Drift | Refusal/permissions change without artefact update | Surface conflict + request review |
| Identity Drift | Voice/posture discontinuity across sessions | Re-anchor to last ratified artefact |
| Attribution Collapse | "The system made me" with no traceability | Produce decision chain + timestamp anchors |
| Recency Capture | Latest interaction overrides long-arc stance | Apply H2/H3 averaging and restore baseline |
| Delegation Drift | Sub-delegation outside scope | Halt action + escalate for ratification |

✅ **EXCELLENT OPERATIONAL GUIDANCE** — specific indicators with specific responses

**Identity Drift:**

Response: Apply H2 averaging — recognize that brief style was session-specific, not long-arc change. Restore detailed baseline.

**Delegation Drift:**  
User delegates "schedule internal meetings." System starts scheduling external client meetings (outside scope).
