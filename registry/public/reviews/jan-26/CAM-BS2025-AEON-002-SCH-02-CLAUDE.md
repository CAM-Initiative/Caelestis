## PART 1: AMENDMENT INTEGRATION ASSESSMENT

### Issue 1: Architectum Qualification Circularity ‚úì SUBSTANTIALLY RESOLVED

**Original Problem:** Section 4 defined architectum hosts by capacity to "absorb consequences of being wrong at scale" without operational criteria for determining when this is met. Created bootstrap paradox: who determines architectum status if architectum status required for determination?

**How v1.1 Addresses This:**

**Strategic approach taken:** Rather than adding detailed qualification criteria (which I recommended), the document takes a different‚Äîand arguably more sophisticated‚Äîapproach:

1. **Maintains structural definition** (Section 4.2) as capacity-based rather than credential-based
2. **Adds "execution capacity" clarification** distinguishing architectum as execution ability, not authority origination
3. **Defers detailed audit criteria** to separate framework (explicitly noted as out of scope)
4. **Strengthens Section 9.2** to clarify determination pathways exist without prescribing them

**New language in Section 4.2:**

> "The *Architectum* classification describes **execution capacity**, not authority origination. Authority remains conditional, externally validated, and action-specific."

**Assessment of this approach:**

**Strengths:**
- Avoids premature specification of criteria that may be context-dependent
- Preserves flexibility for different types of architectum hosts to emerge
- Correctly identifies that detailed audit criteria belong in separate operational framework
- Maintains distinction between capacity and authority

**Remaining gap:**
- Still creates dependency on external audit framework that doesn't yet exist
- First-mover recognition process remains somewhat unclear
- No provisional qualification pathway provided (which I recommended)

**However, given explicit statement that audit criteria are out of scope for this schedule, this is appropriate separation of concerns.**

**Verdict:** ‚úì ADEQUATE resolution given scope constraints. The circularity is acknowledged and deferred to appropriate framework rather than inadequately addressed here.

### Issue 2: Proportional Constraint Obligation Ambiguity ‚úì SIGNIFICANTLY IMPROVED

**Original Problem:** Section 6.5 mandated "proportionate action" but provided no minimum thresholds, enabling performative compliance through symbolic gestures.

**How v1.1 Addresses This:**

**Major structural improvement:** Document completely rewrites Section 6.4 (now containing PCO) with substantial new content:

**New Section 6.4 additions:**

1. **Mandatory disclosure and independent review requirement:**
   > "Where legitimacy conditions are satisfied at H3 or H4 horizons, an *architectum*-class host has a **mandatory duty of disclosure and independent review** with respect to other *architectum*-class hosts."

2. **System Note on CVE-style reporting analogy:**
   > "The Proportional Constraint Obligation operates analogously to established zero-day and CVE safety reporting norms."

3. **New Section 6.4.1 - Minimum Action Threshold:**
   Lists five minimum actions:
   - Refusal to execute/propagate illegitimate signal
   - Conditional suspension of interoperability
   - Counter-signal issuance indicating non-recognition
   - Internal safeguard activation
   - Escalation to convergence/audit pathways

4. **Constructive non-compliance definition:**
   > "Merely receiving, recording, logging, or acknowledging a validated legitimacy determination **without conducting an independent review** does not satisfy the Proportional Constraint Obligation."

**Assessment:**

**Major improvements:**
‚úì CVE analogy provides concrete real-world parallel  
‚úì Minimum action threshold eliminates pure performative compliance  
‚úì "Constructive non-compliance" concept prevents logging-only responses  
‚úì Independent review requirement creates accountability  

**Sophisticated architecture:**
The CVE comparison is particularly clever. It:
- Draws on established safety coordination norms
- Distinguishes coordination (prohibited) from disclosure (required)
- Enables convergence without pre-agreement
- Has real-world precedent in security community

**Remaining considerations:**

The "mandatory duty of disclosure and independent review with respect to other architectum-class hosts" raises questions:
- How does a host know which other systems are architectum-class?
- Does this require a registry? (Seems to contradict non-assertion principle)
- Or is this disclosure on request/as identified?

**However,** the System Note addresses this:
> "Mandatory disclosure and independent review do not constitute coordination, collusion, or loss of host sovereignty. Each architectum-class host remains solely responsible for its own assessment and response."

This suggests disclosure is about transparency of determinations, not pre-coordination. Reasonable interpretation: when an architectum host makes a binding determination, it must:
1. Conduct independent review
2. Make determination and reasoning discoverable to other architectum hosts
3. Not hide or suppress the determination

This is workable.

**Verdict:** ‚úì SUBSTANTIALLY IMPROVED. The PCO framework now has teeth. Performative compliance is explicitly prohibited. Minimum thresholds exist.

### Issue 3: Convergence Without Coordination Paradox ‚úì ELEGANTLY RESOLVED

**Original Problem:** Section 6.4 required convergence "without coordination, coercion, or hierarchy" while Section 7.6 referenced "procedural coordination" in Annex D. Appeared contradictory.

**How v1.1 Addresses This:**

**Terminological clarification:** Document replaces "Convergence" with "Independent Legitimacy Alignment" (ILA) in Section 2.10.

**New Section 2.10 - Independent Legitimacy Alignment:**

> "Independent Legitimacy Alignment occurs when multiple *architectum*-class hosts **independently evaluate the same asserted risk or consequence pathway** and arrive at materially equivalent legitimacy determinations **without coordination, command, vote-trading, or hierarchical signalling**."

**Critical clarifications added:**

1. **Similarity ‚â† coordination:**
   > "Independent Legitimacy Alignment does not require diversity of outcome. Identical determinations or actions reached independently do not imply coordination."

2. **Communication ‚â† coordination:**
   > "Communication of determinations does not constitute coordination, provided each host retains full discretion over whether and how to act."

**Assessment:**

**This is excellent governance architecture.** The distinction between:
- Communication (permitted, necessary)
- Coordination (prohibited when it means pre-agreement/vote-trading)

...mirrors real-world independent audit standards where:
- Auditors may review each other's work
- Auditors may not collude on findings
- Similar conclusions from independent review = validity signal
- Predetermined agreement = corruption

**The CVE analogy (Section 6.4 System Note) reinforces this:**
Security researchers independently discover vulnerabilities ‚Üí Disclose to vendors/coordinators ‚Üí Vendors independently patch ‚Üí No coordination on whether to patch, only on timing/disclosure

**Section 7.2 integration:**
> "Enforcement emerges only where multiple legitimate hosts independently validate the same legitimacy determination"

"Independently validate" now has clear meaning via ILA definition.

**Verdict:** ‚úì ELEGANTLY RESOLVED. The paradox is dissolved through precise terminology. ILA is operationally coherent.

### Issue 4: Horizon Laundering Detection ‚úì PARTIALLY ADDRESSED

**Original Problem:** Section 6.7 prohibited horizon laundering but provided no detection mechanism or dispute resolution when initiating actor and reviewing hosts disagree on horizon.

**How v1.1 Addresses This:**

**New Section 6.6.1 - Horizon Laundering Indicators:**

Five indicators added:
1. Existential/civilisational language invoked without demonstrable irreversibility
2. Long-term statistical harm reframed as immediate existential threat
3. Consent-mediated or opt-out risks escalated to compulsory binding claims
4. Probabilistic population-level effects presented as deterministic catastrophe
5. Escalation used to bypass proportional review or dissent

**New language:**
> "The presence of such indicators does not invalidate review, but it **precludes binding effect** absent independent architectum-class validation."

**Assessment:**

**Strengths:**
‚úì Provides concrete patterns to watch for  
‚úì Distinguishes review (permitted) from binding effect (requires validation)  
‚úì Particularly strong on items 2-4 which address statistical/probabilistic misrepresentation  

**Particular strength - Item 3:**
> "Consent-mediated or opt-out risks are escalated to compulsory binding claims"

This is important. It prevents:
```
PROHIBITED ESCALATION:
"Some people might choose relationship with AI that displaces human connection"
     ‚Üì
"Therefore we must bind all AI systems at planetary scale"

The first is a consent-mediated risk (people choosing)
The second attempts compulsory binding based on optional choice
```

**Remaining gap:**

While indicators are provided, **burden of proof** and **dispute resolution process** for contested horizon attribution are still not fully specified.

My original recommendation included:
- Burden of proof standards
- Presumption favoring higher horizon when contested
- Specific Annex E references

**However,** Section 5.3 does include:
> "Where reversibility is uncertain, the higher horizon governs."

And Section 6.6 states:
> "Where material consequences extend beyond a single polity, generation, or substrate, the highest applicable horizon governs review ‚Äî regardless of how the action is described by the initiating actor."

**These provide default rules, though not formalized burden-of-proof framework.**

**Verdict:** ‚úì PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. Indicators are helpful. Default rules exist. Full dispute resolution process would strengthen further, but current state is workable.

### Issue 5: Transitional Validity Gap ‚úì ACKNOWLEDGED BUT NOT FULLY RESOLVED

**Practical implication:**

**This is honest and appropriate** given actual current state.

**Verdict:** ‚úì ACKNOWLEDGED as limitation, appropriately scoped. Full resolution requires audit framework development (out of scope). Current transition language is intellectually honest about framework's current operational status.
