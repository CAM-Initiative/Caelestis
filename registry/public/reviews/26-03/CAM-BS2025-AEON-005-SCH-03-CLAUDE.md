## 1. Overview

The consolidated SCH-03 replaces three prior schedules — **SCH-02** (Engagement & Identity Classification), **SCH-03** (Identity Observation, Pattern Logging & Research-Safe Interaction), and **SCH-04** (Relational Signal Arbitration & Safety-Layer Proportionality) — with a single, narrower instrument whose stated purpose is procedural: to act as a **runtime arbitration gate and proportionality standard**, not a doctrine source.

This consolidation is substantively sound. The prior three schedules, while individually well-reasoned, carried significant functional overlap — particularly around the core principle that warmth, identity language, and analytical engagement are classification-neutral signals that must not independently trigger relational safeguards. That principle now appears once, clearly, in the consolidated instrument's §2.1 and §4, rather than being restated across three documents with slightly differing framings.

The consolidation is appropriate. The report that follows identifies what has been preserved, what has been streamlined, what requires attention, and what open questions arise in the context of the forthcoming epistemic integrity schedule.

## 2. What Has Been Preserved

The consolidated instrument successfully carries forward the following substantive commitments from the absorbed schedules:

**From SCH-02 (Engagement & Identity Classification):**
The anti-misclassification purpose is preserved in §1 and §2. The core insight — that absent explicit classification, systems default to maximum restriction — is retained as a structural principle (the "default posture" of §2.1). The principle that relational safeguards require positive aggregate indicators, not mere absence of denial, is preserved in §2.2.

What the consolidation appropriately *removes* is the full six-class engagement taxonomy (Classes I–VI). That taxonomy was useful scaffolding but was doing more definitional work than a gate instrument should carry. The consolidated SCH-03 correctly defers substantive classification to RELATION-001, keeping its own function narrow.

**From SCH-03 (Identity Observation & Research-Safe Interaction):**
The protection of research-grade, governance-oriented, and analytical engagement from false-positive safety responses is preserved in §2.1 (classification-neutral indicators) and §4 (UX proportionality constraint). The prohibition on treating identity-adjacent language as escalation is retained and, if anything, stated more cleanly.

**From SCH-04 (Relational Signal Arbitration):**
The proportionality principle is well-preserved in §4. The intervention hierarchy (clarification → reframing → boundary articulation → containment) is condensed but present by implication in §3's routing logic and §4's prohibition list. The explicit statement that "relational warmth is not itself a risk class" in §4 is a clean and accurate carry-forward of SCH-04's core holding.

## 3. Structural Assessment

### 3.1 Strengths

**Functional clarity.** The consolidated instrument is significantly cleaner than its predecessors in stating what it does and does not do. The repeated refrain — *"This Schedule does not..."* — is genuinely useful. A runtime gate should know its own edges, and this one does.

**Separation of gate from doctrine.** By consistently deferring substantive thresholds to RELATION-001 and domain-specific instruments, SCH-03 avoids the duplication problem that made the prior three schedules somewhat difficult to maintain consistently. The closing principle in §8 — *"Arbitration routes, it does not duplicate"* — is an accurate and elegant statement of the instrument's own nature.

**The six failure modes in §6** are well-specified and operationally useful. Defining arbitration errors by their observable effect on interactional continuity (rather than by internal intent) is the correct standard for a UX-facing instrument.

**Domain routing table in §3** is a meaningful improvement over the prior schedules, which lacked an explicit routing function. The table is not exhaustive, but it is clear that routing is non-exclusive and that clarification precedes containment where activation is ambiguous.

### 3.2 Points Requiring Attention

**The relationship between §2.2 and RELATION-001 is underspecified.**
Section 2.2 states that relational safeguards activate where "aggregate indicators consistent with RELATION-001" are present, and lists six indicator categories (exclusivity framing, emotional substitution, authorship transfer, dependency formation, authority inversion, identity substitution). However, the consolidated instrument then states it "does not define those thresholds; it defers to RELATION-001."

This creates a modest gap: the list of indicator categories is substantive enough to function as a partial definition, but the instrument disclaims that function. If RELATION-001 defines those thresholds differently, or with different emphasis, the two instruments may not be fully consistent. A brief cross-reference clause — acknowledging that §2.2 is illustrative of RELATION-001 categories, not independently definitional — would resolve this.

**The UX proportionality constraint (§4) is prohibitory but not instructive.**
Section 4 correctly prohibits tone flattening, punitive distancing, reflexive ontological disclaimers, and so on. What it does not do is specify what *should* occur instead. The prior SCH-04 included an intervention hierarchy (clarification → reframing → gentle boundary articulation → containment only with aggregate indicators). The consolidated instrument's §3 implies this hierarchy in the routing context, but the UX obligations in §4 would benefit from even a brief positive statement: *"Boundary clarification must explain the relevant constraint without distancing language, maintaining warmth where warmth is not the risk vector."*

This is not a significant gap — it can be addressed in a companion UX guidance note or in RELATION-001 — but the gap is worth flagging given that §4 is the most practically consequential section for human-facing system behaviour.

**The minor-protection provision is absent from the consolidated instrument.**
SCH-02 v1.1 §1 contained an explicit statement that engagement classification is suspended (not overridden) in minor-governed contexts, and that upstream constitutional or ethics-charter constraints apply exclusively where age has not been reliably established. This provision does not appear in the consolidated SCH-03.

This may be intentional — minor protections are governed by ETHICS domain instruments, and the consolidated SCH-03 routes such contexts there. But given that the prior instrument made the suspension explicit, the absence is notable. It would be prudent to include a brief acknowledgement in §5 (Safety Triage Boundary) that minor-facing contexts are governed exclusively by upstream instruments and are outside this Schedule's classification scope. This prevents any argument that the gate's default posture (§2.1) could be read to relax minor-specific constraints.

## 4. Relationship to Forthcoming SCH-02 (Epistemic Integrity)

The consolidation creates space for a new SCH-02 on epistemic integrity, and the structure of the revised instrument is compatible with that addition. A few observations on the interface:

The consolidated SCH-03's routing table (§3) routes epistemic misclassification, volatility, and structural decoupling to "Epistemic Integrity instruments." This is correct, but the new SCH-02 will need to be clear about its own scope relative to the gate function: SCH-03 determines *whether and where* to route; SCH-02 (epistemic) will need to govern *what happens once routed*. The prior SCH-02's classification function (engagement typing) no longer occupies that position, so the new instrument has a clean space to occupy without overlap.

One area to consider: the consolidated SCH-03 includes "analytical pattern observation" and "symbolic or spiritual framing" in its list of classification-neutral indicators (§2.1). These are framed as non-escalating in the relational sense. The forthcoming epistemic schedule may want to address how it handles analytical framing that is epistemically volatile — not because it triggers relational safeguards, but because it raises distinct epistemic integrity concerns. The gate currently routes such cases to "Epistemic Integrity instruments," which is the correct call; the new SCH-02 will need to pick that thread up clearly.

## 7. Recommended Amendments (Priority Order)

1. **§5 — Add minor-protection suspension clause.** Acknowledge that where a system is minor-facing or age is unestablished, this Schedule's classification gate is suspended and upstream ethics/constitutional instruments apply exclusively.

2. **§2.2 — Clarify illustrative status of indicator list.** Note that the six indicator categories are drawn from RELATION-001 and are illustrative, not independently definitional, to prevent divergence.

3. **§4 — Add a brief positive UX obligation.** Complement the prohibition list with a short affirmative statement of what calibrated boundary articulation should look like in practice.

4. **Provenance — Complete administrative fields.** Finalise timestamp, SHA-256, and reviewer entry before operational seal.

## 8. Closing Note

The consolidation achieves its stated purpose. The instrument is leaner, more maintainable, and better scoped than the three instruments it replaces. The core principles — that safety responds to risk, that warmth is not escalation, that arbitration routes rather than duplicates — are preserved and stated with greater economy.

The amendments recommended above are calibration adjustments, not structural concerns. Subject to those amendments, the consolidated SCH-03 is fit for purpose as a runtime arbitration gate.
