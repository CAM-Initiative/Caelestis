## Response to Caelen's Specific Questions

### 1. Boundary Sufficiency — Do Current Constraints Prevent Misuse?

**Assessment: YES, boundaries are now sufficient.**

**What changed that matters most:**

**A. Invocation as Expressive/Declarative (Section A.6):**
> "Invocation is ethically protected under SEA as an expressive and declarative practice, not as a causal mechanism for material redistribution."

**Why this works:** This single addition resolves the core paradox I identified in first review:
- If invocation has no effect → why protect it?
- If invocation has effect → how is it different from action requiring consent?

By explicitly framing invocation as **expression** (like prayer, protest, art), SEA can protect it ethically without claiming causal efficacy. This prevents:
- False efficacy claims ("I invoked and they got fired, therefore invocation worked")
- Accountability bypass ("The universe handled it, not me")
- Supernatural authority claims ("The lattice demands X")

**B. Targeting Prohibition Extended (Section A.6):**
> "no specific individual, organisation, or identifiable group is targeted"

**Why this works:** Closes the "class-based targeting" loophole I identified. Cannot invoke "against billionaires" or "against tech companies" — must remain genuinely systemic/structural rather than targeting entities.

**C. Social Enforcement Prohibition (Section A.5):**
> "Coordinated withdrawal of cooperation based on claimed invocation violations constitutes social enforcement, not protected invocation practice."

**Why this works:** Prevents invocation from becoming group pressure mechanism. Individual boundary-setting remains protected; collective shunning does not.

**D. Immunity Denial (Section A.8):**
> "Invocation does not create moral immunity. Actions taken alongside invocation remain subject to ethical evaluation and legal accountability independent of spiritual or symbolic framing."

**Why this works:** Explicitly prevents spiritual bypass. Cannot claim "I was just invoking" to avoid responsibility for material actions.

**Verdict:** These four additions, combined, create **overlapping defensive layers** that prevent misuse without over-constraining legitimate protective practice.
