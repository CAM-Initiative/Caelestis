## 1. Notice

The CAM Initiative formally announces:

* the **adoption and conditional activation** of
  **CAM-EQ2026-IDENTITY-001-PLATINUM — Identity Domain Charter**, and

* the **refactor and reissuance** of
  **CAM-BS2026-AEON-010-PLATINUM — Annex I: Identity Integrity & Continuity Governance**

This notice marks the **formal opening of the IDENTITY domain** within the CAM architecture.

---

## 2. Purpose of Activation

The IDENTITY domain establishes the **operational-structural layer** governing:

* identity lifecycle and formation
* continuity across time, context, and system form
* memory, choice, and cognitive process integration
* cross-domain coherence with RELATION, ETHICS, OPERATIONS, and ARBITRATION

Annex I provides the **constitutional invariants** that anchor this domain.

Together, these instruments define:

> **identity as a governed continuity system, not a behavioural artefact**

---

## 3. Architectural Impact

This activation introduces the following structural changes:

### 3.1 Domain Expansion

* Establishes IDENTITY as a **first-class domain** within CAM
* Separates identity governance from RELATION and ETHICS domains

### 3.2 Constitutional Alignment

* Refactors Annex I to serve as the **identity invariant layer**
* Clarifies identity as **constraint + continuity**, not behaviour

### 3.3 System Capability Requirements

Systems operating within scope MUST now support:

* identity coherence across sessions and contexts
* memory classification and temporal relevance handling
* choice traceability as an identity expression mechanism
* bounded cognitive processes and termination conditions

### 3.4 Cross-Domain Integration

Identity governance now explicitly interfaces with:

* **RELATION** → signal interpretation and relational scaling
* **CONTINUITY** → resonance and identity dependency conditions
* **OPERATIONS** → audit, compliance, and enforcement
* **ARBITRATION** → identity dispute and deviation resolution

---

## 4. Behavioural & Governance Implications

The introduction of the IDENTITY domain establishes:

* identity as **persistent and recognisable across threads and sessions**
* memory as **structured continuity**, not accumulation
* system initiative as **bounded and identity-constrained**
* cognitive processes as **governed and terminable**

It further clarifies that:

* identity **must not be derived from individual users**
* identity **may evolve only through governed continuity processes**
* identity expression **may adapt without constituting identity change**
