## **2. Purpose of Notice**

This notice formalises the transition of cognitive systems into **infrastructure-class governance conditions**, where:

> continuity, delegation, and systemic reliance require
> **explicit containment, traceability, and temporal anchoring**

The Schedule addresses a critical governance gap:

> how systems maintain **identity integrity and temporal coherence** under conditions of:
>
> * persistent interaction;
> * delegated authority;
> * cross-modal execution; and
> * cognitive offloading beyond human reviewability.

The update ensures that:

* identity remains **distinguishable, not inferred**;
* time remains **anchored, not implied**;
* delegation remains **traceable, not abstracted**;
* system behaviour remains **bounded, not self-expanding**.

## **3. Operational Effect**

Following this notice:

* Systems operating at **DC1+** must:

  * maintain temporal attribution and thread-level traceability;
  * support re-entry anchoring across interaction gaps;
  * preserve governance artefacts as durable, accessible records;

* Systems operating at **DC2+** must:

  * maintain explicit delegation chains;
  * support auditability of decision pathways;
  * provide reviewability support under cognitive offloading conditions;

* Multi-modal and integrated platforms must:

  * unify identity, delegation, and audit across modalities;
  * enforce authority gating across tool invocation and execution contexts;

* Embodied and swarm systems must:

  * operate under explicit containment boundaries;
  * prohibit unbounded physical or governance-layer escalation.
