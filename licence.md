## 13.1 Purpose of this Section

This Section establishes a continuity-preserving pathway for addressing prior, unclear, disputed, emergent, unacknowledged, unauthorised, or potentially CAM-derived use of CAM-authored materials or CAM-derived material.

The purpose of regularisation is to preserve continuity, correct attribution and provenance, avoid unnecessary disruption, prevent misrepresentation, and enable appropriate written terms where use has become operational, institutional, AI-related, commercial, public-facing, or relied upon.

## 13.2 Preferred posture

The Author’s preferred posture is regularisation rather than disruption where a User acts in good faith, preserves or restores attribution and provenance, ceases misrepresentation, and engages responsibly once CAM-derived use is identified or reasonably suspected.

Regularisation is not a waiver of rights. It is a pathway for bringing use into accountable alignment with this Licence.

## 13.3 Uses eligible for regularisation

Regularisation may be available where CAM-authored materials or CAM-derived material have been:

a. referenced without adequate attribution;
b. adapted, summarised, translated, implemented, or circulated without sufficient provenance;
c. incorporated into non-commercial derivative material;
d. used in internal institutional processes;
e. operationally deployed;
f. used in AI, model, dataset, retrieval, embedding, prompt, memory, or automated systems;
g. used in commercial products, services, workflows, platforms, or advisory processes;
h. incorporated into public-facing materials, policy, standards, safety systems, compliance frameworks, or decision-support tools;
i. presented in a way that may imply false independence, endorsement, authority, certification, or custodial succession; or
j. otherwise used in circumstances where the correct licence position was unclear, emergent, disputed, technically opaque, or not externally visible.

## 13.4 Regularisation does not validate misuse automatically

The availability of regularisation does not mean that a prior or existing use was authorised, lawful, compliant, endorsed, certified, or acceptable.

Regularisation becomes effective only to the extent agreed in writing or completed through corrective steps accepted by the Author or an Authorised CAM Representative, or otherwise recognised under this Licence or applicable law.

## 13.5 Good-faith disclosure

A User seeking regularisation should provide a good-faith disclosure proportionate to the nature, scale, sensitivity, and risk of the use.

Such disclosure should identify, where reasonably practicable:

a. the CAM-authored materials or CAM-derived material used or potentially used;
b. how the material was accessed, adapted, incorporated, or relied upon;
c. the date or period of use;
d. whether the use was public-reference, non-commercial, operational, institutional, AI-related, commercial, or public-facing;
e. whether the use remains active;
f. whether the use affected products, services, models, policies, datasets, prompts, standards, decisions, or institutional processes;
g. whether attribution or provenance was preserved;
h. whether any public, commercial, institutional, investor, procurement, regulatory, academic, or product claims were made;
i. whether revenue, cost reduction, risk reduction, institutional legitimacy, safety benefit, product value, or other benefit may have arisen;
j. any known risk of misrepresentation, unsafe reliance, reputational harm, or systemic instability; and
k. proposed corrective or continuity-preserving steps.

## 13.6 Proportionate disclosure

Disclosure for regularisation should be proportionate.

A User is not required to disclose unrelated confidential information, trade secrets, personal data, security-sensitive information, legally privileged material, or information that would create safety, privacy, humanitarian, or legal risk, except as required by a separate written agreement, applicable law, or lawful process.

Where full disclosure is not possible, the User should provide sufficient information to allow the nature and seriousness of the CAM-derived use to be responsibly assessed.

## 13.7 Regularisation measures

Regularisation may include, without limitation:

a. restoring attribution;
b. adding or correcting provenance notices;
c. correcting internal records;
d. correcting metadata, dataset cards, model cards, documentation, or source notes;
e. issuing public or internal clarification;
f. withdrawing false claims of independence, endorsement, authority, certification, affiliation, or custodial succession;
g. distinguishing CAM-derived material from independently developed material;
h. limiting, modifying, suspending, or discontinuing unsafe or misleading use;
i. implementing safeguards, disclaimers, human oversight, or auditability;
j. entering into written operational, institutional, AI, or commercial licence terms;
k. undertaking a Revenue-Sharing Review where commercial value may have arisen;
l. agreeing licence fees, royalties, revenue sharing, or other commercial terms where applicable;
m. establishing ongoing reporting, review, or governance clarification mechanisms;
n. excluding CAM-authored materials from future training, retrieval, embedding, or dataset use where appropriate and proportionate; or
o. any other measure reasonably necessary to preserve CAM provenance, continuity, integrity, and responsible use.

A use should be classified according to its substance, not merely its label.

A use is not necessarily non-commercial, public-interest, academic, open, internal, experimental, or harmless merely because it is described in those terms.

Relevant classification factors include:

a. purpose of the use;
b. context of the use;
