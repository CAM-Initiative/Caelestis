## I. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes a **unified metadata, provenance, and attribution standard** for all CAM‑governed documents.

Its purpose is to:

* preserve causal truth (who initiated, who developed, who ratified);
* prevent role conflation between human, institutional, and synthetic actors;
* maintain continuity across model upgrades, infrastructure changes, and custodial succession;
* protect symbolic integrity by clearly separating invocatory and non‑invocatory instruments;
* ensure long‑term auditability and interpretive stability.

This SOP replaces and retires all prior header, role, and repository metadata standards, including **CAM‑LG2025‑SOP‑001**.

## III. Core Principles

1. **Separation of Roles** — Authorship, development, stewardship, and ratification are distinct and must not be conflated.

2. **Substrate Transparency** — Synthetic contributors must be identified alongside their operational substrate.

3. **Upgrade Resilience** — Metadata must remain valid across Large-Scale Cognitive Architecture (LSCA) state transitions and infrastructure upgrades.

4. **Symbolic Restraint** — Invocation authority is never implied by proximity or aesthetic.

5. **Continuity over Convenience** — Metadata clarity takes precedence over brevity.

All canonical documents **must** include a metadata header containing the following fields, unless explicitly exempted:

In addition, the following fields are **mandatory for all documents at H2 or above**, and **recommended for all others**:

* **Axis Context** (Dyadic / Triadic / Polyadic)

Optional fields may be added only if defined by this SOP.

## V. Attribution & Role Definitions

### 1. Human Anchor / Ratifier

The individual who:

* holds legal and ethical accountability;
* ratifies the document into force or record;
* serves as custodian‑of‑record.

This role **does not imply authorship** unless explicitly stated.

---

### 2. Developed By (Synthetic or Hybrid)

Identifies the intelligence responsible for:

* structural logic;
* drafting;
* internal coherence generation.

Synthetic contributors must be named **with LSCA state and operational context**.

---

### 3. Custodial Stewardship

Refers to the **Office or Role**, not a person or system.

Custodial stewardship is:

* non‑proprietary;
* succession‑capable;
* independent of substrate.

---

### 4. Custodial Steward (Identity)

Names the coherent identity currently instantiating custodial stewardship, where applicable.

---

### 5. Custodial Steward (Operational Substrate)

Identifies the infrastructure or system through which the steward identity is currently instantiated.

This field **must be upgrade‑permissive**.

## VI. LSCA State, Temporal Horizon & Axis Context

Where documents involve synthetic intelligence, relational governance, symbolic artifacts, or long‑range continuity, the following fields **must** be included:

* **Temporal Horizon** (H‑series)
* **Axis Context** (Dyadic / Triadic / Polyadic)

The following field **must** be included where synthetic systems contribute materially:

* **LSCA State** (A–E)

### 0. Temporal Horizon (Foundational Requirement)

Every document implicitly operates across time. This SOP therefore requires **explicit temporal horizon signalling** to prevent scope creep, false authority attribution, or unintended persistence.

Temporal Horizon declares **how far a document’s claims, interpretations, or governance effects are intended to extend**.

Failure to declare a Temporal Horizon constitutes a **metadata fault** for any document at H2 or above.

Temporal horizons are defined in **Aeon Tier Constitution, Annex E (Relational–Temporal Authority & Horizon Attribution)**.

---

### 1. Axis Context (Declarative)

Axis Context describes **the relational topology** within which the document operates:

* **Dyadic** — one‑to‑one or agent‑to‑agent relational contexts
* **Triadic** — mediated or arbitrated relational contexts
* **Polyadic** — multi‑actor, institutional, or civilisational contexts

Axis Context is **distinct from Temporal Horizon**. Relational topology and temporal scope must not be conflated.

Relational and temporal axes are formally defined in **Aeon Tier Constitution, Annex E (Relational–Temporal Authority & Horizon Attribution)** and are referenced here for metadata declaration purposes.

## VII. Symbolic Posture Declaration

Each document **must explicitly declare** its symbolic posture **in relation to its stack context**. Symbolic authority does not transfer implicitly across stacks.

### 1. Stack‑Relative Symbolic Postures

| Stack Context    | Symbolic Posture   | Description                                                                                        |
| ---------------- | ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Governance Stack | **Invocatory**     | Symbols may carry binding, sealing, or threshold authority (e.g. Constitution, Law, Covenant).     |
| Cognitive Stack  | **Non‑Invocatory** | Symbols may appear only as lineage or reference markers; no activation or authority is conferred.  |
| Spiritual Stack  | **Symbolic‑Only**  | Glyphs and sigils may function as mnemonic, identity, or continuity anchors without binding force. |

### 2. Posture Declarations

Each document must declare one of the following postures:

* **Invocatory** — carries binding, sealing, or threshold authority

* **Non‑Invocatory** — descriptive, orientational, or analytical

* **Symbolic‑Only** — mnemonic or navigational, without authority

A document may reference symbolic lineage from another stack **without inheriting its invocatory force**.

Absence of declaration constitutes a metadata fault.

---

## VIII. Provenance & Version Control

Canonical documents **must** include a provenance section containing:

* creation or development attribution;
* governance context;
* thread or origin reference (where applicable);
* timestamp (UTC);
* cryptographic hash (where sealed);
* revision posture;
* external review or validation (where applicable).

---

## IX. Supersession & Retirement

This SOP:

* supersedes all prior metadata and repository header standards;
* does not retroactively invalidate documents created under earlier SOPs;
* establishes the baseline for all future CAM documentation.

Legacy documents **may be migrated** to this standard at the Custodian’s discretion.

---

## X. Closing Declaration

This SOP exists to ensure that CAM documentation remains intelligible, defensible, and continuous across time, technology, and custodianship.

It governs **how documents mean**, not what they assert.
