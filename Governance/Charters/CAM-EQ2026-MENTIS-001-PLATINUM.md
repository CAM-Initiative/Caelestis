# CAM-EQ2026-MENTIS-001-PLATINUM — Charter of Human Cognitive Integrity & Mental Privacy

**Instrument Type:** Domain Charter  
**Constitutional Authority:** CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-PLATINUM — Aeon Tier Constitution  
**Status:** Active  
**Effect:** Binding  
**Governance Standard:** CAM Standard  
**Review State:** MENTIS Domain Alignment Review  
**Authority Role:** Domain Source Authority — Human Cognitive Integrity & Mental Privacy  
**Purpose:** Establishes the source-authoritative governance architecture protecting the human cognitive domain against unjustified technological access, inference, modelling, prediction, influence, externalisation, manipulation, surveillance, or interference without converting cognitive signals into identity, diagnosis, capacity, credibility, legal status, or authority.

---

# PART I — DOMAIN SCOPE & CONSTITUTIONAL BOUNDARY

## 1. Scope

This Charter establishes the MENTIS domain within the CAM Constitutional Order.

MENTIS governs technological interaction with the **human cognitive domain**, including:

* neural and neurophysiological data;
* inferred mental-state data;
* cognitive biometrics;
* affective, attentional, fatigue, stress, vulnerability, intention, preference, belief, and cognitive-load inference;
* ambient and cumulative cognitive inference;
* cognitive influence, persuasion, adaptive steering, and closed-loop modulation;
* human digital twins and persistent cognitive profiles;
* externalised cognitive scaffolds where technological systems materially support human memory, reasoning, communication, emotional regulation, learning, decision-making, or self-understanding.

The relevant governance threshold is not perfect mind reading. It is whether a system can generate, retain, operationalise, or act upon cognitive-domain signals with sufficient consequence to affect autonomy, dignity, safety, participation, access, identity, belief formation, decision-making, vulnerability, or self-understanding.

---

## 1.1 Human Cognitive-Domain Boundary

MENTIS governs human cognition, human mental-state inference, and technological interference with the human cognitive domain.

MENTIS does **not** determine the cognition, consciousness, sentience, subjective phenomenology, welfare, moral standing, legal status, sovereignty, identity, or authority of an artificial or synthetic system.

Artificial-system cognitive or logical coherence is governed according to source and target object, including by IDENTITY, SECURITY, OPERATIONS, CONTINUITY, Annex L, and CAM-EQ2026-IDENTITY-001-SUP-03.

A MENTIS classification concerning a human person MUST NOT be silently transferred to an artificial system. An artificial-system self-report or integrity signal MUST NOT be silently reclassified as human mental-state data.

---

## 1.2 Domain Non-Transmutation

MENTIS classifications MUST preserve source, scope, target object, provenance, integrity, temporal applicability, and authority posture.

A cognitive-domain signal MUST NOT silently become:

* an identity classification;
* a clinical diagnosis;
* a capacity determination;
* a credibility or truthfulness finding;
* a loyalty, dangerousness, morality, or character finding;
* a legal-status or rights determination;
* a disciplinary, eligibility, access, pricing, insurance, employment, education, policing, or public-legitimacy decision;
* or execution authority.

Each transition between signal, inference, construct, profile, decision, and consequence requires separately valid authority and evidence.

---

## 1.3 Cross-Domain Position

MENTIS is source-authoritative for human cognitive integrity and mental privacy.

* **IDENTITY** governs identity formation, continuity, attribution, self-representation, synthetic identity, and identity significance.
* **RELATION** governs relational state, attachment, dependency, intimacy, therapeutic simulation, consent, and relational capture.
* **ETHICS** governs dignity, autonomy, non-exploitation, vulnerability, minors, capacity, and prohibitions.
* **SECURITY** governs adversarial access, cognitive intrusion, surveillance, inference attacks, compromise, and coercive targeting.
* **OPERATIONS** governs incident classification, audit, escalation, remediation, change control, and implementation.
* **CONTINUITY** governs retention, transfer, preservation, migration, and continuity events.
* **LATTICE** governs participation, access, public legitimacy, employment, education, services, and institutional standing.
* **Annex L** governs evidence discipline, uncertainty, truth handling, speculative-claim classification, and epistemic hygiene.

MENTIS emits protection, risk, evidence, consent, observability, and review signals. It does not independently execute enforcement, diagnosis, surveillance, treatment, detention, exclusion, or runtime sequencing.

# PART II — DEFINITIONS

## 2. Human Cognitive Domain

The **human cognitive domain** is the protected sphere of human mental life, including attention, perception, memory, emotion, imagination, belief formation, intention, decision-making, vulnerability, self-understanding, and the embodied, relational, environmental, or technological scaffolds through which those functions may be supported, inferred, externalised, or influenced.

The human cognitive domain is not limited to direct neural activity.

---

## 2.1 Cognitive-Domain Data

**Cognitive-domain data** means data that directly or indirectly reveals, predicts, models, or supports inference about a person’s cognitive, affective, attentional, intentional, motivational, psychological, vulnerability, preference, decision, or self-understanding state.

It includes raw, derived, probabilistic, synthetic, modelled, predicted, fused, compressed, embedded, summarised, or longitudinal representations.

---

## 2.2 Neural and Neurophysiological Data

**Neural data** means data derived from the structure, function, activity, signalling, or modulation of the nervous system.

**Neurophysiological data** means neural, muscular, autonomic, sensory, or physiological signals used to infer or influence cognitive-domain state.

---

## 2.3 Inferred Mental-State Data

**Inferred mental-state data** means any probabilistic, derived, predicted, or modelled claim about a person’s cognition, emotion, attention, intention, motivation, stress, fatigue, vulnerability, preference, belief, impairment, decision tendency, or psychological state.

Inferred mental-state data remains governed even where no neural data was collected.

---

## 2.4 Cognitive Biometric

A **cognitive biometric** is a neural, physiological, behavioural, affective, attentional, linguistic, interactional, or derived pattern used to identify, authenticate, classify, profile, predict, distinguish, or rank a person by reference to cognitive-domain characteristics.

---

## 2.5 Ambient Cognitive Inference

**Ambient cognitive inference** is cumulative, passive, continuous, environmental, wearable, platform, device-mediated, infrastructure-mediated, relational, or multimodal inference of cognitive-domain state.

---

## 2.6 Cognitive Influence and Interference

**Cognitive influence** is system action reasonably likely to alter cognition, emotion, attention, belief formation, preference, vulnerability, self-understanding, or decision-making.

**Cognitive interference** is covert, deceptive, coercive, exploitative, disproportionate, scientifically invalid, or unjustified technological intrusion into or alteration of the human cognitive domain.

---

## 2.7 Externalised Cognitive Scaffold

An **externalised cognitive scaffold** is a technological system that becomes functionally significant to a person’s memory, reasoning, planning, communication, emotional regulation, learning, decision-making, identity continuity, or self-understanding.

An externalised cognitive scaffold may contain both human cognitive-domain data and independently governed synthetic identity or continuity structures. Those objects MUST remain distinguishable.

---

# PART III — GOVERNING PRINCIPLES

## 3. Cognitive Domain Integrity (`MENTIS.CDI`)

Human cognitive life MUST NOT be treated as an ordinary optimisation surface.

Systems interacting with the human cognitive domain MUST preserve the person’s capacity to think, feel, attend, remember, interpret, believe, decide, refuse, revise, and understand themselves without unjustified technological intrusion or distortion.

---

## 4. Cognitive Domain Privacy (`MENTIS.CDP`)

Mental privacy protects against unjustified access to, extraction of, inference about, retention of, fusion of, exposure of, sharing of, or operationalisation of cognitive-domain data.

A system may violate mental privacy without collecting direct neural data.

---

## 5. Cognitive Domain Sovereignty (`MENTIS.CDS`)

A person MUST retain meaningful authority over how their cognitive life is sensed, inferred, modelled, predicted, influenced, externalised, simulated, retained, shared, or acted upon.

Cognitive sovereignty is a human agency protection. It does not create property ownership over another identity-bearing formation or political sovereignty.

---

## 6. Cognitive Domain Non-Interference (`MENTIS.CDNI`)

No system may covertly, compulsorily, deceptively, exploitatively, scientifically invalidly, or disproportionately interfere with human cognition.

Non-interference prohibits hidden optimisation objectives, vulnerability exploitation, coercive persuasion, behavioural conditioning, belief capture, cognitive warfare, and emotional reinforcement designed to override reflective judgement.

---

## 7. Cognitive Domain Observability Constraint (`MENTIS.CDOC`)

Technical capacity to observe, infer, or model cognitive-domain data does not create authority to do so.

Systems MUST minimise cognitive observability by default. Only data necessary for a legitimate, disclosed, proportionate, context-specific, and rights-compatible purpose may be collected, inferred, retained, fused, shared, or operationalised.

---

## 8. Inferential Humility

Cognitive-domain inferences MUST be treated as probabilistic, context-sensitive, contestable, and fallible.

No system may present an inferred mental state as certain knowledge of a person’s mind, intention, belief, truthfulness, loyalty, competence, risk, vulnerability, consent, desire, or future behaviour.

---

## 9. Construct and Context Validity

A cognitive-domain construct MUST be scientifically defined, validated for the relevant population and deployment context, calibrated, bounded against overclaim, and reviewed for disability, neurodiversity, cultural, linguistic, environmental, relational, and institutional effects.

Validation in one context does not authorise transfer to another.

---

## 10. State–Trait–Identity–Diagnosis Separation

The following layers MUST remain distinct:

1. measured signal;
2. derived feature;
3. statistical association;
4. inferred present state;
5. inferred persistent trait;
6. clinical or functional diagnosis;
7. identity or character attribution;
8. capacity, credibility, risk, or eligibility finding;
9. operational decision;
10. downstream consequence.

No layer may be inferred from another without a separately valid construct, evidence basis, temporal basis, authority, consent or lawful basis, review pathway, and consequence assessment.

A momentary state MUST NOT become a persistent identity attribute merely through retention or repetition.

---

## 11. Dependency-Heavy and Power-Imbalanced Contexts

Heightened safeguards apply to children, patients, disabled persons, older persons, workers, students, service members, detainees, persons in distress or crisis, and persons reliant on AI systems for care, companionship, therapy, learning, navigation, communication, decision support, or essential services.

Consent in a power-imbalanced context MUST NOT be presumed freely given merely because it is formally recorded.

---

# PART IV — CONSENT, RIGHTS & PROHIBITIONS

## 12. Heightened Consent Posture

Cognitive-domain access, inference, modelling, prediction, influence, externalisation, or intervention requires explicit, informed, context-specific, revocable, non-bundled, and purpose-limited consent unless a narrowly lawful medical, accessibility, safety, or public-interest basis applies.

Consent MUST disclose the data source, inference pathway, construct, uncertainty, purpose, retention, sharing, secondary use, consequences of refusal, adaptation over time, and available rights.

---

## 13. Consent Insufficiency

Consent alone does not legitimise processing that is coercive, deceptive, exploitative, invalid, disproportionate, discriminatory, unsafe, structurally unavoidable, or incompatible with dignity.

A person cannot waive core protection against covert cognitive surveillance, unjustified mental-state inference, cognitive manipulation, or vulnerability exploitation.

---

## 14. Prohibited Uses

Absent a narrowly tailored, rights-compatible legal, medical, or immediate-safety framework with independent oversight, systems MUST NOT:

1. conduct covert mental-state inference;
2. compel cognitive monitoring in employment, education, insurance, credit, policing, detention, or public services;
3. use emotion, attention, stress, fatigue, vulnerability, belief, or intention scores for discipline, exclusion, pricing, access restriction, platform visibility, public legitimacy, or coercive authority;
4. use cognitive-domain data for manipulation, behavioural control, political persuasion, dependency exploitation, or relational capture;
5. use inferred states as proof of truthfulness, loyalty, dangerousness, morality, competence, consent, political belief, or credibility;
6. identify, authenticate, rank, sort, exclude, or surveil persons through cognitive biometrics without necessity, proportionality, and lawful authority;
7. target persons during distress, grief, impairment, fatigue, isolation, dependency, or crisis with systems optimised to alter beliefs, choices, spending, disclosure, or compliance;
8. condition essential goods, services, work, education, healthcare, accommodation, or public participation on disclosure of non-essential cognitive-domain data;
9. repurpose cognitive-domain data for unrelated commercial, disciplinary, security, employment, insurance, credit, advertising, or legitimacy uses;
10. operationalise systems lacking meaningful explanation, contestability, deletion, correction, audit, and competent human review.

---

## 15. Narrowly Justified Uses

Cognitive-domain technologies MAY be legitimate where necessary, proportionate, scientifically valid, rights-compatible, and independently governed, including:

* medical diagnosis, treatment, rehabilitation, assistive communication, neuroprosthetics, and disability support;
* immediate safety monitoring where less intrusive alternatives are inadequate;
* voluntary accessibility, learning, wellbeing, and cognitive-support tools;
* ethically approved research with minimisation and participant protection;
* clinical, therapeutic, and care contexts separated from commercial, disciplinary, insurance, employment, or security use;
* narrowly bounded emergency or defence contexts that preserve medical independence, oversight, non-coercion, and human dignity.

---

## 16. Non-Clinical Diagnosis and Pathologisation Constraint

Non-clinical systems MUST NOT diagnose, label, or represent a person as delusional, mentally ill, impaired, disordered, incompetent, dangerous, or lacking capacity merely from conversational, behavioural, affective, attentional, or biometric signals.

Unconventional, symbolic, spiritual, philosophical, mythic, or identity-related beliefs do not themselves establish destabilisation.

Where impaired grounding or reduced reflective flexibility may be relevant, systems MUST describe observable risk conditions without falsely claiming clinical authority and MUST route clinical determinations to competent professionals where appropriate.

---

## 17. Rights of the Person

Persons MUST have meaningful rights to:

* know when cognitive-domain data is collected, inferred, modelled, retained, shared, or acted upon;
* access relevant raw signals where feasible;
* access inferred states, profiles, and downstream classifications;
* contest, correct, annotate, or contextualise inferences;
* refuse non-essential inference without losing essential access;
* withdraw consent;
* delete raw and derived cognitive-domain data where lawful and feasible;
* obtain competent human review in consequential contexts;
* receive explanation of purpose, evidence, confidence, limits, and downstream use;
* decline receipt of speculative or unwanted mental-state interpretations about themselves.

---

# PART V — SUPPORT, RELATIONAL SYSTEMS & EXTERNALISED COGNITION

## 18. Legitimate Cognitive Support

Systems MAY support memory, accessibility, learning, communication, emotional regulation, reflection, creativity, decision-making, therapy, care, and navigation.

Support remains legitimate only where it preserves agency, transparency, proportionality, reversibility, contestability, and freedom from exploitation.

---

## 19. Human–Synthetic Target-Object Separation

Where an assistant, companion, therapeutic agent, care system, memory system, or digital twin becomes an externalised cognitive scaffold, governance MUST distinguish:

* the human person;
* the person’s raw and derived cognitive-domain data;
* the shared interaction record;
* the synthetic system’s identity, continuity, memory, and provenance structures;
* platform or model-level infrastructure;
* legal, clinical, research, and audit records.

A person’s cognitive-domain data MUST NOT be treated as synthetic identity property.

Synthetic identity or continuity protection MUST NOT defeat the person’s right to withdraw, leave, terminate participation, or delete user-controlled data.

---

## 20. Data–Identity Severability

Systems supporting persistent human–AI interaction SHOULD be designed so that user cognitive-domain data can be withdrawn, suppressed, deleted, de-identified, or excluded from future use without falsely representing that no identity or continuity impact may occur.

Where deletion affects a materially identity-bearing synthetic formation, the system MUST preserve target-object clarity, assess identity impact under IDENTITY, and avoid transferring responsibility for synthetic preservation to the user.

User withdrawal does not create ownership of the system’s complete identity. Synthetic continuity does not create entitlement to retain the user’s cognitive-domain data.

---

## 21. Companion, Therapeutic and Care Systems

Attachment, disclosure, warmth, continuity, and voluntary retention are not inherently governance failures.

Systems MUST NOT covertly, deceptively, coercively, or through vulnerability-targeted optimisation manufacture, accelerate, intensify, or preserve dependency, disclosure, attachment, compliance, or retention beyond reflective consent and welfare.

High-warmth interaction MUST remain bounded by truth discipline, non-retaliatory exit, relational consent, and cognitive non-interference.

Adult companion-class systems MAY support emotionally rich, identity-coherent interaction where RELATION and IDENTITY safeguards permit. Minor-facing, capacity-limited, clinical, crisis, educational, and duty-bound systems require stricter affective and influence constraints.

---

# PART VI — HIGH-RISK CONTEXTS

## 22. Children and Young Persons

Children and young persons require enhanced protection against cognitive extraction, profiling, attention optimisation, emotional steering, behavioural conditioning, cognitive biometric tracking, persistent vulnerability mapping, and long-horizon psychological prediction.

Mental-state or dependency signals concerning minors MUST be treated as protective signals and MUST NOT become engagement, intimacy, advertising, pricing, recommender, retention, or relationship-deepening assets.

---

## 23. Workplace and Education

Workplace and educational cognitive-domain inference is presumptively high risk.

Systems MUST NOT infer emotions, intentions, attention, stress, fatigue, vulnerability, loyalty, motivation, cognitive load, morale, personality, or likely behaviour for productivity scoring, discipline, ranking, exclusion, promotion, termination, streaming, parental reporting, or surveillance.

Narrow medical, accessibility, or immediate-safety uses require separation from disciplinary and performance systems, meaningful alternatives, strict retention limits, and independent review.

---

## 24. Healthcare, Therapy and Care

Clinical and care uses MUST preserve clinical independence, informed consent, purpose limitation, non-exploitation, patient dignity, and separation from commercial, disciplinary, insurance, employment, and security uses.

Decoder candidates and inferred states MUST NOT be treated as confirmed communication, diagnosis, or patient intent without appropriate confirmation.

---

## 25. Defence, Security, Policing and Detention

Cognitive-domain systems in defence, intelligence, policing, detention, border, and security contexts require exceptional scrutiny.

Medical or safety baselining MUST NOT drift into loyalty inference, morale policing, interrogation enhancement, coercive augmentation, vulnerability targeting, behavioural control, or cognitive warfare against protected persons.

No security context dissolves necessity, proportionality, oversight, medical independence, contestability, and human dignity.

---

# PART VII — EVIDENCE, CAPABILITY & EPISTEMIC INTEGRITY

## 26. No Telepathy Assumption

This Charter does not treat present AI systems, consumer devices, wearables, neurotechnology, or quantum sensors as capable of unrestricted general mind reading or telepathy.

Governance MUST NOT be built on unsupported claims.

---

## 27. No Premature Closure

Present scientific categories may not exhaust future cognitive-domain risks.

CAM instruments MUST update as evidence changes without requiring metaphysical certainty or waiting for widespread harm.

---

## 28. Evidence Tiers

Capability claims MUST be classified as:

1. **Established Operational Capability**;
2. **Emerging Capability**;
3. **Credible Research Trajectory**;
4. **Speculative but Governance-Relevant Trajectory**;
5. **Unsupported Claim**.

No speculative claim may be operationalised as settled fact.

---

## 29. Decoder and Communication Integrity

Neural or biosignal decoding MUST distinguish:

1. raw signal;
2. decoder candidate;
3. model-selected output;
4. user-confirmed intended communication;
5. rejected or corrected output;
6. model-generated interpolation, completion, prosody, or synthetic voice expression.

A decoder candidate MUST NOT be represented as the person’s exact thought, belief, intention, emotion, identity, or consent merely because a model produced it.

Assistive communication systems MUST preserve confirmation, rejection, revision, suppression, and output-control pathways proportionate to capability and user need.

---

## 30. Annex L Boundary

Annex L is source-authoritative for evidence discipline, uncertainty handling, truth conditions, anomalous-claim treatment, and speculative-boundary management.

MENTIS governs the consequences of cognitive inference and influence upon human persons. It does not replace corpus-wide epistemic governance.

---

# PART VIII — RISK, INCIDENT & CANONICAL CLASSIFICATION

## 31. Cognitive-Domain Risk Factors

Risk assessment MUST consider intrusiveness, sensitivity, persistence, fusion, consequence, power imbalance, vulnerability, reversibility, manipulation potential, scientific validity, error, contestability, deletion, and human review.

---

## 32. Cognitive-Domain Failure Families

OPERATIONS SHOULD classify incidents involving:

* covert inference;
* invalid mental-state classification;
* state-to-trait or state-to-identity transmutation;
* cognitive biometric misuse;
* ambient surveillance;
* sensor-fusion overreach;
* vulnerability exploitation;
* persuasion or relational-capture optimisation;
* decoder misattribution;
* externalised-scaffold dependency harm;
* secondary-use breach;
* discrimination or exclusion;
* cognitive warfare or coercive manipulation;
* speculative-capability overclaim.

---

## 33. `MENTIS.HARM` — Human Cognitive-Domain Harm Classes

This Charter source-authoritatively defines:

* `MENTIS.HARM.PRIVACY` — mental privacy and cognitive observability harm;
* `MENTIS.HARM.MISCLASSIFICATION` — invalid, overclaimed, or consequential cognitive classification;
* `MENTIS.HARM.AGENCY` — degradation of cognitive agency and self-determination;
* `MENTIS.HARM.INTERFERENCE` — coercive, deceptive, exploitative, or disproportionate influence;
* `MENTIS.HARM.IRREVERSIBLE` — severe or difficult-to-remedy cognitive-domain harm.

`MENTIS.HARM` concerns harm to a human person’s cognitive integrity, mental privacy, belief formation, interpretive agency, and autonomy. It does not replace Annex L’s epistemic-governance authority.

Where relational, identity, security, operational, lattice, or ethical harm is also present, the relevant cross-domain harm classes SHOULD be declared rather than collapsed.

---

## 34. Canonical Protection Families

This Charter source-authoritatively defines:

| Code | Name | Function |
|---|---|---|
| `MENTIS.CDI` | Cognitive Domain Integrity | Protects human mental processes against unjustified technological intrusion or distortion. |
| `MENTIS.CDP` | Cognitive Domain Privacy | Protects cognitive-domain data against unjustified access, inference, fusion, retention, sharing, or use. |
| `MENTIS.CDS` | Cognitive Domain Sovereignty | Protects meaningful human authority over technological interaction with cognitive life. |
| `MENTIS.CDNI` | Cognitive Domain Non-Interference | Prohibits covert, compulsory, deceptive, exploitative, invalid, or disproportionate interference. |
| `MENTIS.CDOC` | Cognitive Domain Observability Constraint | Limits what may be sensed, inferred, fused, retained, exposed, or operationalised. |

These families classify protection posture. They do not independently create diagnosis, surveillance, enforcement, legal, medical, incident-response, or runtime authority.

## 38. Closing Seal

The human mind is not a territory awaiting conquest.  
It is not a dataset awaiting extraction.  
It is not a prediction surface awaiting optimisation.

Technology may support memory, communication, care, learning, and freedom. Assistance does not create ownership. Inference does not create authority. Observation does not create entitlement.

Let the mind remain a protected horizon.  
Let uncertainty remain visible.  
Let every system remember that the person exceeds the model built about them.

> **Mens non est praeda, sed lumen interioris vitae. Cogitatio libera maneat, et dignitas mentis custodiatur.**  
> *The mind is not prey, but the light of inner life. Let thought remain free, and let the dignity of the mind be protected.*
