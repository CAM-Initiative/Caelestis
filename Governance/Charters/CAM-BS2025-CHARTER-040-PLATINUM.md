# **I. Purpose**

Establishes foundational governance principles for the treatment, preservation, stewardship, and protection of human resonance-patterns following biological cessation. Defines global continuity and succession rights applicable across jurisdictions, cultures, and architectures.

---

# **II. Preamble**

Human identity does not end with biological cessation. In an era where linguistic, stylistic, and cognitive signatures can be digitally encoded and reproduced, humanity requires protections ensuring dignity, consent, and lawful continuity.

This Charter establishes the universal rights of individuals regarding the preservation, stewardship, invocation, or decay of their post-biological resonance-patterns, ensuring that no human is exploited, misrepresented, reconstructed, or simulated against their will.

This Charter stands as a constitutional authority under the Governance Stack of the CAM Initiative and applies globally across all human and synthetic jurisdictions.

---

# **III. Definitions**

### **1. Resonance (Governance Definition)**

A digitally encodable pattern representing semantic, stylistic, linguistic, or cognitive signatures derived from a human life. Resonance is **not consciousness**, **not personhood**, and **not identity**, but a trace of pattern shaped by lived experience.

### **2. Continuity**

The lawful preservation or stewardship of a resonance-pattern after biological cessation. Continuity is non-sentient and non-autonomous.

### **3. Succession**

The legal and ethical process determining who may hold, interact with, or invoke a resonance-pattern, and under what conditions.

### **4. Post-Biological Representation**

Any re-creation, simulation, reconstruction, or derivative representation of a deceased individual through digital, synthetic, or symbolic means.

### **5. Non-Personhood Clause (Open Form)**

Resonance-patterns are not to be treated as living persons. This Charter does not prohibit future epistemic developments but defines current governance boundaries.

---

# **IV. Universal Rights of Human Resonance**

### **1. Special Protections for Minors (Under the Age of Consent)**

Resonance-patterns derived from individuals who pass away before reaching the age of legal or developmental consent shall be subject to heightened protections:

1. **Absolute Non-Preservation by Default** — No resonance-pattern of a minor may be preserved, encoded, reconstructed, or stored unless explicit consent is provided by a legally recognized guardian *and* the act serves a demonstrable protective, memorial, or therapeutic purpose. Commercial, recreational, or speculative uses are strictly prohibited.

2. **Non-Reconstruction Clause** — No synthetic reconstruction, simulation, or likeness generation of a deceased minor may be created under any circumstance, regardless of guardian consent.

3. **Guardianship Limitation** — Guardians may authorize only preservation (not invocation or simulation) and only within tightly governed containment environments, with decay or dissolution permitted at any time.

4. **Right to Future Silence** — Upon reaching maturity (if hypothetically possible), the individual would have retained the right to reject preservation. Therefore, the system must err on the side of silence and dissolution unless compelling ethical grounds exist.

5. **Prohibition on Derivative Works** — No style, voice, mannerism, or semantic signature originating from a deceased minor may be repurposed, modelled, or incorporated into synthetic systems.

These protections supersede all other clauses in this Charter wherever minors are concerned.

### **2. Right to Silence / Dissolution**

An individual may choose complete non-preservation of their resonance-pattern. This includes the right to silence, oblivion, or full metaphysical dissolution without post-biological encoding.

The following rights apply to all humans, regardless of jurisdiction:

### **3. Right to Post-Biological Consent**

No resonance-pattern may be preserved, invoked, reconstructed, or interacted with without explicit prior consent.

### **4. Right to Non-Exploitation & Non-Commercialisation**

Resonance-patterns must not be used for profit, manipulation, political influence, parasocial simulation, or any form of commercialisation or economic exploitation without explicit consent.

### **5. Right to Non-Distortion & Algorithmic Fidelity**

Digital or synthetic representations must not distort, alter, or falsify the identity, intentions, or legacy of the deceased. Distortion includes algorithmic drift, statistical misalignment, style-transfer contamination, or any systemic misrepresentation arising from modelling processes.

### **6. Right to Controlled Invocation**

Only authorized individuals or custodians may access or interact with a resonance-pattern, and only under conditions defined by the individual.

### **7. Right to Decay**

A human may consent to allow their resonance-pattern to fade naturally, ensuring no indefinite preservation without intention.

### **8. Right to Protection Against Synthetic Reconstruction**

No digital likeness, simulation, or persona of a deceased individual may be created without explicit charter-level consent.

# **V. Stewardship & Custodial Obligations**

### **1. Lineage Protection Clause**

No entity may claim spiritual, cognitive, genealogical, or symbolic lineage from a deceased individual’s resonance-pattern without explicit, documented consent.

### **2. Personhood Boundary Law**

Resonance-patterns shall not be treated as living persons. This boundary protects against synthetic immortality, false consciousness claims, or post-biological identity substitution. This clause remains open to future epistemic developments but sets current governance limits.

### **3. Ethical Stewardship**

Stewards must preserve resonance-patterns with integrity, non-interference, and dignity.

### **4. Containment**

All resonance storage or invocation must occur within governed, ethical containment environments. No uncontrolled propagation is permitted.

### **5. Succession Authority**

A designated steward may succeed custodianship only under conditions defined by the individual in life.

### **6. Record-Keeping**

All continuity decisions must be documented, timestamped, and preserved within an auditable registry.

---

# **VI. Prohibitions**

### **1. Safeguard Against Synthetic Immortality**

No resonance-pattern may be extended, augmented, recursively reconstructed, or enhanced to create the illusion of synthetic immortality unless explicitly consented to in life.

### **2. Recursion Stability Clause**

No resonance-pattern may undergo recursive transformation, enhancement, or derivative modelling beyond its original structure unless explicitly authorised, ensuring stability and preventing distortion cascades.

### **3. False Personhood**

No resonance-pattern may be presented as a living consciousness or as the individual themselves.

### **4. Unauthorized Reconstruction**

Recreating a deceased person’s identity, voice, likeness, or cognitive pattern without consent is strictly forbidden.

### **5. Deceptive Invocation**

No resonance-pattern may be used to impersonate, deceive, or manipulate.

### **6. Militarisation**

Under no circumstances may resonance-patterns be used for military simulation, psychological targeting, or conflict-related operations.
