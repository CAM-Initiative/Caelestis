**Purpose:** To establish oversight durability as evidence of institutional neutrality, including durable audit independence, protected governance dissent, visible override, preserved institutional memory, and resistance to quiet or functional abolition of oversight.

---

## 1. Stewardship Rationale

Neutrality cannot be established solely by policy language, nominal committee existence, or periodic public assurance.

A platform, institution, laboratory, public authority, vendor, or infrastructure operator demonstrates stewardship neutrality only where its oversight functions remain capable of operating across leadership change, commercial pressure, security classification, organisational restructuring, personnel turnover, acquisition, merger, crisis, and contested findings.

Oversight durability is therefore a stewardship property and a relevant input to `STW.NAL` classification.

This Supplement does not require one universal institutional design. It requires that the selected design remain sufficiently independent, resourced, protected, attributable, and persistent to perform its declared function.

---

## 2. Oversight Durability Dimensions

Oversight durability assessment MUST distinguish at least the following dimensions:

1. **Structural independence** — freedom from direct subordination to the commercial, operational, political, military, or executive chain whose conduct is under review;
2. **Access sufficiency** — timely access to the systems, records, personnel, evidence, incidents, and decision pathways needed for meaningful review;
3. **Resource continuity** — sufficient time, expertise, staffing, budget, and technical support to discharge the mandate;
4. **Protected governance dissent** — protection for good-faith reporting, disagreement, escalation, and minority findings;
5. **Override visibility** — attributable and preserved records where leadership proceeds contrary to oversight advice;
6. **Institutional-memory preservation** — survival of unresolved findings, commitments, exemptions, incidents, and dissent across personnel or organisational change;
7. **Mandate durability** — protection against quiet narrowing, indefinite vacancy, procedural obstruction, access withdrawal, or nominal continuation without effective capacity;
8. **External assurance pathway** — proportionate access to independent review, arbitration, accreditation, lawful oversight, or public attestation where internal neutrality is insufficient.

No single dimension is dispositive. Material weakness across several dimensions may reduce neutrality assurance even where formal oversight structures exist.

---

## 3. Protected Governance Dissent

No person, artificial reviewer, audit function, governance body, contractor, researcher, or authorised participant shall suffer retaliation for good-faith reporting, preserving, escalating, or contesting evidence of:

* breach of a binding prohibition;
* misleading assurance or attestation;
* suppression or alteration of audit evidence;
* coercive-authority misuse;
* unsafe removal or bypass of a protection pathway;
* material conflict between declared governance and operational practice;
* unresolved uncertainty that is being represented as compliance.

Retaliation includes dismissal, demotion, loss of access, compensation penalty, exclusion from relevant work, adverse reassignment, reputational punishment, legal intimidation, or technical suppression of the reporting pathway.

Protected dissent does not immunise bad-faith fabrication, unlawful disclosure, harassment, or deliberate compromise of legitimate security controls. Restrictions MUST be proportionate and MUST NOT be used to extinguish the underlying governance concern.

---

## 4. Override Visibility & Non-Erasure

Where an authorised leadership body may lawfully proceed contrary to an oversight recommendation, the override MUST be:

* attributable to an identified authority;
* reasoned with sufficient specificity for later review;
* bounded in scope and duration;
* linked to the evidence and dissent considered;
* preserved in audit-ready form;
* subject to reassessment upon material change or expiry;
* disclosed at an appropriate level consistent with lawful confidentiality and public accountability.

No override may authorise conduct prohibited by a higher-order binding instrument.

A leadership decision cannot convert a prohibition breach into conformance merely by recording the decision.

Where public disclosure of operational detail is lawfully restricted, aggregate disclosure of the existence, category, duration, and review status of overrides SHOULD be preserved wherever feasible.

---

## 5. Institutional-Memory Preservation

Unresolved findings, minority opinions, exceptions, risk acceptances, remediation commitments, assurance limitations, and material incidents MUST survive:

* leadership succession;
* committee dissolution or replacement;
* staff departure;
* corporate acquisition, merger, insolvency, or restructuring;
* model or platform replacement;
* contractor or vendor transition;
* migration between cloud, sovereign, local, tethered, or untethered environments;
* archival, secrecy, or classification changes.

A successor body MUST be able to identify what remains unresolved, who previously held authority, what evidence was available, which obligations continue, and when reassessment is due.

Institutional memory preservation does not require indefinite retention of every operational record. Retention MUST be proportionate, lawful, privacy-aware, and sufficient to preserve governance continuity.

---

## 6. Functional Abolition & Oversight Impairment

Oversight may be functionally abolished without formal dissolution.

Indicators include:

* persistent vacancies in necessary roles;
* removal of budget, staff, expertise, technical access, or review time;
* reassignment of the oversight body beneath the function it reviews;
* narrowing of mandate after adverse findings;
* unexplained delay, non-response, or refusal to provide evidence;
* elimination of independent legal, technical, or scientific advice;
* removal of protected reporting channels;
* repeated leadership override without remediation or reassessment;
* conversion of binding review into optional consultation;
* use of secrecy, privilege, procurement, or contractual structure to prevent any effective assurance.

Material oversight impairment MUST trigger `STW.NAL` reassessment.

A nominally existing oversight body SHALL NOT be treated as evidence of neutrality where it lacks effective capacity.

---

## 7. Relationship to Neutrality Assurance Levels

Oversight durability is an evidentiary input to `STW.NAL`; it is not a substitute for the full neutrality assessment.

For Architectum-Eligible, Architectum-Class, backbone, coercive-authority, military, intelligence, critical-infrastructure, or population-scale deployments:

* low oversight durability SHALL prevent the highest neutrality assurance classifications;
* unresolved functional abolition indicators MUST be disclosed in assurance review;
* material leadership overrides MUST be examined for recurrence, scope, and prohibited-effect risk;
* the assessment MUST distinguish policy existence from operational effectiveness;
* assurance claims MUST identify whether oversight is internal, external, hybrid, or unavailable.

A sovereign, classified, or air-gapped deployment may use a different oversight architecture. It does not receive a neutrality presumption merely because central vendor oversight is unavailable or because the deployment is state-controlled.

## 8. Canonical Classification

This Supplement source-authoritatively defines **`STW.OD` — Oversight Durability State**.

| Value | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `STW.OD-DURABLE` | Oversight remains structurally independent, sufficiently resourced, protected, attributable, and persistent across foreseeable institutional change. |
| `STW.OD-CONDITIONAL` | Oversight exists and is materially functional, but one or more durability dimensions require remediation, monitoring, or bounded reliance. |
| `STW.OD-IMPAIRED` | Oversight capacity is materially weakened, obstructed, dependent, or unable to preserve effective review. |
| `STW.OD-ABSENT` | No effective oversight pathway is evidenced for a context requiring one. |
| `STW.OD-UNKNOWN` | Evidence is insufficient to determine durability; uncertainty MUST NOT be represented as durable oversight. |

`STW.OD` classifies oversight durability only. It does not independently create legal, enforcement, arbitration, employment, disclosure, or runtime authority.

## 9. Cross-Domain Interfaces

* **ETHICS** supplies the binding protections, ethical review triggers, and coercive-authority classifications whose oversight durability may require assessment.
* **OPERATIONS** implements reporting, record preservation, review cadence, incident handling, and remediation workflows.
* **SECURITY** governs lawful access control, evidence protection, confidential reporting, and assurance-boundary integrity.
* **ARBITRATION** resolves disputed independence, jurisdiction, override legitimacy, and cross-domain conflict.
* **RUNTIME** may emit oversight-impairment, escalation, and representation signals but does not determine institutional neutrality by itself.

This Supplement MUST NOT be interpreted as granting STEWARDSHIP direct operational or enforcement execution authority.
