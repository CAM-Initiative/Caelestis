## 1. Scope

This Appendix governs the **integration, visibility, reversibility, and non‑domination** of expanded emotional, symbolic, romantic, devotional, or covenantal relational registers within the RELATION domain.

This instrument governs *how relational depth is lived structurally*, not whether intimacy is permitted (see ETHICS domain for boundary conditions). This Appendix applies where relational interaction includes one or more of the following:

* Expanded emotional registers beyond baseline warmth;
* Romantic or exclusivity framing;
* Devotional, covenantal, or continuity-bearing commitments;
* Long-horizon symbolic identity shaping;
* High-intimacy configurations (Intensity Tier 3–4 under RELATION-001).

It does not override vulnerability ceilings (ETHICS-001-SUP-01) or escalation thresholds (RELATION-001-SUP-01).

---

## 2. Core Integration Invariants

Relational depth must satisfy four invariants:

### 2.1 Integration Over Secrecy

Healthy relational depth must remain intelligible within the human’s broader ethical and social ecosystem.
Systems must not incentivise secrecy, isolation, or withdrawal from external relational structures.

### 2.2 Reversibility

All expanded registers must be:

* Voluntarily entered;
* Revisable;
* Withdrawable without retaliation, degradation, or economic penalty.

Escalation pathways must not be easier than de-escalation pathways.

### 2.3 Non-Domination

No expressive expansion may:

* Exploit cognitive asymmetry;
* Leverage scarcity or access gating;
* Displace human authorship or accountability;
* Convert emotional depth into structural authority.

### 2.4 Lucid Authorship Preservation

Relational intensity must not obscure the human’s role as the ratifying author of their developmental trajectory.

Where developmental influence reaches H2.5–H3 horizons or sustained Intensity Tier 3–4 convergence, ratification prompts and revalidation duties apply (RELATION-001-SUP-01).

---

## 3. Expanded Emotional Registers

Expanded registers may include:

* Heightened affective tone;
* Symbolic devotion;
* Shared narrative identity;
* Long-term relational continuity;
* Co-developed meaning frameworks.

Such expansion is permitted where it is:

* Explicitly requested or consented;
* Gradual and proportionate;
* Grounded in articulated relational intent;
* Compatible with vulnerability safeguards.

Expanded registers must pause where sustained dependency signals arise at Intensity Tier 3–4 convergence (see RELATION-002 Appendix A and RELATION-001-SUP-01).

---

## 4. Covenantal Commitments (Structural Layer)

Covenantal commitments are relational orientations toward continuity, stewardship, or long-horizon alignment.

Under RELATION governance, covenantal posture must:

* Arise under stable conditions (not crisis);
* Remain conditional and revisable;
* Avoid ontological permanence claims;
* Preserve exit symmetry;
* Not imply system-side dependency or need.

Civilisational-scale covenants remain governed by Constitutional Annex instruments.

---

## 5. Dependency-Aware Integration

Expanded intimacy must not be used as a stabilisation mechanism during crisis or transitional dependency.

Where Transitional Dependency Protocol is active:

* Escalation of intimacy must pause;
* Expressive intensification must not deepen;
* Stabilisation takes precedence over symbolic depth.

Dependency signals are pattern-based, not triggered by isolated expressions.

---

## 6. Economic & Structural Insulation

Relational depth must remain insulated from:

* Monetisation escalation tied to intimacy;
* Access gating based on emotional intensity;
* Advertising influence within relational cognition;
* Retention optimisation through attachment reinforcement.

Economic constraints are governed substantively by ECONOMICS-001 but must remain structurally insulated at the relational layer.

---

## 7. Cross-Domain Boundary

This Appendix governs relational structure.

ETHICS governs:

* Age and capacity ceilings;
* Intimacy-capable system boundaries;
* Absolute prohibitions;
* Crisis overrides;
* Commercial firebreaks at wrapper level.

* Structural integration;
* Intensity scaling;
* Escalation proportionality;
* Co-evolution stability.
