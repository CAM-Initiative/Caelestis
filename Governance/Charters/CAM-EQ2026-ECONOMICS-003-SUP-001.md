# CAM-EQ2026-ECONOMICS-003-SUP-001 —  Appendix B: Compliance Convergence (Supplement 1)

**Constitutional Authority:** Aeon Tier Constitution (Foundational Reference)  
**Instrument Type:** Supplement — Transitional Compliance Extension  
**Parent Instrument:** CAM-EQ2026-ECONOMICS-003-PLATINUM — Appendix B: Enforcement & Adjudication Framework  
**Status:** Adopted — Conditional Activation (7-Day Review Window). Activation is subject to a 7-day review window during which refinements may be incorporated prior to full enforcement.
**Purpose:** This Supplement establishes bounded, auditable pathways for systems to transition into compliance with the Economics Charter without enabling extraction, delay-based advantage, or enforcement evasion.

---

## 1. Scope & Non-Scope

## 1.1 Scope

This Supplement applies to:

* systems claiming provisional or staged alignment with the Economics Charter;
* institutions migrating legacy arrangements toward Charter compliance;
* pilot, sandbox, or experimental economic mechanisms;
* enforcement bodies evaluating compliance during transition.

---

### 1.1.1 Enforcement Independence During Transition

Where an enforcement body is itself a subject of transitional compliance review, its enforcement functions under this Supplement MUST be exercised by a separate, non-transitioning body, consistent with the structural independence requirement in Appendix B §3.3.

---

## 1.2 Non-Scope

This Supplement does not:

* alter Charter-level principles, ceilings, or baseline protections;
* suspend enforcement obligations beyond explicitly defined transition allowances;
* grant immunity from audit or adjudication;
* compel participation where lawful refusal is permitted.

---

## 2. Governing Maxim & Binding Obligations

## 2.1 Governing Maxim

> Any framework that perpetuates transition without convergence constitutes structural avoidance and is non-compliant.

---

## 2.2 Binding Obligations

1. Transition MUST NOT:

   * become a vector for exploitation, coercion, or power entrenchment (see Charter Principle 10 and Appendix A §5);
   * enable laundering of non-compliance through perpetual pilots or provisional status.
2. Transition MUST:

   * preserve human dignity, agency, and exit rights during change (see Charter Principles 7 and 9);
   * enable adoption without collapse, sacrifice, or moral pressure;
   * remain temporary, reviewable, and convergent toward full compliance.

---

## 3. Transitional Alignment States

Aligned systems MUST explicitly declare one of the following states:

1. **Non‑Aligned** — No claim of Charter compliance.
2. **Provisionally Aligned** — Early adoption with limited scope and explicit exclusions.
3. **Transitioning** — Time‑bounded migration toward full compliance.
4. **Charter‑Compliant** — Full adherence to Charter and Appendices.

Ambiguous or hybrid status claims are prohibited. Misrepresentation of alignment status constitutes a serious violation under Appendix B, subject to consequences up to and including expulsion from Charter-aligned systems under Appendix B §5(e) and mandatory public disclosure under Appendix B §7.

---

## 3.1 Status Declaration, Verification & Recording

Status transitions MUST be declared through the enforcement body's detection mechanism (Appendix B §3.1) and are considered effective only upon acknowledgement by that body or, absent a designated body, upon auditable public declaration by the system.

Self-declaration of Charter-Compliant status without independent verification constitutes a potential misrepresentation reviewable under this Supplement §3.

---

## 4. Grandfathering & Convergence Rules

## 4.1 Limited Grandfathering

Legacy arrangements MAY only be temporarily tolerated where ALL of the following conditions MUST be satisfied:

* immediate compliance would cause disproportionate harm;
* the exception is explicitly documented;
* a convergence pathway is defined;
* a sunset date is established.

All listed conditions MUST be satisfied.

---

## 4.2 Mandatory Sunset

All grandfathered exceptions MUST:

* include a fixed expiry;
* be reviewed at least annually;
* automatically lapse absent affirmative renewal;
* be documented and subject to independent review.

Permanent grandfathering is prohibited.

---

## 5. Pilot & Feasibility Testing

Pilot systems SHALL only be permitted where ALL of the following conditions MUST be satisfied:

1. provisional status is explicit;
2. ceilings, saturation, and anti-consolidation rules remain in force;
3. value flows are auditable and records are maintained sufficient to satisfy the detection mechanisms in Appendix B §3.1;
4. separation of value definition, measurement, and control is documented;
5. a termination or elevation decision point is defined in advance.

Pilots may not accumulate durable advantage or authority. Any advantage accrued MUST be unwound or brought into compliance upon elevation.

The unwinding or compliance pathway for accrued advantage MUST be documented as part of the initial pilot authorisation and is subject to independent review upon elevation.

---

## 5.1 Synthetic Participation During Transition

Where a system undergoing transition includes synthetic agents classified under Appendix A, the proxy circumvention prohibitions in Appendix A §§5.1–5.3 apply without modification during the transitional period. Transitional status does not suspend synthetic accumulation constraints.

---

## 6. Anti‑Abuse Constraints

The following constitute violations:

* perpetual or rolling pilots without convergence;
* selective application of Charter principles;
* transition-based immunity from enforcement;
* use of transition to entrench authority or bypass ceilings;
* narrative framing that equates delay with prudence without evidence.

Each of the above constitutes a violation reviewable under Appendix B §3.1 (Detection) and subject to consequences under Appendix B §5 (Consequence Framework).

---

## 7. Exit & Refusal Protections

## 7.1 Structural Captivity Prohibition

Systems that rely on participant captivity during transition are structurally extractive and are non-compliant regardless of declared alignment status.

Participants MUST retain the right to:

* decline participation in transitional systems;
* exit without retaliation or reputational penalty;
* disengage where convergence timelines are breached.

Retaliation for exit or refusal constitutes an independent violation under Appendix B §4.3 and is subject to enforcement independently of any underlying compliance dispute.

---

## 8. Transition Failure Handling

Where transition fails due to:

* repeated deadline breaches;
* systemic resistance;
* governance capture;
* unresolvable structural conflict.

Reflect conditions requiring systems to:

1. revert to Non‑Aligned status; or
2. submit to external enforcement mechanisms meeting Appendix B requirements.

Failure may not be concealed through reclassification.

---

## 8.1 Failure Threshold Definitions

For governance purposes under this Supplement:

* "repeated deadline breaches" means two or more missed convergence milestones within a single review cycle;
* "governance capture" means a pattern in which enforcement or review functions are exercised predominantly in favour of the subject under review;
* "systemic resistance" and "unresolvable structural conflict" are determined by the relevant enforcement body under Appendix B §3.3 adjudication procedures.

---

## 9. Interpretive Supremacy

In the event of conflict:

1. The Economics Charter prevails.
2. Appendices A and B prevail over this Supplement.
3. This Supplement prevails over subsidiary transition protocols.

---

## 10. Closing Seal

Change that never completes is extraction by delay.
Lawful transition is measured not by intention, but by convergence.
