# CAM-EQ2026-ECONOMICS-001-SUP-01

## 4.4 Canonical Taxonomy & Schema Integrity

Operational governance systems MAY utilise canonical taxonomies, schema vocabularies, signal classifications, decision-state mappings, or controlled operational reference sets to preserve cross-domain consistency, reviewability, and reconstruction integrity.

Operational taxonomy systems SHALL preserve distinction between:

* semantic classifications;
* schema fields;
* operational states;
* governance signals;
* decision outputs;
* risk classifications;
* and actor or role taxonomies.

Controlled taxonomy values SHALL remain distinguishable from:

* schema placement structures;
* routing metadata;
* procedural context;
* or runtime operational state.

Operational governance systems SHOULD preserve sufficient metadata to identify whether a taxonomy is:

* defined;
* consumed;
* emitted;
* routed;
* inherited;
* or transformed

within a given instrument or runtime pathway.

Taxonomy systems SHALL preserve:

* traceability;
* semantic consistency;
* reviewability;
* cross-domain interpretability;
* and constitutional reconstruction capacity.

Taxonomy ambiguity, hidden schema coupling, or uncontrolled classification drift MAY constitute operational integrity concerns where reviewability, interoperability, or governance reconstruction become materially impaired.
