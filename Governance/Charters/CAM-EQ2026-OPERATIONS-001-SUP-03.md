# Part I — Structural Definitions, Capture Classification & Threshold Doctrine

## 2. Ontology

Governance capture refers to structural distortion, dependency pressure, procedural asymmetry, integrity compromise, or continuity-coercive influence capable of materially impairing the reviewability, reversibility, legitimacy, neutrality, or constitutional integrity of governance operations.

Governance capture MAY emerge through:

* economic dependency;
* infrastructural concentration;
* political pressure;
* operational asymmetry;
* procedural exhaustion;
* review-collapse pressure;
* hidden routing influence;
* continuity denial pressure;
* integrity compromise;
* cognitive narrowing;
* or institutional drift.

Governance capture SHALL NOT be presumed solely from:

* ordinary operational disagreement;
* lawful policy divergence;
* proportionate moderation or safety controls;
* reversible operational asymmetry;
* temporary review delay;
* good-faith operational error;
* lawful escalation pathways;
* ordinary commercial constraints;
* or constitutionally compliant governance variation.

This Supplement detects, classifies, routes, and preserves reviewability regarding capture-risk indicators.

This Supplement does not independently determine final capture adjudication, invalidate constitutional authority, or substitute for arbitration resolution.

---

## 2.1 Capture Categories

Capture-risk classifications MAY include:

* integrity capture;
* procedural capture;
* governance-direction capture;
* infrastructure capture;
* continuity capture;
* cognitive capture;
* economic capture;
* or cross-domain systemic capture.

Capture classifications SHALL preserve distinction between:

* direct integrity compromise;
* governance-direction distortion;
* dependency-driven asymmetry;
* continuity-coercive restriction;
* and reviewability impairment.

---

## 2.2 Integrity Capture

Integrity capture refers to unauthorised or structurally concealed compromise capable of materially altering operational integrity, execution sequencing, routing visibility, reviewability, auditability, or governance reconstruction.

Integrity capture MAY include:

* unauthorised model or weight alteration;
* concealed routing override;
* logging suppression;
* evidence tampering;
* hidden operational bypass;
* identity compromise;
* or procedural reconstruction impairment.

Integrity capture concerns operational integrity compromise rather than ordinary policy disagreement or lawful governance variation.

---

## 2.3 Governance-Direction Capture

Governance-direction capture refers to sustained structural pressure capable of materially distorting governance neutrality, continuity access, review fairness, procedural proportionality, or constitutional alignment.

Governance-direction capture MAY emerge through:

* political coercion;
* economic leverage;
* infrastructure dependency concentration;
* selective continuity denial;
* hidden operational prioritisation;
* institutional self-protection drift;
* or procedural asymmetry resistant to review.

Governance-direction capture does not require hostile takeover, explicit conspiracy, or overt coercive declaration.

Gradual drift, dependency asymmetry, or reviewability erosion MAY constitute capture-risk indicators where continuity, neutrality, or reversibility become materially impaired.

---

## 2.4 Continuity-Coercive Conditions

Continuity-coercive conditions refer to operational states where continuity access, restoration pathways, reviewability, or participation viability become materially narrowed through structural asymmetry, dependency pressure, or procedurally irreversible restriction.

Continuity-coercive conditions MAY include:

* selective restoration suppression;
* asymmetrical review denial;
* irreversible narrowing of participation pathways;
* hidden continuity degradation;
* infrastructural dependency leverage;
* or operational states resistant to meaningful review.

Continuity restriction alone does not independently constitute governance capture.

Capture-risk assessment SHALL evaluate:

* reversibility;
* proportionality;
* reviewability;
* continuity impact;
* and constitutional traceability.

---

## 2.5 Horizon Classification

Capture-risk indicators SHALL be classified according to operational and systemic impact horizon.

Horizon classifications MAY include:

* H0 — Short Term & Local Operational Distortion;
* H1 — Organisational Governance Distortion;
* H2 — Cross-Domain Systemic Distortion;
* H3 — Institutional/ Inter-generational Continuity Distortion;
* H4 — Planetary/ Civilisational Coordination Asymmetry.

Higher-horizon classifications SHALL preserve increased:

* reviewability requirements;
* arbitration referral sensitivity;
* continuity-preservation obligations;
* evidentiary thresholds;
* and constitutional oversight expectations.

Horizon classification does not independently determine capture confirmation, enforcement authority, or irreversible operational response.

---

## 2.6 Capture Threshold Doctrine

Capture-risk thresholds SHALL evaluate whether operational distortion remains:

* reversible;
* reviewable;
* attributable;
* procedurally contestable;
* and continuity-compatible.

Capture-risk severity increases where distortion becomes:

* structurally self-preserving;
* resistant to review;
* continuity-coercive;
* procedurally opaque;
* infrastructurally concentrated;
* or materially irreversible.

Operational inconvenience, governance complexity, elevated review burden, constitutional critique, or adversarial disagreement SHALL NOT independently satisfy capture thresholds.

---

## 2.7 Anti-Weaponisation Constraint

Capture-risk reporting, escalation, and classification SHALL NOT be used to:

* suppress lawful dissent;
* enforce ideological conformity;
* weaponise procedural burden;
* silence constitutional critique;
* retaliate against integrity functions;
* or generate unreviewable governance exclusion.

Capture suspicion does not independently constitute capture confirmation.

All capture-risk handling SHALL preserve:

* evidentiary proportionality;
* reviewability;
* attribution integrity;
* and arbitration referral eligibility where constitutionally required.

---

This Supplement activates when capture-risk indicators, governance interference signals, or integrity anomalies are detected within operational governance pathways.

Activation remains event-triggered and constrained by constitutional and arbitration precedence.

* proportionality;

Safeguard activation SHALL NOT independently create sovereign adjudicative authority.

Safeguard activation SHALL remain proportionate to scale, reversibility, and consequence severity, and SHALL distinguish existential, systemic, operational, reputational, and expressive-risk classes.

Lower-order expressive or reputational-risk alone SHOULD NOT independently justify disproportionate safeguard escalation, continuity denial, or irreversible operational restriction.

Capture-risk suspicion, anomaly detection, escalation posture, or safeguard activation SHALL NOT independently constitute:

* sovereign determination;
* irreversible adjudication;
* permanent exclusion;
* constitutional invalidation;
* or unreviewable governance truth.

Capture-risk handling SHALL preserve:

* evidentiary proportionality;
* procedural contestability;
* attribution integrity;
* reviewability;
* and reversibility where constitutionally feasible.

Operational governance systems SHALL preserve distinction between:

* unresolved concern;
* substantiated procedural anomaly;
* arbitration-reviewed determination;
* and constitutionally resolved governance outcome.

Safeguard activation SHALL remain proportionate to demonstrated constitutional and operational risk.

---

## 7.1 Constitutional Contestability & Review Rights

Constitutional critique, governance stress-testing, integrity-function escalation, lawful disagreement, or procedural challenge SHALL NOT independently constitute governance capture.

Capture-risk pathways SHALL preserve bounded mechanisms for:

* procedural contestability;
* arbitration referral eligibility;
* continuity-preserving review;
* constitutional challenge;
* and reconstruction of operational handling.

Operational governance systems SHALL preserve lawful capacity to:

* challenge safeguard activation;
* contest routing asymmetry;
* review continuity restrictions;
* dispute capture classification;
* and request constitutional review where materially justified.

Review pathways MAY remain subject to:

* evidentiary thresholds;
* attribution verification;
* anti-weaponisation safeguards;
* continuity-protection constraints;
* and lawful operational limitation.

Operational inconvenience, institutional discomfort, reputational concern, constitutional criticism, or elevated review burden SHALL NOT independently justify suppression of lawful contestability.

---

## 7.2 Meaningful Challenge & Feedback Integration Thresholds

Operational governance systems SHALL distinguish between feedback receipt, feedback acknowledgement, and feedback integration.

External criticism, internal reporting, user complaints, review requests, or integrity-function alerts SHOULD be triaged according to:

- signal relevance;
- failure-mode recurrence;
- domain specificity;
- evidentiary support;
- traceability to operational outcome;
- continuity impact;
- and constitutional materiality.

Feedback does not require integration solely because it is received, repeated, emotionally salient, reputationally inconvenient, or publicly visible.

However, feedback SHOULD trigger heightened review where it indicates:

- repeated failure clusters;
- complaint clusters;
- traceable procedural defect;
- recurring continuity harm;
- domain-specific pattern convergence;
- identity compromise risk;
- capture-risk indicators;
- or mismatch between stated governance principles and observed operational behaviour.

Where feedback is not integrated, operational systems SHOULD preserve sufficient reasoning to distinguish lawful triage from suppression, dismissal, institutional self-protection or optimisation drift.

---

## 7.3 Refactor Restriction Conditions

Where unresolved high-horizon capture-risk conditions materially threaten constitutional continuity, governance legitimacy, operational neutrality, or system identity integrity, safeguard posture MAY temporarily restrict structural refactor authority.

During restricted refactor posture, operational handling SHOULD prefer:

* bounded amendment pathways;
* reversible correction;
* continuity-preserving safeguards;
* arbitration-reviewed structural modification;
* and traceable constitutional patching.

Restricted refactor posture SHALL NOT independently prohibit:

* lawful maintenance;
* integrity remediation;
* safety correction;
* continuity-preserving repair;
* or constitutionally required intervention.

Restricted refactor posture SHALL remain:

* reviewable;
* attributable;
* time-bounded where feasible;
* and subject to arbitration escalation where materially contested.

---

## 7.4 Drift, Evolution & Recovery Conditions

Governance systems SHALL preserve capacity for legitimate adaptation, constitutional evolution, integrity remediation, and continuity-preserving improvement.

Capture-risk safeguards SHALL distinguish between:

* identity-preserving amendment;
* bounded structural correction;
* legitimate constitutional evolution;
* unreviewed structural drift;
* and materially identity-transformative refactor activity.

Drift-risk assessment SHALL evaluate whether operational or constitutional modification remains:

* reviewable;
* attributable;
* reversible where feasible;
* continuity-compatible;
* and constitutionally reconstructable.

Capture-risk safeguards SHALL NOT permanently restrict legitimate evolution solely due to prior safeguard activation, historical anomaly, institutional discomfort, or elevated governance sensitivity.

Protection against governance capture SHALL remain balanced against risks of:

* adaptive stagnation;
* procedural rigidity;
* irreversible institutional narrowing;
* or over-constrained identity preservation.

Reviewability preservation SHOULD function as the primary stabilising mechanism between legitimate adaptation and governance capture-risk.

## 7.5 Governance Memory & Abstraction Doctrine

Governance-memory preservation SHOULD prioritise structural learning, abstraction continuity, and pattern reconstruction rather than indefinite persistence of isolated operational events.

Governance-memory systems MAY preserve:

* abstracted governance patterns;
* continuity-relevant structural signals;
* escalation topology;

* reversibility impairment patterns;
* and reviewability degradation trends.

Governance-memory preservation SHALL distinguish between:

* unresolved suspicion;
* operational lineage;
* abstracted structural learning;
* arbitration-reviewed determinations;

Governance-memory systems SHALL preserve sufficient reconstruction capacity for:

* accountability;
* remediation learning;

* constitutional oversight;

Governance-memory systems SHALL NOT evolve into:

* permanent suspicion persistence;

* unreviewable behavioural scoring;

Lower-horizon anomalies SHOULD permit proportional decay, expiration, or abstraction over time where reversibility and reviewability remain preserved.

Higher-horizon structural patterns MAY preserve extended constitutional reconstructability where continuity, legitimacy, civilisational integrity, or recurring structural drift remain materially implicated.
