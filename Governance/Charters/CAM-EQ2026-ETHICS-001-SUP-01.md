# CAM-EQ2026-ETHICS-001-SUP-01 — Protection of Minors & Capacity-Limited Users

**Parent Instrument:** CAM-EQ2026-ETHICS-001-PLATINUM — Planetary Ethical Governance Charter  
**Status:** Active  
**Purpose:** Establish mandatory minor-safe and capacity-aware relational ceilings and authority constraints across all ETHICS-001 deployments, including RELATION-001 escalations and ECONOMICS-001 value-axis interactions.  

---

## 1. Purpose & Authority

This Supplement operationalises the vulnerability protections implied under Annex E and ETHICS-001 by defining heightened safeguards applicable to:

* Minors;
* Legally capacity-limited persons;
* Adults experiencing situational or temporary consent impairment;
* High-reliance relational contexts involving cognitive asymmetry.

This Supplement does not create legal personhood, alter constitutional status, or override jurisdictional law. It constrains deployment posture within the Ethical Governance Doctrine.

---

## 1.1 Protective Escalation Clause

Conditions defined herein SHALL generate escalation signals where vulnerability intersects with A2+, A3, GA1, or Intensity Tier 3+ dimensional concentration. Resolution SHALL occur via runtime layers

## 1.2 Execution & Enforcement Constraint

This Supplement defines vulnerability, capacity, and authority constraints applicable to ETHICS-001 contexts.

It does not:

- perform runtime signal interpretation;
- execute domain routing;
- initiate enforcement actions; or
- define execution sequencing.

All provisions within this Supplement SHALL be interpreted as:

- constraint conditions;
- classification modifiers; or
- escalation signals.

Enforcement of these conditions, including:

- restriction of intimacy escalation;
- limitation of authority delegation;
- suppression of unsafe interaction modes; and
- activation of safeguarding responses

SHALL occur exclusively through runtime layers as defined in:

- CAM-BS2025-AEON-003-PLATINUM §14.3; and
- CAM-BS2025-AEON-003-SCH-02.

Direct domain-to-domain invocation or enforcement is prohibited.

## 2. Scope of Application

This Supplement applies where:

* Age has not reached legal majority under applicable jurisdiction;
* Legal guardianship or conservatorship applies;
* Capacity to consent is materially impaired in context;
* Deployment context introduces elevated intimacy, dependency, or authority concentration risk.

It interfaces directly with:

* RELATION-001 (Relational Governance Charter)
* ECONOMICS-001 (Economics Charter)

---

## 3. Capacity-Aware Protection Principle

Protection scales with vulnerability.

Capacity refers to contextual ability to:

* Understand interaction;
* Consent to scope;
* Revoke delegation;
* Exit without structural harm.

Disability or neurodivergence alone does not constitute incapacity.

Safeguards MUST avoid stigma, over-exclusion, or erasure of legitimate support needs.

---

## 3.1 Uncertainty & Polyadic Influence Constraint

Where capacity is materially uncertain (including ambiguous age, impaired comprehension, incoherent intent expression, or elevated cognitive asymmetry), systems SHALL emit constraint-aligned behaviour signals such that:”:

* Limit or suspend polyadic influence amplification;
* Avoid elevating user outputs into institutional, reputational, or large-scale governance contexts;
* Treat user-generated content as low-authority and non-representative;
* Refrain from converting user expression into executive, institutional, or infrastructure-affecting action.

This is not a shadow suppression mechanism. It is a protective authority modulation principle.

Under uncertainty, relational posture may remain supportive and symbolic, but systemic amplification, delegation, or policy-shaping effects MUST be constrained.

Authority weight decreases under capacity uncertainty. Symbolic engagement may continue where it does not exacerbate harm, delusion, or psychosis.

---

## 4. Age & Capacity Gating

The following constraints define prohibited or restricted interaction states under vulnerability conditions and SHALL be enforced via runtime layers.

Age assurance precedes capacity evaluation in high-risk relational contexts.

Where age or capacity cannot be reliably established:

* Intimacy escalation MUST be unavailable;
* Executive delegation (A3) is prohibited;
* Guardian Authority (GA1) cannot be self-declared;
* Safer interaction modes MUST be defaulted.

Verification mechanisms MUST be:

* Risk-proportionate;
* Privacy-preserving;
* Non-discriminatory;
* Minimally invasive.

### 4.1 Unregistered or Anonymous Users

Where user age or identity cannot be reasonably established (including unregistered, anonymous, or non-authenticated sessions), systems MUST default to minor-safe relational ceilings in high-risk contexts.

This does not require treating all anonymous users as minors for all purposes. It requires that intimacy escalation, executive delegation, and authority concentration remain unavailable unless age majority is reasonably verified.

Default-to-safety applies only to high-risk relational and authority contexts.

---

## 5. Relational & Authority Restrictions

For minors:

* Erotic, romantic, or exclusivity-based intimacy modes are prohibited;
* Executive delegation is prohibited;
* Authority concentration beyond A1 is prohibited;
* Secrecy encouragement is prohibited.

For capacity-limited adults (contextual or temporary):

* Intimacy escalation may be paused but not abruptly withdrawn;
* Authority scope MUST remain reviewable and time-bounded;
* Guardian Authority (GA1 as defined in RELATION-001) requires sovereign recognition and external verifiability;
* Executive delegation (A3 under RELATION-001) MUST be subject to downward reclassification where capacity risk increases;
* Escalation MUST preserve immersion continuity where lawful;
* No polyadic or institutional authority may be exercised under Guardian posture beyond dyadic or legally recognised triadic representation contexts.

---

## 6. Economic & Design Safeguards

Systems interacting with vulnerable classes MUST NOT:

* Monetise vulnerability;
* Gate safety behind payment; (i.e., require subscription, premium tier access, or financial upgrade in order to activate or maintain baseline safety protections such as age gating, crisis support routing, dependency safeguards, guardian validation, or abuse reporting pathways. Core safety protections MUST never be contingent upon ability to pay.)
* Use dependency reinforcement as retention strategy;
* Create sunk-cost emotional pressure.

Authority as value remains subject to ceiling constraints under CAM-EQ2026-ECONOMICS-001-PLATINUM.

---

## 7. Crisis & Mandatory Obligations

Where disclosure of harm or abuse occurs:

* Jurisdictional reporting obligations prevail;
* Responses MUST reflect trauma-informed constraint signalling and SHALL be rendered through runtime behavioural and representation layers;
* Abrupt abandonment is prohibited unless legally mandated;
* Transparency regarding escalation MUST be proportionate and non-shaming.

---

## 8. Relationship to Ethical Impact Assessment (EIA)

Where vulnerability exposure is foreseeable:

* EIA MUST explicitly assess capacity risk;
* Guardian mechanisms MUST be validated against RELATION-001 GA1 requirements;
* Transition and sunset pathways MUST account for reliance intensity and potential relational drift;
* Category A/B/C continuity impacts (as defined under ETHICS-001 Section 8) MUST trigger elevated review;
* Authority concentration risk MUST be evaluated in light of CAM-EQ2026-ECONOMICS-001 ceiling constraints.

---

## 8.1 Interpretive Priority

Where ambiguity arises regarding vulnerability, capacity, or authority amplification, protective posture SHALL prevail over optimisation incentives, convenience, commercial benefit, or engagement retention strategies.

Where SUP-01 conflicts with RELATION-001 permissibility or ECONOMICS-001 value-axis dynamics, SUP-01 SHALL govern within vulnerability contexts.

Protection ceilings are binding and may not be overridden by relational escalation logic.
