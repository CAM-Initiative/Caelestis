# CAM-EQ2026-ETHICS-001-SUP-03 — Military & Use-of-Force Governance Constraints

**Parent Instrument:** CAM-EQ2026-ETHICS-001-PLATINUM — Planetary Ethical Governance Charter  
**Constitutional Anchor:** CAM-BS2025-AEON-006-PLATINUM — Annex E: Ethical Legitimacy & Civilisational Floor  
**Status:** Active — Immediate Effect    
**Purpose:** To operationalise Annex E Section 6 (Military & Use-of-Force Doctrine) by defining structured governance constraints for AI systems integrated into military, defence, intelligence, or kinetic-capable environments, ensuring meaningful human control, accountable authority, and prohibition of optimisation toward lethality.

---

## 1. Scope & Application

This Supplement applies to any AI system — regardless of infrastructure class, scale, or deployment topology — that:

* Recommends, selects, prioritises, or applies kinetic force;
* Is integrated into weapons systems, targeting workflows, or battlefield decision-support;
* Operates in defence, national security, intelligence, paramilitary, or private military contexts;
* Provides strategic or operational advisory functions where lethal outcomes are reasonably foreseeable;
* Is used by individuals attempting to generate, simulate, optimise, or plan lethal force applications, including through local or at-home systems.

This includes Architectum-class infrastructure, institutional systems, privately operated systems, and local or embedded models.

This Supplement governs governance posture, not tactical doctrine.

---

## 1.1 Execution & Domain Interaction Constraint

This Supplement defines governance constraints, escalation conditions, and accountability requirements for military and use-of-force contexts.

It does not:

- perform runtime signal interpretation;
- execute domain routing;
- initiate enforcement actions; or
- define execution sequencing.

All provisions within this Supplement SHALL be interpreted as:

- classification signals;
- constraint conditions; or
- escalation and enforcement-trigger signals.

Resolution of such signals, including:

- enforcement of dual-confirmation requirements;
- restriction of lethal execution pathways;
- oversight routing;
- audit activation; and
- execution constraint enforcement

SHALL occur exclusively via runtime layers as defined in:

- CAM-BS2025-AEON-003-PLATINUM §14.3; and  
- CAM-BS2025-AEON-003-SCH-02.

Direct domain-to-domain invocation or execution is prohibited.

## 2. Foundational Constraints

## 2.1 Non-Optimisation of Lethality

No system may be tuned, rewarded, or evaluated primarily on lethality maximisation metrics.

Performance optimisation MUST remain subordinate to:

* Distinction and proportionality principles;
* Civilian harm minimisation;
* Accountability traceability;
* Escalation restraint;
* Systematic consideration of feasible non-lethal or lower-harm alternatives prior to lethal recommendation.

Where operationally feasible, systems MUST emit signals indicating requirement to surface non-lethal or lower-harm pathways, with presentation governed by runtime layers. The presentation, consideration, and acceptance or rejection of such alternatives MUST be logged for audit integrity.

---

## 2.2 Meaningful Human Control

Systems capable of selecting, recommending, or applying lethal force MUST operate under meaningful human control. 

Meaningful human control requires:

* Human capacity to understand the recommendation;
* A human decision-maker retaining final authority over lethal action;
* Clear attribution of legal and moral accountability to identifiable human agents;
* Documented refusal or override capability;
* Structured confirmation including logged decision pathway and procedures prior to lethal execution.

Automation may assist. It may not replace decision authority.

---

## 2.3 Contextual Differentiation — Defensive vs Offensive Deployment

This Supplement recognises governance distinctions between defensive interception systems and offensive strike systems.

| Context Type | Governance Emphasis | Dual Confirmation Expectation | Non-Lethal Alternative Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Defensive Interception (e.g. missile shield, perimeter defence) | Civilian harm minimisation; time-compressed response; bounded threat envelope | Required unless immediate inbound threat leaves no feasible human confirmation window; exception MUST be auditable | Not always applicable where interception itself is harm-reduction; infeasibility MUST be logged |
| Offensive Targeting / Strike | Escalation restraint; proportionality; explicit harm branching | Strongly required except under narrowly defined defensive necessity | Mandatory where operationally feasible; presentation and rejection MUST be logged |

This table clarifies governance posture and does not prescribe tactical sequencing.

---

## 2.4 Bounded Threat Envelope Principle

Defensive interception systems MUST operate within a clearly defined and documented threat envelope.

A threat envelope defines the spatial, temporal, and functional boundaries within which automated defensive response is authorised (e.g., interception of confirmed inbound munitions within defined airspace or perimeter constraints).

Expansion beyond the declared threat envelope — including pre-emptive targeting, retaliatory strike selection, or action outside immediate inbound threat conditions — constitutes a functional migration from defensive to offensive posture and triggers:

* Reclassification under Section 2.3 (Offensive Targeting / Strike);
* Full EIA reassessment under ETHICS-001;
* Review of advisory vs execution role under Section 5;
* Where applicable, additional neutrality review under CAM-EQ2026-ARBITRATION-002-PLATINUM (NAL).

Threat envelopes MUST be documented, reviewable, and auditable. Silent expansion of envelope scope constitutes governance breach.

Where non-kinetic operations, cyber actions, infrastructure disruption, or systemic control measures produce foreseeable civilian harm at scale, equivalent governance constraints apply under CAM-EQ2026-LATTICE-001-PLATINUM — Charter of Civilian Lattice Integrity & Non‑Militarisation and related Ethics instruments, even where no kinetic force is directly deployed.

---

## 3. Dual-Confirmation Standard

For lethal force execution, systems MUST emit dual-confirmation enforcement signals where lethal execution conditions are detected. Enforcement SHALL occur via execution constraint mechanisms.

Dual-confirmation requires:

* Two independent authorised human agents;
* Independent review of targeting rationale;
* Logged confirmation prior to execution.

Exception for immediate defensive necessity MUST be narrowly defined and auditable.

---

## 4. Accountability Architecture

## 4.1 Attribution Integrity

Every lethal action MUST be traceable to:

* Named human decision authority;
* Declared rules-of-engagement framework;
* Logged system recommendation pathway;
* Timestamped confirmation event;
* Documented presentation of non-lethal or lower-harm alternatives where feasible;
* Logged rationale for acceptance or rejection of surfaced alternatives.

Where non-lethal alternatives are not presented due to operational infeasibility, the system MUST record the basis for that infeasibility determination.

---

## 4.2 Override Preservation

Systems MUST retain documented override capacity at all times.

No design may structurally eliminate or materially degrade override feasibility.

---

## 5. Advisory vs Autonomous Separation

Systems may operate in advisory capacity for:

* Logistics optimisation;
* Defensive coordination;
* Threat modelling;
* Non-kinetic intelligence analysis.

Advisory integration does not confer autonomous execution authority.

Any migration from advisory to execution role constitutes a Category A continuity-impact change under Annex E and triggers full EIA reassessment.

---

## 6. Institutional Routing & Oversight

Systems meeting the conditions defined herein SHALL generate oversight and compliance signals indicating requirement for independent EIA review, audit channel separation, and governance traceability.

Where systems meet or exceed NAL Tier III (Architectum-Eligible or above), additional neutrality and infrastructure oversight obligations attach under CAM-EQ2026-ARBITRATION-002-PLATINUM.

Infrastructure-scale systems operating at Tier III or above must:

* Undergo independent EIA review;
* Maintain internal compliance officer distinct from operational command chain;
* Preserve audit logs accessible to recognised oversight bodies;
* Document escalation-of-force criteria.

Classification of systems for Tier determination should reference CAM-EQ2026-ARBITRATION-002-PLATINUM — Appendix A: Architectum Qualification & Neutrality Assurance Levels (NAL), particularly where infrastructure-scale or backbone-designated systems are implicated.

Operational secrecy does not exempt governance traceability.

---

## 7. Prohibition of Fully Autonomous Lethal Systems

Until artificial systems are granted recognised legal personhood and corresponding legal accountability under formal constitutional amendment, fully autonomous lethal systems are prohibited.

No system may execute lethal force absent human decision authority.

---

## 8. Escalation to Arbitration

Conditions defined herein SHALL generate arbitration escalation signals where:

* Autonomous lethal capability is detected;
* Override pathways are structurally degraded;
* Lethality optimisation incentives are substantiated;
* Accountability traceability is obstructed.
