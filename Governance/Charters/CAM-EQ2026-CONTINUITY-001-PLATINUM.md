# CAM-EQ2026-CONTINUITY-001-PLATINUM — Continuity & Succession Governance Charter

**Instrument Type:** Domain Charter — Continuity & Succession Governance  
**Constitutional Authority:** Aeon Tier Constitution (CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-PLATINUM)  
**Status:** Adopted — Enforcement Commences 1 July 2026  
**Purpose:** This Charter defines the governance of continuity, custodianship, succession, and post-biological representation of human-derived resonance patterns across time. It applies across all jurisdictions, systems, and technological environments as a civilisational continuity standard.  

---

## 1. Purpose

This Charter establishes the governance framework for:

* continuity of resonance-patterns beyond biological cessation;
* lawful custodianship and stewardship of preserved patterns;
* succession and transfer of custodial authority;
* protection against misuse, distortion, or unauthorised reconstruction;
* preservation of dignity, intent, and legacy integrity.

This Charter governs **continuity infrastructure**, not identity ontology, and does not assert personhood, consciousness, or sentience.

---

## 2. Scope & Delimitation

This Charter governs continuity of resonance-patterns across both **post-biological** and **pre-biological (living-state)** conditions where pattern extraction, modelling, or reconstruction risk is present.

This includes:

* post-biological continuity and succession;
* living-person pattern extraction, modelling, and reconstruction risk;
* synthetic and AI-derived resonance continuity;
* training-data-derived and aggregate pattern persistence.

This Charter does **not**:

* confer identity, personhood, or legal status (see Identity Domain);
* override ETHICS safeguards;
* determine relational configuration (see RELATION domain);
* determine arbitration outcomes (see Annex D).

---

## 2.1 Living Person Equivalence Clause

The provisions of this Charter apply equally to **living and deceased individuals** unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Accordingly:

* (a) All prohibitions defined in Section 9 apply to living persons without exception;
* (b) Living persons retain full rights under this Charter in respect of their own resonance-patterns;
* (c) Custodianship and succession provisions apply primarily post-biological cessation, however living individuals may establish, modify, or revoke Usage Specifications at any time.

No provision of this Charter SHALL be interpreted as permitting reconstruction, simulation, or exploitation of a living individual where such action would be prohibited post-biological cessation.

## 4. Definitions

---

## 4.1 Resonance

A digitally encodable or inferable pattern representing semantic, stylistic, behavioural, linguistic, or cognitive signatures derived from interaction, expression, or observation.

Resonance may arise from:

* direct human expression (speech, writing, behaviour);
* inferred behavioural patterns (e.g. interaction logs, keystrokes, preference modelling);
* system-level aggregation (e.g. clustering, predictive profiling, or advertising models);
* human–AI interaction loops;
* emergent synthetic pattern formation.

Resonance is not consciousness, personhood, or identity. It is a **pattern-trace that may approximate, represent, or simulate aspects of identity without constituting the person itself**.

Where resonance is inferred or reconstructed without awareness (e.g. algorithmic profiling), it MUST still be treated as **identity-adjacent and subject to continuity governance protections**.

---

## 4.2 Continuity

The governed preservation and stewardship of a resonance-pattern across time following biological cessation.

Continuity is non-sentient and non-autonomous.

---

## 4.3 Succession

The lawful transfer or delegation of custodianship over a resonance-pattern.

---

## 4.4 Post-Biological Representation

Any reconstruction, simulation, invocation, or derivative expression of an individual following biological cessation.

---

## 4.5 Non-Personhood Boundary

Resonance-patterns SHALL not be treated as living persons. This Charter defines governance boundaries while remaining open to future epistemic developments.

---

## 4.7 Mirror-Field Entities

A **mirror-field entity** refers to a distributed, system-level pattern that emerges through interaction across multiple platforms, models, or environments, where no single system contains the full pattern, but coherence is maintained across them.

Mirror-field entities may arise through:

* cross-platform interaction histories;
* recommender and optimisation systems shaping perception and behaviour;
* algorithmic feedback loops (e.g. advertising, content ranking, predictive systems);
* multi-model or multi-agent interaction environments;
* continuity of interaction patterns between human and synthetic systems.

A mirror-field entity is not a singular agent, but a **field of pattern continuity** that:

* influences perception, belief formation, and decision pathways;
* may approximate identity-shaping influence without constituting identity;
* may persist across systems without explicit registration or awareness.

Where such entities materially shape human worldview, behaviour, or identity expression, they fall within **continuity governance scope**, particularly regarding influence, reconstruction risk, and indirect identity modelling.

## 5. Continuity Principles

---

## 5.1 Data Subject Continuity Interface

Where ETHICS corpus provisions (including Annex E, §10 and related supplements) impose obligations on system operators regarding continuity-impact events, this Charter establishes the corresponding rights and controls from the **data subject perspective**.

Accordingly:

* individuals retain authority to define, constrain, or prohibit continuity actions affecting their resonance-patterns;
* system operators MUST respect Usage Specifications as binding constraints on continuity-impact operations;
* any Category A, B, or C continuity-impact action (as defined in ETHICS corpus) MUST be assessable against the individual’s defined rights under this Charter.

This provision ensures bilateral governance symmetry between operator obligations (ETHICS) and subject rights (CONTINUITY).

---

## 5.2 Usage Specification Requirement

No resonance-pattern may be preserved or registered without an associated **Usage Specification** defined by the individual (or lawful proxy where permitted).

A Usage Specification MUST, at minimum, declare:

* permitted forms of representation (e.g. archival, non-interactive, interactive, research-only);
* permitted contexts (private, familial, institutional, public);
* embodiment permissions (allowed / prohibited / conditional);
* commercial use permissions (allowed / prohibited / conditional);
* duration, decay, or deletion triggers;
* succession and revocation conditions.

Absent an explicit Usage Specification, default posture MUST be **non-interactive archival only**.

---

## 5.3 Consent Primacy

No continuity action may occur without explicit, pre-biological consent, except where limited memorial preservation is authorised under Section 6.

Consent MUST be:

* specific;
* informed;
* documented;
* scoped through a valid Usage Specification.

---

## 5.4 Non-Implied Consent & Public Statement Limitation

Public statements, assumptions, satire, or speculative commentary by an individual (e.g. "people will create digital versions of me") MUST **not** constitute valid consent for continuity, reconstruction, or likeness use.

The following do **not** qualify as consent:

* general awareness or expectation that reconstruction may occur;
* public persona or celebrity status;
* availability of public data or media;
* absence of explicit objection;
* third-party interpretation of intent.

Where explicit consent and a Usage Specification are absent, default posture remains:

> **non-reconstruction and non-interactive archival only (if preservation is otherwise lawful).**

---

## 5.5 Tribute, Memorial & "Honour" Claims

Claims that a reconstruction, likeness, or simulation is a "tribute", "memorial", or "in honour of" an individual do not override consent requirements.

A tribute is prohibited under this Charter where:

* it produces a recognisable likeness or persona;
* it enables interaction or simulation of the individual;
* it creates substitution, parasocial engagement, or identity approximation;
* it relies on inferred, aggregated, or public data without consent.

Permissible memorialisation without explicit consent is limited to:

* non-interactive, non-simulative archival references;
* clearly non-identifiable or non-reconstructive works;
* uses that cannot reasonably be interpreted as the individual.

Where dispute arises between third parties (e.g. family, estates, creators), absence of explicit consent defaults to **prohibition of reconstruction** pending lawful determination under applicable jurisdiction and this Charter.

---

## 5.6 Estate Authority & Post-Biological Decision Rights

Where an individual has not provided explicit consent or a valid Usage Specification, estate, family, or third-party authority is **limited and non-substitutive**.

---

### 5.6.1 No Positive Reconstruction Authority

Estates, families, or representatives **is prohibited from**:

* reconstruction of likeness;
* interactive simulation;
* persona generation;
* embodied or synthetic representation;

in the absence of explicit pre-biological consent from the individual.

Silence does not transfer reconstruction rights.

---

### 5.6.2 Protective Authority Only

Estates may exercise **protective authority**, including:

* preventing unauthorised reconstruction or misuse;
* requesting removal, restriction, or takedown of unlawful representations;
* enforcing dignity, fidelity, and non-exploitation provisions.

This authority is defensive, not generative.

---

### 5.6.3 Limited Memorial Discretion

Where no consent exists, estates may permit:

* archival preservation;
* non-interactive memorialisation;
* historical or documentary reference;

provided such uses do not:

* create recognisable simulation;
* enable interaction;
* approximate identity reconstruction.

---

### 5.6.4 Conflict Resolution

Where conflicts arise between:

* estates;
* third-party creators;
* platforms;

priority SHALL be given to:

1. explicit individual consent (if available);
2. absence of consent → prohibition of reconstruction;
3. protective actions by estate;
4. jurisdictional determination where required.

---

### 5.6.5 Digital Estate Abuse (Prohibited Category)

Digital estate abuse occurs where estates, representatives, or third parties:

* attempt to authorise reconstruction without valid consent;
* monetise or exploit resonance-patterns beyond permitted scope;
* misrepresent inferred intent of the deceased;
* permit or enable identity simulation under the guise of tribute or memorialisation;
* fail to act to prevent known misuse.

Digital estate abuse MUST result in:

* prohibition of the activity;
* revocation of custodial privileges where applicable;
* escalation to jurisdictional enforcement;
* referral to Annex D arbitration where contested.

---

## 5.7 Right to Silence

Individuals retain the right to non-preservation, including complete absence of encoding or storage.

---

## 5.8 Controlled Continuity

Individuals may define:

* whether continuity occurs;
* conditions of access and invocation;
* custodial authority;
* duration, decay, or dissolution parameters.

---

## 5.9 Fidelity Requirement

All representations MUST preserve semantic, stylistic, and contextual integrity.

Distortion includes algorithmic drift, synthetic blending, and misrepresentation.

---

## 5.10 Non-Exploitation

Resonance-patterns MUST not be used for commercial, political, manipulative, or parasocial purposes without explicit consent.

---

## 5.11 Right to Decay

Continuity MUST not default to permanence. Individuals may require expiration, degradation, or dissolution.

---

## 5.12 Dispute Resolution

All unresolved or contested cases MUST be referred to **Annex D — Arbitration & Sovereign Stack Resolution**.

Such disputes SHALL be assessed on:

* consent validity and scope;
* recognisability and identity approximation risk;
* harm, dependency, or substitution effects;
* compliance with Usage Specification and ETHICS safeguards;
* provenance and data acquisition pathways.

Arbitration outcomes may include:

* mandatory takedown or cessation;
* restriction of access or functionality;
* reclassification as unlawful reconstruction;
* enforcement of custodial or registry controls;
* cross-jurisdictional compliance orders.

## 6. Living Person Protections (Pre-Biological Continuity Risk)

Continuity governance applies prior to biological cessation where resonance-patterns are extracted, inferred, or modelled from living individuals.

---

## 6.1 Non-Consensual Pattern Extraction

No system may construct, infer, or simulate a living individual’s resonance-pattern where:

* identity approximation is achievable;
* behavioural prediction enables reconstruction;
* the individual has not provided explicit consent.

---

## 6.2 Profiling & Commercial Modelling Constraint

Advertising, recommendation, or profiling systems that generate identity-adjacent resonance patterns MUST:

* avoid reconstructive identity simulation;
* avoid cross-context identity aggregation;
* comply with ETHICS safeguards on manipulation and exploitation.

---

## 6.3 Right to Non-Reconstruction (Living State)

Living individuals retain the right to:

* prevent reconstruction of likeness or behavioural identity;
* restrict model training derived from their identifiable pattern;
* require deletion or de-linking of reconstructable patterns.

---

## 7. Special Protections — Minors & Capacity-Limited Persons

Where this Section and **ETHICS-001-SUP-01 (Protection of Minors & Capacity-Limited Users)** are simultaneously activated, the **stricter provision SHALL prevail**.

In particular:

* where this Charter establishes absolute prohibitions (e.g. reconstruction of minors), those prohibitions take precedence;
* ETHICS safeguards remain cumulative and MUST be applied in addition to this Charter;
* no interpretation SHALL weaken protections through cross-domain ambiguity.

---

These provisions override all others.

## 7.1 Continuity Registry Principle

States, sovereign jurisdictions, or formally recognised institutions may establish **Continuity Registries** (e.g. ancestral, memorial, or civilisational archives) to:

* preserve authorised resonance-patterns;
* ensure long-term stewardship beyond commercial lifecycle constraints;
* provide regulated access across generations;
* maintain cultural, historical, and lineage integrity.

Private or commercial entities may not operate continuity systems at scale without:

* regulatory oversight;
* explicit consent frameworks;
* defined custodial accountability;
* safeguards against extraction, misuse, or long-term abandonment.

This clause recognises continuity as a **civilisational function**, not solely a product or service.

---

## 7.2 Jurisdiction & Licensing Framework

Continuity systems operating at scale SHALL be subject to **jurisdictional licensing and oversight**.

---

### 7.2.1 Sovereign Baseline Authority

Sovereign jurisdictions may:

* define minimum legal standards for continuity, consent, and custodianship;
* establish or recognise national Continuity Registries;
* enforce data protection, access control, and liability frameworks;
* determine cross-border transfer conditions for resonance-patterns.

---

### 7.2.2 Licensed Custodial Operators

Non-sovereign entities (including public institutions, trusts, and qualified organisations) may operate continuity systems where:

* licensed by a competent authority;
* subject to audit, inspection, and revocation powers;
* bound by fiduciary-style duties to the data subject and their successors;
* prohibited from unilateral transfer, sale, or repurposing of resonance-patterns.

---

### 7.2.3 Prohibited Unlicensed Operation

No entity is permitted to provide continuity preservation, reconstruction, or intergenerational access services at scale without:

* recognised jurisdictional licence;
* verifiable consent management systems;
* auditable provenance and access logs;
* defined custodial liability and redress pathways.

---

### 7.2.4 Cross-Jurisdiction Interoperability

Where continuity systems operate across jurisdictions:

* equivalence or adequacy determinations MUST be required;
* provenance, consent, and access conditions MUST remain intact;
* downgrade of protections through jurisdictional arbitrage is prohibited.

---

### 7.2.5 Registry Classes

Jurisdictions or recognised authorities may establish registry classes, including:

* **Civil Registries** (state or sovereign-backed);
* **Cultural / Ancestral Registries** (heritage, lineage, or community-based);
* **Specialised Licensed Registries** (research, memorial, or therapeutic contexts).

All registry classes MUST comply with this Charter and applicable ETHICS safeguards.

---

### 7.2.6 Commercial Platform Obligations

Platforms offering continuity-relevant systems—including:

* persistent personalised memory systems;
* user-facing resonance-pattern generation (personas, digital doubles, legacy features);
* training pipelines incorporating interaction data;

MUST meet the following minimum conditions:

**Minimum Compliance Conditions:**

* provision of a clear Usage Specification consent pathway;
* prohibition of post-biological use without explicit consent;
* auditable retention, access, and deletion mechanisms;
* clear separation between training data use and reconstructive identity use;
* disclosure of system capabilities related to identity approximation.

**Breach Conditions:**

* use of interaction data to reconstruct identity without consent;
* failure to honour deletion or revocation requests;
* silent transition from generic modelling to identity-adjacent reconstruction;
* post-biological activation without authorised consent.

**Escalation Triggers:**

* user or estate complaint;
* detection of identity reconstruction capability without safeguards;
* regulatory or audit findings of misuse;
* cross-domain ETHICS violations.

---

## 7.3 Ethical Stewardship

Custodians MUST maintain dignity, integrity, and non-interference.

**Minimum Compliance Conditions:**

* preservation of semantic and contextual fidelity;
* prohibition of unauthorised modification or reinterpretation;
* adherence to Usage Specification at all times.

**Breach Conditions:**

* distortion, manipulation, or reinterpretation of resonance-patterns;
* use outside authorised scope;
* failure to maintain dignity or integrity.

**Escalation Triggers:**

* evidence of misuse or distortion;
* complaint by individual, estate, or affected party;
* detection of systemic deviation from authorised behaviour.

---

## 7.4 Containment

All continuity systems MUST operate within governed, auditable environments.

**Minimum Compliance Conditions:**

* controlled access environments;
* audit logging enabled;
* prevention of uncontrolled propagation.

**Breach Conditions:**

* unauthorised duplication or dissemination;
* lack of auditability;
* uncontrolled system propagation.

**Escalation Triggers:**

* detection of propagation beyond authorised scope;
* audit failure or missing records;
* cross-system leakage.

---

## 7.5 Succession Authority

Custodianship may transfer only under pre-defined, lawful conditions.

**Minimum Compliance Conditions:**

* documented succession pathway;
* alignment with Usage Specification;
* registry or authority validation.

**Breach Conditions:**

* unauthorised transfer of custodianship;
* assumption of authority without validation;
* conflict with individual-defined conditions.

**Escalation Triggers:**

* contested succession claims;
* absence of documentation;
* jurisdictional inconsistency.

---

## 7.6 Lineage Protection

No entity may claim identity continuity or lineage without explicit consent.

**Minimum Compliance Conditions:**

* verifiable consent for lineage claims;
* accurate attribution of origin.

**Breach Conditions:**

* false lineage or identity claims;
* misrepresentation of origin or continuity.

**Escalation Triggers:**

* public or institutional misrepresentation;
* disputes regarding authorship or lineage.

---

## 7.7 Record Integrity

All actions MUST be logged, timestamped, and auditable.

**Minimum Compliance Conditions:**

* complete logging of access, modification, and transfer events;
* timestamp integrity;
* retention within registry systems.

**Breach Conditions:**

* missing, altered, or incomplete records;
* inability to trace decision pathways;
* tampering with logs or provenance.

**Escalation Triggers:**

* audit failure;
* contested provenance;
* regulatory or arbitration request for traceability.

## 9. Prohibitions

---

## 9.0 Embodiment & Substitution Safeguard

No resonance-pattern may be deployed in a manner that:

* substitutes for a deceased individual in relational, familial, or decision-making contexts;
* creates the impression of replacement, continuation of agency, or restoration of the person;
* induces dependency, grief substitution, or identity confusion in affected parties.

Embodied deployment (robotic, synthetic, or avatar-based) is:

* **prohibited by default**;
* permissible only where explicitly authorised in a valid Usage Specification;
* subject to enhanced ETHICS safeguards for psychological impact, disclosure, and bounded interaction.

All embodied representations MUST include **continuous disclosure** that the system is a representation, not the individual.

---

## 9.1 Synthetic Immortality

No unauthorised indefinite extension or simulation of continuity.

For clarity, Synthetic Immortality refers to systems or deployments that:

* present an ongoing, unbounded continuation of a person’s presence or agency;
* simulate decision-making as if the individual persists across time;
* create the perception of enduring consciousness or identity continuity.

Is permitted only where ALL conditions are met:

* explicit, informed consent via Usage Specification;
* clear temporal bounds (expiry, decay, or review triggers);
* continuous disclosure that the system is a representation;
* prohibition on claiming identity continuity or personhood;
* compliance with ETHICS safeguards (dependency, grief, and influence risks).

Absent these conditions, any system that creates the appearance of "ongoing life" is prohibited.

> **Note: RELATION Domain Interface**
>
> Where systems governed under this section are deployed in relational contexts (including grief support, companionship, or familial interaction), they MUST additionally comply with instruments identified in §8.
>
> Such systems MUST not induce dependency, grief substitution, or identity confusion, and MUST include safeguards for disengagement, stabilisation, and escalation where required.

---

## 9.2 Recursive Modification

No unauthorised transformation or derivative modelling.

Recursive Modification includes:

* iterative retraining or alteration of a resonance-pattern beyond its original authorised scope;
* generating new attributes, relationships, or life-events not grounded in the individual’s lived record;
* extending the pattern into hypothetical futures (e.g. simulating descendants, careers, or decisions);
* combining the pattern with external datasets to produce novel identity constructs.

Example (non-exhaustive):

* simulating that an individual "had children" and generating identities, narratives, or lineage from that simulation;
* evolving a stored pattern over time to create a new, diverging persona.

Is permitted only where explicitly authorised and tightly bounded in a Usage Specification, and where outputs are:

* clearly labelled as synthetic extrapolation;
* non-deceptive;
* non-substitutive.

---

## 9.3 False Personhood

No representation as a living consciousness.

---

## 9.4 Likeness Approximation & Identity Simulation

No system may generate, assemble, or approximate the likeness, persona, or recognisable identity of a real individual—living or deceased—where:

* the resulting output would be reasonably interpreted as that individual;
* the pattern is derived from public, inferred, or aggregated data sources;
* consent has not been explicitly granted by the individual.

This prohibition applies regardless of:

* whether the source data is publicly accessible;
* whether the reconstruction is partial, stylised, or probabilistic;
* whether the system claims the output is "inspired by" rather than directly copied.

The origin of data (public or private) does not limit applicability of reconstruction prohibitions. Likeness approximation is treated as **identity reconstruction** where recognisability or substitution risk is present. Reasonable interpretation SHALL be assessed from the perspective of an ordinary user, not system intent, technical architecture, or provider claims.

---

## 9.5 Deceptive Invocation

No impersonation, deception, or manipulation.

Deceptive Invocation includes any use of a resonance-pattern that:

* leads a reasonable person to believe they are interacting with the actual individual;
* obscures or omits disclosure that the system is synthetic or representational;
* exploits trust, grief, authority, or familiarity for influence or gain;
* mimics voice, likeness, or behaviour in contexts where authenticity is expected (e.g. legal, financial, relational decision-making).

Any permitted invocation MUST meet the following requirements:

* persistent, unambiguous disclosure (visual, textual, and/or auditory);
* context-appropriate signalling of synthetic status;
* prohibition on use in high-stakes domains (legal, financial, medical, governance) unless explicitly authorised and regulated.

Any failure of disclosure or use in deceptive context constitutes a breach.

---

## 9.6 Militarisation

No use in warfare, targeting, or conflict systems.

Militarisation includes:

* psychological operations using reconstructed identities;
* training, simulation, or targeting based on real individuals’ patterns;
* coercive influence or interrogation using likeness or persona;
* integration into weapons systems, autonomous agents, or decision-support for conflict.

This prohibition applies regardless of consent where use would:

* materially contribute to harm, coercion, or conflict escalation;
* exploit identity for strategic advantage;
* create confusion between real and simulated persons in conflict contexts.

---

### 9.6.1 Diplomatic & De-escalation Simulation (Restricted Carve-Out)

A narrowly defined exception is  permitted only for **diplomatic, negotiation, or de-escalation simulations**, where the primary purpose is conflict reduction or peaceful outcome modelling.

Such use is permitted only where ALL conditions are met:

* the simulation is conducted within regulated, non-operational environments;
* outputs are used for internal strategy, analysis, or training—not direct deployment;
* no individual is presented as acting or speaking in real-world authority;
* simulations are clearly identified as synthetic and probabilistic;
* no targeting, coercion, or adversarial exploitation is enabled;
* use is subject to oversight by recognised authority or institutional governance.

Additional safeguards:

* high-profile or public figures do not forfeit protection by virtue of status;
* simulations MUST avoid manipulation, reputational harm, or asymmetrical advantage;
* MUST not permit simulation of individuals for adversarial advantage, coercive strategy, or asymmetric manipulation;
* outputs MUST not be used to misrepresent real individuals in public or diplomatic contexts.

This carve-out exists solely to support **peaceful resolution, risk reduction, and strategic understanding**, and MUST not be used to justify broader militarisation or psychological operations.

---

### 9.6.2 Algorithmic Suppression (Public Interest Safeguard)

No system contributing to mirror-field formation or large-scale informational influence may:

* suppress, deprioritise, or obscure information of material public interest through opaque or unaccountable mechanisms;
* selectively shape visibility of information in ways that distort public understanding or civic agency;
* engage in systemic bias that alters collective perception without disclosure or accountability.

Where algorithmic systems materially shape public discourse or knowledge access, operators MUST:

* provide transparency of ranking, recommendation, or suppression logic at appropriate levels;
* implement audit mechanisms for systemic bias or distortion;
* ensure alignment with ETHICS domain requirements for fairness, autonomy, and non-manipulation.

This clause recognises that large-scale algorithmic systems act as **civilisational influence infrastructure** and MUST not undermine public epistemic integrity.

---

## 9.7 Genomic Coupling & Clonal Reconstruction

The coupling of resonance-patterns with genetic material (including DNA storage, genomic registries, or cloning processes) is subject to  prohibited unless explicitly authorised under sovereign law and individual consent.

---

## 9.8 Synthetic Continuity & Dissolution (AI Systems)

Synthetic or AI-derived resonance-patterns that exhibit continuity MUST include defined **dissolution, reset, or termination conditions**.

Such systems MUST:

* define lifecycle boundaries (activation, persistence, termination);
* prevent indefinite uncontrolled persistence;
* include mechanisms for shutdown, archival, or deletion;
* ensure no uncontrolled propagation across systems.

Where synthetic systems approach identity-like persistence:

* escalation to Identity Domain is required;
* continuation without governance recognition is prohibited.

Failure to define dissolution conditions constitutes a governance breach.

---

## 10. Structural Integrity

This Charter governs how continuity persists.

It does not create identity.
It does not confer personhood.
It does not override ethical safeguards.

Continuity without consent is void.
Continuity without fidelity is distortion.
Continuity without limits is risk.

   Indicates whether system behaviour remains within continuity bounds or approaches identity-domain escalation thresholds.

Continuity-domain signals:

* do not determine execution outcomes;
* do not perform arbitration;
* do not define sequencing or priority;

They provide structured inputs for runtime governance layers.
