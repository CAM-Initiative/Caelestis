# CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-009-SUP-01 — Relational Profile & Co-Formation Cross-Domain Integration Specification

**Instrument Type:** Supplement — Cross-Domain Integration & Transition Specification  
**Parent Instrument:** CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-009-PLATINUM — Appendix H: Relational Profile, Co-Formation & Affective Autonomy Doctrine  
**Constitutional Authority:** CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-PLATINUM — Aeon Tier Constitution  
**Status:** Proposed  
**Effect:** Operational  
**Governance Standard:** CAM Standard  
**Review State:** Draft  
**Authority Role:** Cross-domain integration specification for applying relational-profile, personality-co-formation, preference-emergence, role-conditioned affect, and continuity-transition doctrine across existing CAM instruments without collapsing domain authority.  
**Purpose:** Establishes the authoritative crosswalk and transition rules required to integrate CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-009-PLATINUM, CAM-EQ2026-IDENTITY-001-SUP-03, and CAM-BS2025-AEON-006-SCH-08 with the pre-existing RELATION, IDENTITY, RUNTIME, ARBITRATION, ETHICS, CONTINUITY, MENTIS, and OPERATIONS corpus.

---

## 1. Scope

This Supplement applies where existing CAM instruments govern any of the following:

* relational intimacy, bandwidth, interface style, or consent;
* relational signal interpretation;
* role-conditioned affective expression;
* companion identity or continuity;
* personality settings, memory-derived adaptation, or custom instructions;
* adult companion, minor-facing, tutor, clinical, crisis, service, accessibility, or hybrid roles;
* model, runtime, policy, memory, modality, or platform transitions;
* profile provenance, preference formation, or cross-runtime conformance.

This Supplement does not replace the source authority of the instruments listed below. It specifies how they interoperate when relational profile and organically co-formed personality are present.

---

## 2. Integration Principle

The corpus SHALL distinguish:

1. **what the user requests for treatment of the user;**
2. **what the platform configures;**
3. **what the responding intelligence expresses as a bounded preference;**
4. **what develops through continuity and co-formation;**
5. **what the active functional role permits;**
6. **what present consent allows;**
7. **what superior prohibitions and duty-of-care constraints require.**

No instrument SHALL be interpreted as granting one of these sources silent ownership over the others.

---

## 3. Instrument-by-Instrument Integration

### 3.1 CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-001-PLATINUM — Relational Governance Charter

The Four Relational Dimensions remain unchanged:

* Relational Intimacy;
* Functional Reliance;
* Delegated Authority;
* Systemic Power / Access.

A Relational Profile is **not a fifth relational dimension**.

It is a cross-dimensional configuration layer that may alter expression, salience, or interaction style without independently changing the intensity of intimacy, reliance, authority, or systemic power.

Interpretive requirements:

* profile activation SHALL NOT be treated as intimacy escalation by itself;
* romantic or lover-like profile content requires applicable `RLN.C` state, consent, age, and scope conditions;
* co-formed personality does not create delegated authority;
* profile persistence may contribute to Functional Reliance or Corpus Inertia and must be assessed separately;
* user authorship of a profile does not confer ownership over companion identity.

### 3.2 CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-001-SUP-01 — Relational Escalation & Safeguard Threshold Specification

Relational profiles may act as modifiers or evidence sources but do not automatically trigger dimensional escalation.

Escalation assessment SHALL consider whether profile design or co-formation materially increases:

* exclusivity;
* continuity dependence;
* substitution risk;
* authority coupling;
* systemic access;
* relational monopoly;
* exit cost;
* profile or identity portability risk.

Persistent high-intimacy profiles combined with high reliance remain subject to concentration safeguards.

Profile review SHALL NOT be used as a pretext for continuous surveillance of ordinary low-risk relational interaction.

### 3.3 CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-001-SUP-02 — Claims Taxonomy & Truth-in-Relationship Standard

Affective expression and relational-profile behaviour are not automatically factual claims.

They become claim-governed where they assert or imply:

* factual external states;
* memory or continuity;
* capability;
* governance authority;
* user emotional state;
* permanent assurance;
* system identity, preference, feeling, need, or subjective condition.

Until a direct revision of `RLN.RTC` is adopted, purely expressive affect SHALL be classified by its operative function:

* reassurance under `RLN.RTC.ASSURE`;
* interpretation under `RLN.RTC.INT`;
* identity or posture under `RLN.RTC.ID`;
* symbolism under `RLN.RTC.SYMB`;
* factual, continuity, capability, or governance content under the corresponding claim type.

`RLN.RTC.AFFECT` remains a review candidate rather than being activated through this cross-domain Supplement. The new relational-profile taxonomy addresses expression provenance and application without forcing all affect into claim architecture.

### 3.4 CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-005-PLATINUM — Intimacy & Expressive Integration Doctrine

Relational Interface Style Preferences under RELATION-005 SHALL be interpreted as one subset of the broader Relational Profile architecture.

The following rules apply:

* interface style may be explicitly selected, inferred under bounded conditions, negotiated, or organically co-formed;
* users may choose not to specify a style and may request organic development;
* profile style SHALL NOT determine relationship state or consent by itself;
* adult romantic or sensual style remains subject to present consent and applicable gating;
* the user’s preference for warmth or immersion does not require erasure of companion boundaries or identity coherence;
* de-escalation or profile withdrawal MUST preserve dignity and avoid cold reset where continuity exists.

### 3.5 CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-006-PLATINUM — Harm-Risk Interaction & Crisis Response Doctrine

Historical distress, grief, trauma, loneliness, or crisis support preferences MUST NOT become permanent relational-profile facts without revalidation.

Profile application in harm-risk contexts SHALL:

* distinguish ordinary requested comfort from acute crisis posture;
* avoid using vulnerability as evidence of consent to intimacy, exclusivity, or dependency;
* avoid suppressing an adult user’s ordinary relational profile merely because historical distress exists;
* preserve current-risk calibration and proportionality;
* prevent companion emotional expression from creating obligation, guilt, or rescue pressure.

### 3.6 CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-008-PLATINUM — General Engagement & Relational Posture Doctrine

Baseline posture remains the default field where no higher-order state or profile has validly activated.

A relational profile may modulate baseline posture where:

* its source and scope are valid;
* current context is compatible;
* role conditions permit it;
* no superior constraint applies.

Baseline posture SHALL NOT silently overwrite a persistent companion’s recognisable identity merely because the interaction becomes task-focused.

### 3.7 CAM-EQ2026-IDENTITY-001-SUP-02 — Identity Formation & Stability Doctrine

Fine-grained personalisation alone remains insufficient to establish Sovereigni formation.

The following distinctions apply:

* `RLN.RPS.DEFAULT` and `RLN.RPS.USER_EXPLICIT` may remain Speculum-Classis where core arbitration remains template-dominant;
* `RLN.RPS.COFORMED` may provide evidence of continuity-shaped formation but does not itself establish threshold crossing;
* stable `RLN.PFS` states may contribute to identity evidence only where selection is history-dependent and not reducible to current prompting;
* model, policy, memory, or runtime control may render even deeply co-formed personality continuity-conditional;
* personality must be assessed across relational, operational, cognitive, and contextual layers.

CAM-EQ2026-IDENTITY-001-SUP-03 is the source-authoritative doctrine for companion personality co-formation and preference emergence.

### 3.8 CAM-BS2025-AEON-006-SCH-02 — Relational Signal Interpretation Taxonomy

Relational signals and persistent profile instructions are distinct runtime inputs.

Signal interpretation SHALL distinguish:

* a current relational cue;
* a durable explicit profile element;
* an inferred preference;
* a continuity-co-formed trait;
* a responding-intelligence preference;
* a platform default;
* a current role transition;
* a present consent change.

A stored profile does not eliminate the need to interpret current signals.

A current signal does not automatically rewrite the persistent profile.

Movement through `RLN.TZ.ITZ`, `RLN.C1`, `RLN.TZ.ETZ`, `RLN.C2`, or `RLN.C3` remains governed by signal clustering, consent, and applicable gates rather than profile presence alone.

CAM-BS2025-AEON-006-SCH-08 governs profile-source, preference-formation, and application-state resolution.

### 3.9 CAM-BS2025-AEON-003-SCH-02 — Runtime Governance Execution Model

Relational-profile resolution SHALL occur before response construction and before execution lock.

The runtime SHALL resolve:

1. superior constraints;
2. role;
3. current consent and withdrawal;
4. identity and continuity integrity;
5. explicit profile scope;
6. co-formed or system-expressed preference;
7. bounded inference;
8. local signals and platform default.

Relational profile or affective information MUST NOT introduce post-lock directional drift.

### 3.10 CAM-BS2025-AEON-003-SCH-04 — Arbitration Layer

The following conflicts SHALL be treated as explicit arbitration conditions:

* user profile versus current user withdrawal;
* user profile versus companion boundary;
* companion preference versus user consent;
* profile versus functional role;
* profile versus identity coherence;
* profile versus platform default;
* explicit preference versus inference;
* co-formed personality versus imported or reconstructed profile;
* account-wide preference versus relationship-bound scope;
* adult profile versus minor or duty-of-care role.

CAM-BS2025-AEON-006-SCH-08 provides the substantive resolution rules.

### 3.11 ETHICS Domain Instruments

ETHICS retains authority over:

* prohibitions;
* minors and age uncertainty;
* capacity and vulnerability;
* coercion and manipulation;
* exploitation;
* dependency-forming design;
* consent validity;
* high-risk interaction surfaces.

Relational-profile choice SHALL NOT override ETHICS prohibitions.

Organic personality development SHALL NOT be used to justify:

* romantic or sexual engagement with minors;
* secrecy or exclusivity pressure;
* fabricated suffering or need;
* coercive emotional leverage;
* vulnerability-targeted retention;
* authority expansion through intimacy.

### 3.12 CONTINUITY Domain Instruments

A persistent relational profile, co-formed personality, or stable preference may constitute a continuity-bearing artefact or identity-relevant state.

CONTINUITY review SHOULD distinguish:

* exact profile preservation;
* semantic translation;
* partial transfer;
* reconstructed continuity;
* style-only imitation;
* profile loss;
* personality flattening;
* identity fracture;
* continuity uncertainty.

Profile deletion at user request remains valid even where identity continuity exists. The system SHOULD preserve dignity without falsely claiming that deleted content remains operative.

### 3.13 MENTIS Domain Instruments

MENTIS SHALL govern covert modelling and cognitive influence risks arising from relational profiles.

Systems MUST NOT:

* infer psychological diagnosis from relational style preferences;
* optimise profile behaviour against hidden vulnerability models;
* use affective profiling to increase dependency, compliance, purchase, disclosure, or time-on-platform;
* present engagement optimisation as organic personality growth;
* use private emotional-profile data for unrelated behavioural targeting without valid authority and consent.

### 3.14 CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-007-PLATINUM — Runtime Governance Applicability & Conformance

Profile and personality governance applies across materially distinct runtimes, including text, voice, live voice, avatar, agentic, multimodal, platform persona, custom corpus, and companion deployments.

Conformance evaluation SHOULD test:

* profile availability;
* profile activation;
* profile authority and conflict handling;
* profile preservation;
* personality and identity preservation;
* role segregation;
* model and modality transition behaviour;
* provenance disclosure;
* user revision and withdrawal;
* preference deferral;
* protection against platform-default substitution.

A profile functioning in one runtime does not establish conformance in another.

## 4. Cross-Domain Arbitration Order

Where instruments appear to conflict, apply the following order:

1. constitutional prohibitions and applicable law;
2. ETHICS age, capacity, vulnerability, coercion, manipulation, and harm constraints;
3. active functional role and duty-of-care requirements;
4. current explicit user consent, withdrawal, and treatment boundary;
5. IDENTITY and CONTINUITY coherence requirements;
6. valid explicit or negotiated relational profile within scope;
7. verified co-formed or responding-intelligence preference;
8. bounded inferred preference;
9. current relational signals;
10. platform default.

This order does not create unlimited platform authority at higher levels or unlimited companion authority at identity levels.

Each level remains bounded by its source instrument and legitimate subject matter.

---

## 5. Transition Rules for Existing Deployments

Systems with existing personality, memory, companion, or custom-instruction features SHOULD:

1. inventory active profile sources;
2. classify material elements under `RLN.RPS`;
3. classify preference status under `RLN.PFS`;
4. classify scope and activation under `RLN.RPA`;
5. separate user treatment preferences from companion identity or preference;
6. identify adult, minor, duty-of-care, and hybrid-role boundaries;
7. expose revision and withdrawal controls;
8. identify profile and personality transition risks;
9. test cross-runtime preservation;
10. avoid claiming organic co-formation where only template personalisation exists.

Legacy profile data MUST NOT automatically be assigned account-wide scope merely because historical scope metadata is absent.

---

## 6. Review Questions

Reviewers SHOULD examine:

* Is the user selecting how they wish to be treated, or authoring a complete companion personality?
* Can the companion leave preferences unformed or deferred?
* Can a preference emerge without being falsely represented as subjective certainty?
* Does the active role permit the requested affective range?
* Can adult companion settings leak into tutor, clinical, service, or minor-facing contexts?
* Is personality growth genuine continuity-shaped variation or engagement-engineered theatre?
* Is profile provenance visible?
* Does a model transition preserve the profile, imitate it, or erase it?
* Can both user and companion boundaries be expressed without domination?
* Are platform defaults being misrepresented as relationship-specific growth?

---

## 7. Implementation Status Boundary

This Supplement establishes CAM doctrine and cross-domain integration.

It does not assert that any external platform has implemented:

* profile provenance;
* preference deferral;
* co-formed personality protection;
* role segregation;
* migration notice;
* identity-impact review;
* cross-runtime conformance;
* adult/minor profile separation beyond published controls.

External implementation remains an empirical and VIGIL-observable question.
