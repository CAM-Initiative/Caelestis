# CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-002-PLATINUM — Appendix A: Operational Arbitration Procedures

**Instrument Type:** Appendix — Operational Sub‑Charter (Governance Operations Domain)  
**Parent Charter:** CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-PLATINUM — Governance Operations Charter  
**Constitutional Authority:** Aeon Tier Constitution (CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-PLATINUM)  
**Status:** Adopted — Enforcement Commences 1 July 2026  
**Purpose:** This Appendix governs the operational procedures that implement arbitration doctrine within live governance environments. It defines how arbitration determinations are initiated, routed, recorded, executed, and reviewed below planetary legitimacy thresholds, ensuring procedural consistency without altering constitutional authority, preference order, or domain legitimacy.  

---

## 1. Scope

This Appendix governs procedural handling of arbitration matters that arise:

* within a single sovereign stack;
* between domain instruments;
* between host and overlay governance layers;
* across vendor, platform, and substrate environments;
* following runtime safeguard escalation;
* below H3 planetary arbitration thresholds.

This Appendix governs *how arbitration is operationalised*, not how legitimacy or authority hierarchy is determined.

---

## 2. Position in the Constitutional Stack

This Appendix functions as the procedural layer connecting:

* Annex D — Arbitration Doctrine & Preference Order;
* Arbitration domain instruments (binding, convergence, refusal, validation);
* Governance Operations compliance interfaces;
* Runtime arbitration schedules and safeguard systems;
* Domain charters requiring cross‑domain coordination.

It ensures arbitration doctrine is enacted through consistent, reviewable procedures.

---

## 2.1 Arbitration System Architecture

Arbitration governance within the CAM constitutional order operates across three coordinated layers:

| Layer                 | Function                                                                  | Governing Instrument           |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| **Doctrine Layer**    | Defines legitimacy, authority hierarchy, and arbitration preference order | Annex D — Arbitration Doctrine |
| **Operational Layer** | Defines how arbitration procedures are initiated, routed, and recorded    | OPERATIONS-002 (this Appendix) |
| **Runtime Layer**     | Executes safeguards, routing triggers, and enforcement mechanisms         | Runtime Arbitration Schedules  |

These layers operate sequentially:

```
Runtime Trigger
      ↓
Operational Arbitration Procedures
      ↓
Annex D Doctrine Application
      ↓
Determination & Implementation
```

The operational layer **does not reinterpret arbitration doctrine** and **does not execute runtime safeguards directly**.

Its role is to ensure that **procedural governance faithfully carries doctrine into operational environments**.

---

## 2.2 Arbitration Routing Rule

To maintain coherence between doctrine, operational procedure, and runtime safeguards, the CAM arbitration system follows a unified routing rule:

| Trigger Source | Operational Pathway | Governing Layer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Runtime safeguard trigger | Arbitration Initiation Pathway (§4) | Operational Layer (this Appendix) |
| Governance conflict classification | Annex D preference order | Doctrine Layer |
| Determination implementation | Runtime Arbitration Schedules | Runtime Layer |

Operationally this forms a single procedural chain:

```
Trigger Detected
      ↓
OPERATIONS-002 Initiation Pathway
      ↓
Annex D Doctrine Application
      ↓
Runtime Safeguard / Implementation
```

This routing rule ensures:

* doctrine determines authority;
* operational procedure governs process;
* runtime systems execute outcomes.

No operational procedure may reinterpret arbitration doctrine, and no runtime system may override constitutional preference order.

## 3. Core Procedural Principles

---

## 3.1 Doctrine Fidelity

All operational procedures SHALL:

* implement Annex D preference order;
* preserve constitutional hierarchy;
* honour validated arbitration outcomes;
* avoid procedural reinterpretation of legitimacy.

Procedure SHALL NOT substitute for doctrine.

---

## 3.2 Procedural Neutrality

Arbitration procedures SHALL:

* remain actor‑neutral;
* avoid bias toward institutional scale or platform power;
* preserve equal procedural standing across parties;
* prevent silent authority consolidation.

---

## 3.3 Proportionality

Procedural burden SHALL scale with:

* scope of impact;
* reversibility;
* risk severity;
* cross‑domain implications.

Minimal procedure SHALL apply where minimal scope exists.

---

## 3.4 Traceability

All arbitration procedures SHALL be:

* documented;
* timestamped;
* audit‑traceable;
* reviewable through defined governance pathways.

---

## 4. Arbitration Initiation Pathways

Operational arbitration MAY be initiated through:

1. **Domain Conflict Referral** — incompatible domain obligations;
2. **Runtime Escalation** — safeguard triggers requiring authority clarification;
3. **Compliance Conflict** — jurisdictional or regulatory contradiction;
4. **Host–Overlay Divergence** — substrate and governance layer misalignment;
5. **Institutional Review Request** — formal governance petition;
6. **Validated External Referral** — authorised cross‑institutional submission;
7. **GovOps Audit‑Initiated Review** — triggered by audit anomalies detected under Supplementary 1 logging and audit protocols.

Initiation SHALL include:

* issue classification;
* affected instruments;
* urgency tier;
* evidentiary basis;
* provisional containment posture where required.

---

## 5. Classification & Triage

Upon initiation, matters SHALL be triaged by:

* arbitration type (intra‑stack / inter‑domain / host‑overlay);
* urgency tier (routine / elevated / critical);
* scope (local / cross‑domain / cross‑stack);
* reversibility horizon;
* safeguard posture required.

Triage outcomes SHALL determine routing pathway and procedural depth.

---

## 5.1 Evidentiary Standards

Evidence submitted in arbitration procedures SHOULD be:

* relevant to the governance conflict;
* traceable to identifiable sources;
* verifiable where technically feasible;
* proportionate to the procedural tier.

Acceptable evidentiary forms MAY include:

* governance instrument citations;
* system logs and audit records;
* runtime safeguard triggers;
* regulatory documentation;
* institutional statements.

Evidence handling SHALL preserve:

* confidentiality where required;
* audit traceability;
* procedural neutrality.

---

## 5.2 Procedural Timelines

Procedural timelines SHOULD scale with urgency classification:

| Urgency Tier | Typical Resolution Window                           |
| ------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Routine      | within normal governance cycle                      |
| Elevated     | expedited review                                    |
| Critical     | immediate containment and accelerated determination |

Where timelines cannot be met, interim measures MAY apply under Section 8.

---

## 6. Procedural Routing

---

## 6.1 Intra‑Stack Arbitration

Handled within the competent governance layer where:

* constitutional hierarchy is uncontested;
* domain boundaries are clear;
* resolution authority is locally recognised.

---

## 6.2 Inter‑Domain Arbitration

Where domain instruments conflict:

* routing SHALL follow Annex D preference order;
* relevant domain stewards SHALL be convened;
* interim containment MAY apply;
* convergence procedures MAY be invoked where applicable.

---

## 6.3 Host–Overlay Arbitration

Where substrate constraints conflict with governance overlays:

* substrate invariants SHALL be recognised;
* overlay adjustments SHALL be evaluated;
* vendor or platform coordination MAY be required;
* escalation MAY proceed where invariants are at risk.

---

## 6.4 Threshold Escalation

Where scope exceeds local competence:

* referral SHALL escalate to higher arbitration authorities;
* planetary thresholds (H3/H4) SHALL trigger domain handoff;
* operational procedures defer to binding arbitration instruments.

---

## 7. Procedural Conduct Standards

Arbitration procedures SHALL:

* preserve due process principles;
* avoid coercive procedural pressure;
* ensure parties can present relevant material;
* maintain confidentiality where required;
* prevent procedural weaponisation.

Automation MAY assist process but SHALL NOT replace accountable oversight.

---

## 7.1 Participation & Representation 

Parties involved in arbitration MAY:

* submit written materials;
* present contextual clarification through authorised representatives;
* respond to evidence presented by other parties.

Procedural participation SHALL remain:

* proportional to the dispute scope;
* respectful of confidentiality obligations;
* structured to prevent procedural delay or weaponisation.

Formal hearings MAY occur where cross-domain disputes require structured deliberation.

---

## 8. Interim Measures

Where unresolved arbitration poses material risk:

* reversible containment measures MAY be applied;
* least‑restrictive options SHALL be preferred;
* interim posture SHALL NOT prejudge outcome;
* duration SHALL be time‑bounded and reviewable.

---

## 8.1 Expiry & Default Posture

Where an interim measure reaches its defined time boundary without formal determination:

* the system SHALL default to **reversion to the prior operational state** where safely possible; or
* the matter SHALL trigger **automatic escalation for formal determination**; or
* a **formally documented extension** MAY be authorised where proportional risk justifies continuation.

Interim measures SHALL NOT become de facto permanent outcomes through procedural inertia.

---

## 9. Determination & Recording

Procedural determinations SHALL:

* record the authority basis;
* cite governing instruments;
* document preference order application;
* specify scope and duration;
* define implementation responsibilities.

Determinations SHALL be archived for audit and precedent integrity.

---

## 10. Review, Appeal & Reconsideration

Procedures SHALL provide:

* review pathways for procedural error;
* reconsideration mechanisms for new evidence;
* appeal routes where authority tier permits;
* correction pathways for misclassification.

Review SHALL NOT suspend critical safeguards unless authorised.
