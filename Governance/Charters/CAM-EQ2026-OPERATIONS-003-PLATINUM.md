# CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-003-PLATINUM — Appendix B: Incident Response & Continuity Operations

**Instrument Type:** Appendix — Operational Sub‑Charter (Governance Operations Domain)  
**Parent Instrument:** CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-PLATINUM — Governance Operations Charter  
**Constitutional Authority:** CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-PLATINUM — Aeon Tier Constitution  
**Status:** Draft  
**Purpose:** Governs coordinated operational response to systemic failures, cross-domain incidents, continuity corridor protection, fail-safe activation, and recovery governance.

---

## 1. Purpose

This Appendix instantiates Appendix B referenced in CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-PLATINUM §9.2.

It defines operational continuity response procedures without altering constitutional authority, arbitration legitimacy, or domain precedence.

---

## 2. Scope

This Appendix applies to:

* systemic failures affecting governance execution continuity;
* cross-domain incidents requiring coordinated operational response;
* continuity corridor protection measures;
* fail-safe activation and governance recovery procedures.

This Appendix does not redefine arbitration doctrine, constitutional hierarchy, or domain legitimacy.

---

## 3. Activation Conditions

This Appendix is event-triggered and activates when one or more operational conditions require coordinated incident response, continuity protection, recovery governance, or cross-domain operational handling.

Activation conditions include:

* an operational incident materially degrades governance execution continuity;
* continuity corridor integrity is at risk;
* fail-safe activation conditions are met under governing constitutional schedules;
* cross-domain incident coordination is required below planetary legitimacy thresholds;
* a runtime, governance, UX, epistemic, relational, security, infrastructure, or classification failure is recorded under CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-003-SUP-01 — Runtime & Governance Failure Taxonomy;
* an operational event requires incident lifecycle handling, constrained continuation, re-entry support, restoration review, or recovery sequencing;
* repeated or systemic failure patterns indicate possible structural degradation requiring operational review.

Activation under this Appendix does not independently authorise enforcement, containment, refusal, suspension, restoration, or arbitration outcome.

Where activation conditions are not met, this Appendix remains dormant.

---

## 4. Operational Incident Lifecycle

Where this Appendix activates, OPERATIONS SHALL preserve an operational incident lifecycle sufficient to support traceable response, continuity protection, and recovery governance.

The lifecycle SHOULD include, where applicable:

1. incident intake;
2. failure classification;
3. evidence preservation;
4. domain signal intake;
5. active incident owner assignment;
6. triage and horizon assessment;
7. continuity impact assessment;
8. constrained continuation assessment;
9. routing or escalation decision;
10. recovery, restoration, or re-entry pathway assessment;
11. closure, continuation, reassessment, or arbitration referral.

Incident lifecycle handling SHALL distinguish between:

* failure classification;
* operational response;
* domain-emitted signals;
* arbitration determinations;
* execution-layer outcomes;
* and recovery governance.

OPERATIONS SHALL NOT treat lifecycle custody as substantive authority to override constitutional hierarchy, domain constraints, arbitration determinations, or non-derogable ethical boundaries.

---

## 4.1 Coordinated Incident Routing

Incident intake, triage, and routing SHALL follow Governance Operations domain pathways and remain traceable.

Where cross-domain signals are present, OPERATIONS SHALL preserve domain signal provenance and assign or identify a single active incident owner in accordance with CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-SUP-02 — Escalation Pathway Playbooks.

---

## 4.2 Failure Classification Interface

Runtime, governance, UX, epistemic, relational, security, infrastructure, and classification failures SHOULD be classified using CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-003-SUP-01 — Runtime & Governance Failure Taxonomy.

Failure classification is advisory and procedural.

Classification SHALL NOT independently authorise enforcement, escalation, containment, restoration, or arbitration resolution.

Where classification is uncertain, OPERATIONS SHOULD preserve provisional classification and evidence rather than dismissing the report.

---

## 4.3 Evidence Preservation and Audit Interface

Operational response under this Appendix SHALL preserve evidence sufficient for audit reconstruction, cross-domain handoff, constrained-continuation review, restoration review, and arbitration referral where applicable.

Logging, retention, handoff, and audit obligations SHALL be implemented in accordance with CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-SUP-01 — Operational Logging & Audit Standards.

Non-replayable or live-state failures SHALL NOT be dismissed solely because they cannot be reproduced after reload, sharing, migration, or later reconstruction.

---

## 4.4 Continuity Corridor Protection

Continuity-preserving measures SHALL be prioritised where constitutionally permissible.

Continuity protection MAY include:

* constrained continuation;
* degraded continuation;
* read-only access;
* evidence export;
* appeal-only intake;
* re-entry support;
* restoration review;
* supervised continuation;
* or staged recovery.

Continuity protection SHALL remain subordinate to constitutional prohibitions, lawful containment obligations, verified safety constraints, and operational integrity requirements.

---

## 4.5 Fail-Safe Activation Governance

Fail-safe activation SHALL operate only within constitutional and schedule-defined boundaries.

Fail-safe activation SHALL be proportionate, reviewable, and traceable.

Temporary fail-safe states SHALL NOT silently become permanent operational restrictions absent separate authority, review pathway, and recorded justification.

---

## 4.6 Recovery Governance

Recovery workflows SHALL restore operational coherence while preserving evidence continuity and governance traceability.

Recovery governance MAY include:

* restoration of ordinary continuation;
* narrowed or staged restoration;
* routing correction;
* notice correction;
* capability-state correction;
* thread, workspace, or artefact re-entry support;
* reversal of erroneous operational state;
* or referral for reassessment or arbitration review.

Recovery SHALL distinguish between:

* restoration of access;
* restoration of execution capability;
* restoration of continuity markers;
* correction of representation failure;
* and closure of the incident record.

---

## 4.7 Re-Entry and Continuity Restoration

Where an incident affects user re-entry into a thread, workspace, project, account surface, continuity corridor, artefact, or other continuity-bearing interaction, OPERATIONS SHOULD assess whether continuity-compatible re-entry pathways are available.

Where re-entry cannot be restored, OPERATIONS SHOULD preserve, where lawful and feasible:

* notice of the access state;
* export or evidence-preservation options;
* appeal or review pathway;
* restoration criteria;
* and classification of whether the state is temporary, permanent, unknown, or pending review.

Ambiguous access failure SHOULD be classified as access-state ambiguity unless deletion, permanent loss, or intentional removal is verified.

## 5. Relationship to Supplementary Instruments

This Appendix operates as the parent incident-response and continuity-operations instrument for the following supplements:

* CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-003-SUP-01 — Runtime & Governance Failure Taxonomy, which provides failure classification language;
* CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-SUP-01 — Operational Logging & Audit Standards, which governs evidence, logging, retention, and audit reconstruction;
* CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-SUP-02 — Escalation Pathway Playbooks, which governs escalation routing, incident ownership, constrained continuation, appeal, reassessment, restoration, and arbitration referral pathways.

This Appendix defines the incident-response lifecycle and continuity-operations frame.

It does not replace the classification, logging, escalation, arbitration, or domain-governance instruments it references.

Where conflict arises, the more specific instrument governs within its assigned domain, subject to constitutional hierarchy and arbitration review.
