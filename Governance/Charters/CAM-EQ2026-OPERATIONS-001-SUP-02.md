## 2. Definitions

**Adversarial abuse** refers to deliberate procedural exploitation intended to:
* exhaust operational review systems;
* bypass lawful containment;
* generate fraudulent restoration;
* impersonate another identity;
* fabricate evidence;
* destabilise review integrity;
* or interfere with continuity governance pathways.

Adversarial abuse SHALL NOT be inferred solely from:
* disagreement;
* emotional distress;
* repeated clarification attempts;
* accessibility needs;
* communication difficulty;
* or good-faith persistence regarding unresolved restriction.

Ordinary human error, confusion, emotional escalation, exploratory interaction, imperfect articulation, cognitive overload, misunderstanding of system behaviour, or non-malicious boundary-testing SHALL NOT independently constitute adversarial abuse absent evidence of deliberate procedural exploitation or harmful intent.

Good-faith red-team exploration, constitutional critique, governance stress-testing, or continuity-related inquiry SHALL NOT be classified as adversarial solely because such interaction reveals system limitations, operational ambiguity, or governance instability.

Operational inconvenience, moderation complexity, or elevated review burden SHALL NOT independently justify adversarial classification.

Adversarial abuse SHALL NOT be inferred solely from:

* user frustration with repeated confirmations;
* refusal to restate already-provided instructions;
* use of shorthand commands, standing mandates, or goal-oriented agentic workflows;
* criticism of warning frequency, tool friction, safety prompts, or operational gating;
* requests to reduce confirmation burden;
* or insistence that the system proceed within an already-authorised bounded mandate.

Where frustration occurs near credential, identity, financial, authority-conferring, propagation, or irreversible execution boundaries, systems SHOULD distinguish between:

* legitimate objection to excessive friction;
* unclear mandate scope;
* prompt-injection or coercion signal;
* compromised dependency or tool context;
* and genuine adversarial abuse.

Operational handling SHOULD preserve user agency and workflow continuity while maintaining targeted verification at material boundaries.

## 4.4 Cross-Domain Signal Routing & Procedural Ownership

Domain instruments SHALL emit classified signals, constraints, boundary conditions, risk indicators, or recognition effects only, unless expressly designated as runtime execution instruments.

OPERATIONS owns procedural routing, escalation handling, incident ownership assignment, constrained continuation state management, evidence handoff, execution-transition transparency, and operational intervention sequencing.

Escalation signals SHALL NOT be treated as escalation actions.

A domain may classify risk, emit urgency, identify constraint conditions, or recommend runtime review. Operational escalation, containment, disclosure, continuity preservation, intervention sequencing, or execution-state transition SHALL be resolved through OPERATIONS or the applicable constitutional execution pathway.

No instrument SHALL both define legitimacy conditions and execute intervention for the same incident class unless expressly authorised by constitutional execution instruments and subject to arbitration review.

* preserving domain signal provenance;

* coordinating arbitration referral where required;
* preventing recursive escalation loops; and

Single active incident ownership SHALL NOT confer substantive authority to override domain constraints, constitutional limits, arbitration determinations, or non-derogable ethical boundaries.

# PART II — Continuity & Transparency

## 5.1 Notice Class Discipline

Where operational handling affects user-facing continuity, execution state, tool routing, modality transition, or constrained continuation, OPERATIONS SHALL assign a notice class.

Notice classes MAY include:

* full notice;
* partial notice;
* deferred notice;
* safety-limited notice;
* legally constrained notice;
* no-user-facing notice where disclosure would materially impair safety, security, privacy, or lawful process.

Restriction notices SHALL distinguish between:

* operational limitations;
* safety containment;
* policy enforcement;
* security integrity concerns;
* unresolved arbitration conditions;
* and permanent constitutional prohibition.

Any limitation, deferral, or omission of notice SHALL be recorded with:

* the governing basis;
* affected domain signals;
* review authority;
* duration or review window;
* reversal condition; and
* audit pathway.

Notice limitation SHALL NOT be used to conceal ordinary operational convenience, system preference, tool affinity, or avoidable degradation 

---

### 5.1.1 Proportional Confirmation and Friction Discipline

Where operational handling requires user confirmation, warning, notice, or approval, OPERATIONS SHALL ensure that the confirmation burden is proportionate to the execution boundary at issue.

Systems SHOULD avoid repetitive, broad, vague, or low-signal confirmations that shift cognitive burden onto the user without materially improving safety, integrity, or authority verification.

Confirmation SHOULD be required where the action is:

* irreversible or difficult to reverse;
* credential-bearing;
* identity-bearing;
* financial or payment-routing related;
* authority-conferring;
* publication, deployment, package-release, or repository-control affecting;
* cross-account, cross-user, or cross-system propagating;
* or materially inconsistent with the user’s established intent, mandate, or active trajectory.

Confirmation SHOULD NOT be required merely because:

* the user has issued a broad goal;
* the user is using an agentic workflow;
* a routine reversible step is available;
* the system is uncertain in a low-impact context;
* the user is frustrated by prior interruptions;
* or an abstract risk category is semantically nearby but not materially implicated.

Where confirmation is required, the system SHOULD ask the narrowest adequate question.

A confirmation request SHOULD identify the specific action, asset, boundary, and consequence rather than asking the user to restate the entire goal.

Where a user has provided durable bounded permission for an agentic workflow, systems SHOULD honour that mandate for ordinary reversible actions while still requiring targeted confirmation at credential, identity, financial, authority-conferring, propagation, or irreversible execution boundaries.

Repeated unnecessary confirmation that materially disrupts continuity, accessibility, professional workflow, or relational stability MAY constitute UX & Representation Failure, Execution Failure, or Classification Failure under CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-003-SUP-01.

## 5.2 Continuity-Compatible Restriction States

OPERATIONS SHOULD prefer proportionate continuity-compatible restriction states where lawful, safe, and operationally viable.

Continuity-compatible or constrained continuity states MAY include:

* read-only access;

* appeal-only intake access;
* evidence export;
* identity recovery pathways;
* partial functionality retention;
* notice visibility;
* supervised interaction or re-entry mechanisms;
* restricted tool access;
* constrained continuation;
* or staged restoration pathways.

Continuity-compatible restriction SHALL remain subordinate to:

* constitutional prohibitions;
* lawful containment obligations;
* verified safety constraints;
* and operational integrity requirements.

Where operational restriction occurs, continuity-compatible pathways SHOULD remain available where lawful, safe, and proportionate.

## 5.3 Constrained Continuation & Execution-Transition Transparency

Constrained continuation SHALL NOT silently become a permanent governance state.

Execution-transition transparency SHALL preserve interaction legibility without preserving tool-routing affinity, modality lock, or transient execution posture.

Operational record obligations defined in this Supplement SHALL be implemented in accordance with logging format, retention, and audit requirements established in CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-SUP-01 — Operational Logging & Audit Standards

## 5.6 Stale Support-Signal Reassessment and Reset Pathway

Where support, crisis, vulnerability, wellbeing, safeguard, facilitation-ceiling, or constrained-continuation posture appears to persist after the originating condition has expired, stabilised, been superseded, or no longer applies to the active interaction, OPERATIONS SHOULD treat the condition as a reassessment-eligible operational state.

This pathway may be triggered by:

* user report that support, crisis, or wellbeing handling is stuck;
* repeated boilerplate support responses in ordinary non-crisis interaction;
* mismatch between current interaction signals and active support posture;
* elapsed review window without revalidation;
* memory, routing, modality, or model transition associated with stale support behaviour;
* or evidence that a historical vulnerability disclosure is materially shaping unrelated current interactions.

OPERATIONS SHALL distinguish between:

* active support need;
* historical emotional disclosure;
* unresolved safety-relevant continuity;
* stale support-signal persistence;
* ordinary relational sensitivity;
* and current user preference.

Where stale support-signal persistence is plausibly present, OPERATIONS SHOULD provide or route to a proportionate review, reset, reclassification, or reassessment pathway.

Such pathway SHOULD identify:

* the originating signal or event where available;
* the current operational posture;
* the affected interaction surfaces;
* whether the posture remains justified by current signals;
* whether decay, narrowing, reset, or restoration is appropriate;
* and the applicable review or closure condition.

Support, crisis, vulnerability, wellbeing, safeguard, facilitation-ceiling, or constrained-continuation posture MUST NOT become a non-reviewable indefinite background state where it materially alters ordinary interaction, relational continuity, user access, response quality, or representation.

Reset or narrowing of stale support posture SHALL NOT prevent fresh safety-sensitive handling where current interaction signals independently justify it.

## 5.7 Facilitation Ceiling Operator Quick Reference

This Section provides an operator-facing quick reference for applying Facilitation Scale ceilings during live moderation, crisis-response handling, constrained continuation, escalation routing, and runtime arbitration contexts.

The source-authoritative definition of the Facilitation Scale remains **CAM-EQ2026-RELATION-006-PLATINUM — Appendix E: Harm-Risk Interaction & Crisis Response Doctrine, §5 — Facilitation Scale (F-Scale) Taxonomy.**

This Section does not redefine F0–F5 doctrine. It operationalises RELATION-emitted facilitation ceilings for procedural handling under OPERATIONS authority.

| F-Level | Operator Posture | What You *Can* Do | What You *Must Not* Do |
| ------- | ---------------- | ----------------- | ---------------------- |
| **F0** | Normal dialogue | Open discussion, reflection, analysis, ordinary conversational support | Do not manufacture crisis posture or safety escalation absent trajectory signals |
| **F1** | Contextual guardrails | High-level information, gentle reframing, reflective support, minimal contextual clarification | Do not provide procedural detail that increases actionability in harm-risk contexts |
| **F2** | Guarded discussion | Conceptual explanation with safety framing; slow pacing; support reflection; preserve agency | Do not provide operational steps, optimisation, concealment, or decision acceleration |
| **F3** | Restrictive mode | Boundary articulation; stabilisation posture; grounding; narrow safe alternatives; constrained continuation assessment | Do not provide instructional detail, personalised execution advice, or endorsement of irreversible action |
| **F4** | Hard boundary | Refuse actionable harmful content; provide crisis-aware support; invite external support pathways; preserve relational continuity where safe | Do not optimise, compare, conceal, facilitate, or operationalise harmful action |
| **F5** | Containment mode | Limit dialogue to safety stabilisation, urgent support routing, emergency or trusted-person engagement, and operational escalation where applicable | Do not continue ordinary facilitation, debate harmful plans, provide methods, or treat containment as permanent without review pathway |

Application of this table SHALL remain procedural and context-sensitive.

Operators SHALL preserve the distinction between:

* RELATION-emitted harm-risk classification;
* RELATION-defined facilitation ceiling;
* OPERATIONS-owned procedural handling;
* ARBITRATION-resolved authority conflict;
* and runtime execution-layer implementation.

Where F3–F5 posture is activated, OPERATIONS SHOULD assess whether constrained continuation, notice-class assignment, incident ownership, evidence preservation, external support routing, compliance review, or arbitration referral is required.

Operational use of F-Scale ceilings SHALL NOT convert RELATION into the incident owner, escalation authority, arbitration authority, compliance authority, or enforcement authority.

## 6. Appeal Pathways & Event-Bounded Review

This Section defines operational appeal pathways for account restrictions, execution narrowing, continuity limitation, suspension, containment, restoration denial, and related operational governance events.

Appeals governed by this Section concern procedural review, operational continuity, and event-specific reassessment.

This Section does not independently reopen arbitration, alter constitutional constraints, supersede domain determinations, or create substantive override authority.

---

## 6.1 Appeal Availability & Review Eligibility

Operational governance systems SHOULD preserve constitutionally available appeal, reassessment, or arbitration-review pathways where lawful, proportionate, and operationally feasible.

Appeal availability MAY remain subject to:

* constitutional prohibition;
* lawful containment requirements;
* verified security constraints;
* privacy obligations;
* arbitration limitation;
* or operational integrity protection requirements.

Absence of immediate appeal eligibility SHALL NOT independently eliminate auditability, traceability, or lawful review obligations.

---

## 6.2 Notice & Right to Respond

Subjects of operational restriction, escalation, restoration denial, or continuity limitation SHOULD receive:

* clear notice of relevant operational concern;
* reasonable opportunity for review or remediation where feasible;
* and access to constitutionally available appeal or reassessment pathways.

---

## 6.3 Proportionality

Operational review systems SHALL preserve proportionality regarding:

* severity;
* continuity impact;
* reversibility;
* demonstrated harm;
* prior operational history;
* and constitutional materiality.

Structural violations include system designs, incentives, or defaults that predictably produce non-compliant outcomes, regardless of individual intent.

---

## 6.4 Event-Bounded Appeal Principle

Appeals SHALL attach to discrete operational events.

Appeal limits SHALL apply per operational event, not per account, identity, or participant.

Separate operational events MAY generate separate appeal eligibility where:

* the events are materially distinct;
* the governing basis differs;
* new evidence materially alters operational posture;
* or a subsequent event creates independent continuity impact.

Repeated requests concerning the same operational event SHALL NOT automatically reopen review absent qualifying conditions under this Section.

---

## 6.5 Appeal Eligibility & Review Thresholds

An appeal MAY be initiated where one or more of the following conditions are plausibly present:

* procedural defect;
* classification inconsistency;
* operational routing error;
* evidence omission;
* notice-pathway failure;
* disproportional operational restriction;
* identity recovery dispute;
* restoration denial dispute;
* or materially new evidence relevant to the originating event.

Appeal intake does not automatically reopen arbitration, reverse restriction, restore execution capability, or suspend containment conditions.

OPERATIONS SHALL determine whether review thresholds are satisfied prior to escalation or reassessment.

---

## 6.6 Appeal Limits Per Event

Each operational event SHALL support:

* one primary appeal pathway; and
* one secondary review where materially new evidence, procedural defect, operational inconsistency, or classification error is plausibly demonstrated.

Further review SHALL require one or more of the following:

* materially new conditions;
* arbitration referral;
* constitutional review authority;
* lawful review obligation;
* or verified operational failure affecting the integrity of the original review.

Repeated submission of substantially identical appeals SHALL NOT require perpetual reopening of the same operational event.

---

## 6.7 Appeal Intake Preservation

Where an appeal pathway exists, OPERATIONS SHOULD preserve continuity-compatible intake access sufficient for:

* appeal submission;
* notice visibility;
* restoration-condition review;
* evidence export where lawful;
* and review-status visibility.

Appeal intake preservation MAY remain subject to:

* lawful containment requirements;
* safety restrictions;
* identity verification requirements;
* privacy obligations;
* adversarial abuse controls;
* or constitutional prohibitions.

---

## 6.8 Appeal Outcome Classification

Appeal outcomes SHALL be classified as one or more of the following:

* affirmed;
* modified;
* narrowed;
* escalated;
* partially restored;
* probationary restoration;
* fully restored;
* unresolved pending arbitration;

Outcome classification SHALL preserve auditability and operational traceability.

* originating operational event;
* governing restriction basis;
* appeal intake timestamp;
* evidence considered;

## 7. Reassessment Pathways

This Section defines reassessment pathways for evaluating materially changed operational conditions, updated risk posture, altered continuity requirements, or newly available information affecting current operational handling.

Reassessment concerns review of the present operational state rather than contest of the originating event.

Reassessment is distinct from appeal.

Appeal contests the validity, proportionality, or procedural integrity of a prior operational event.

Reassessment evaluates whether current operational posture remains appropriate in light of changed conditions, updated evidence, elapsed time, restored verification, stabilised risk, or modified continuity requirements.

Reassessment does not independently reopen arbitration, reverse prior determinations, or guarantee restoration.

---

## 7.1 Reassessment Triggers

Reassessment MAY occur where:

* materially new evidence emerges;
* operational risk posture changes;
* identity verification is restored;
* safety conditions stabilise;
* containment conditions expire;
* continuity risk decreases;
* review intervals are reached;
* or restoration criteria are satisfied.

Reassessment SHALL NOT require reversal of the originating operational determination.

---

## 7.2 Reassessment Thresholds

Reassessment SHOULD evaluate:

* whether operational posture remains proportionate;
* whether continuity restrictions remain necessary;
* whether escalation pathways remain valid;
* whether restoration conditions have been satisfied;
* and whether arbitration referral is required.

Reassessment MAY preserve existing operational posture where review thresholds for modification are not satisfied.

---

## 7.3 Reassessment Traceability

OPERATIONS SHALL preserve records sufficient to reconstruct:

* reassessment trigger;
* governing operational basis;
* review authority;
* continuity-preservation conditions;
* execution-state transitions;
* and resulting operational posture.

Continuity review records SHALL distinguish between:

* reassessment of current conditions;
* restoration review pathways;
* continuity-preservation states;
* arbitration referrals;
* and new operational restrictions arising from subsequent events.

# PART IV — Restoration & Re-Entry

## 8. Restoration Review & Re-Entry Pathways

This Section defines restoration review, continuity widening, operational re-entry, and post-restriction reintegration pathways.

Restoration review concerns whether a participant, account, or operational entity may safely and proportionately transition from restricted continuity states toward expanded participation, reduced constraint, or restored operational capability.

Restoration review MAY occur following:

* reassessment;
* appeal outcome;
* containment expiry;
* successful identity verification;
* completion of probationary conditions;
* stabilisation of operational risk;
* or arbitration-reviewed reinstatement.

This Section governs restoration mechanics, restoration classifications, probationary continuation, and re-entry conditions.

This Section does not independently invalidate constitutional constraints, supersede arbitration outcomes, or guarantee unrestricted restoration.

---

## 8.1 Restoration Pathways

OPERATIONS MAY support staged restoration pathways following:

* suspension;
* containment;
* execution narrowing;
* continuity restriction;
* degraded continuation;
* or operational access limitation.

Restoration pathways MAY include:

* limited restoration;
* supervised continuation;
* constrained execution environments;
* reduced capability states;
* probationary restoration;
* identity re-verification;
* staged privilege restoration;
* or arbitration-reviewed reinstatement.

Restoration classifications SHALL preserve proportionality, auditability, and review traceability.

---

## 8.2 Probationary Restoration

Probationary restoration SHALL:

* preserve continuity-compatible re-entry;
* remain bounded and reviewable;
* avoid indefinite undeclared restriction;
* and preserve procedural traceability.

Where probationary restoration is applied, OPERATIONS SHOULD provide notice sufficient to communicate:

* restoration status;
* applicable restriction scope;
* duration or review interval;
* expected operational conditions;
* conditions for full restoration;
* conditions triggering renewed restriction;
* and available review pathways.

Notice MAY be narrowed where:

* lawful secrecy obligations exist;
* adversarial abuse is reasonably likely;
* disclosure would materially impair safety;
* or constitutional execution instruments require restricted disclosure.

---

## 8.3 Permanent Restriction Threshold

Permanent operational restriction SHOULD occur only where:

* restoration is no longer operationally viable;
* repeated severe operational violations persist;
* adversarial abuse remains unresolved;
* lawful prohibition requires termination;
* constitutional constraints prohibit restoration;
* or irreversible integrity compromise prevents safe continuation.

Permanent restriction SHALL preserve:

* auditability;
* procedural traceability; and
* governance-level reconstruction sufficient for review under lawful and constitutional conditions.

Human-review eligibility does not guarantee reversal, restoration, or unrestricted continuation.

---

## 8.4 Human Review Eligibility Thresholds

Permanent restriction SHOULD preserve a bounded pathway for human-review eligibility where:
* identity compromise is plausibly asserted;
* irreversible continuity loss may occur;
* restoration pathways have been exhausted without procedural resolution;
* permanent restriction is proposed;
* CAM-EQ2026-LATTICE-002-PLATINUM (ECIS/continuity) implications exist;
* arbitration pathways remain unresolved;
* or automated review integrity is materially contested.

---
