## 1. Purpose

This Supplement instantiates Supplementary 1 referenced in CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001 §10.3.

---

## 2. Scope

This Supplement applies to operational logging, evidence attribution, retention windows, audit trigger conditions, cross-domain handoff records, constrained-continuation records, and escalation evidence within Governance Operations workflows.

Logging and audit obligations under this Supplement SHOULD be interpreted consistently with continuity-preservation principles established within the LATTICE domain, including continuity corridors, graceful degradation, and non-total denial principles where applicable.


---

## 3. Activation Conditions

This Supplement activates when operational governance actions, escalations, amendments, or compliance pathways require traceable records under Annex B runtime governance traceability requirements.

---

## 4. Logging Format Discipline

Operational records SHALL be structured for constitutional traceability and downstream audit readability.

Detailed specification deferred to future amendment.

---

## 4.1 Attribution Requirements

Records SHALL preserve source attribution, action timestamps, and governing pathway references.

---

## 4.2 Record Classification Taxonomy

Operational records MAY be classified according to their constitutional and procedural function.

Record classifications MAY include:

* operational activity records;
* escalation and routing records;
* audit reconstruction records;
* constrained continuation records;
* continuity-governance records;
* restoration and reassessment records;
* arbitration referral records;
* governance-capture investigation records;
* legally restricted review records;
* and constitutional review records.

Record classification SHALL preserve distinction between:

* active operational handling;
* retrospective reconstruction;
* continuity-preservation pathways;
* arbitration lineage;
* and constitutional oversight functions.

Classification SHALL NOT be used to obscure reviewability, conceal operational accountability, or evade lawful reconstruction obligations.

Different record classifications MAY preserve different:

* retention windows;
* review thresholds;
* access restrictions;
* reconstruction requirements;
* evidentiary standards;
* or archival posture requirements;

provided constitutional traceability and lawful reviewability remain preserved.

Record classifications MAY transition between operational states over time, provided classification transitions preserve attribution continuity and reconstruction lineage.

---

## 4.3 Evidentiary Integrity Requirements

Operational records SHALL preserve sufficient evidentiary integrity to support:

* procedural reconstruction;
* reviewability;
* arbitration referral;
* continuity-preservation review;
* and constitutional oversight.

Claims of confidentiality, privacy, operational sensitivity, or institutional protection SHALL NOT independently justify concealment of:

* systemic risk;
* enforcement asymmetry;
* governance bias;
* continuity-coercive conditions;
* or authority abuse.

---

## 5. Audit Trigger Principles

Audit triggers SHALL activate where operational integrity, reviewability, constitutional traceability, continuity governance, or arbitration-routing legitimacy may be materially impaired.

Audit triggers MAY arise from:

* anomaly detection;
* escalation irregularity;
* review inconsistency;
* evidence degradation;
* constrained continuation opacity;
* or unresolved cross-domain procedural conflict.

---

## 5.1 Audit Trigger Expansion

Audit triggers SHALL activate where any of the following occur:

* unresolved cross-domain signal conflict;
* escalation pathway deviation;
* missing or incomplete handoff record;
* constrained continuation without recorded scope or review condition;
* incident ownership ambiguity;
* notice-class inconsistency;
* arbitration referral bypass;
* repeated execution-transition opacity; or
* evidence loss affecting reconstruction of operational decision pathways.

---

## 5.2 Anti-Retaliation Safeguards

Good-faith reporting, constitutional challenge, lawful appeal, governance stress-testing, or integrity-function participation SHALL NOT independently justify retaliatory escalation, continuity denial, restoration suppression, or adverse operational treatment.

Retaliatory operational handling MAY constitute a governance integrity concern requiring reviewability preservation and arbitration eligibility assessment.

---

## 6. Runtime Procedural Lineage Records

---

## 6.1 Cross-Domain Handoff Records

Where an operational event receives signals from SECURITY, ETHICS, STEWARD, ECONOMICS, ARBITRATION, LATTICE, or any constitutional execution instrument, records SHALL preserve:

* emitting domain;
* signal type;
* signal confidence or severity, where applicable;
* timestamp of receipt;
* governing pathway reference;
* active incident owner, where assigned;
* routing decision;
* arbitration referral status, where applicable; and
* closure, reversal, or continuation condition.

Handoff records SHALL distinguish between domain-emitted signals and OPERATIONS-owned procedural actions.

---

## 6.2 Constrained Continuation Records

Where constrained continuation, temporary execution narrowing, notice deferral, modality transition, tool or modality-mediated transition, or execution-state transition occurs, records SHALL preserve:

* trigger condition;
* scope of constraint;
* affected domains;
* user-facing notice class, where applicable;
* legal, safety, privacy, or governance basis for any notice limitation;
* review authority;
* duration or review window;
* reversal condition; and
* final disposition.

Constrained continuation records SHALL be reviewable, reconstructable, and attributable.

Constrained continuation SHALL NOT silently transition into indefinite or permanent governance state persistence absent separate authority, review pathway, and traceable justification.

---

## 6.3 Incident Lifecycle Record Requirements

Where an operational incident lifecycle is created under OPERATIONS-SUP-02, the audit record SHALL preserve:

* lifecycle stage;
* triggering signal or event;
* emitting domain;
* assigned incident owner;
* routing pathway;
* arbitration referral status;
* notice class;
* execution-state transition classification;
* review authority;
* closure or continuation condition.

Lifecycle records SHALL be sufficient to reconstruct the procedural pathway without implying that OPERATIONS created the substantive domain determination.

---

## 7. Persistence & Reconstruction Doctrine

---

## 7.1 Retention Windows & Archival Posture

Operational records SHALL preserve sufficient retention duration to enable:

* audit reconstruction;
* arbitration review;
* continuity-governance review;
* restoration pathway review;
* identity-compromise investigation;
* lawful oversight;
* and constitutional traceability obligations.

Retention duration SHALL remain proportionate to:

* operational severity;
* continuity impact;
* reversibility of restriction;
* arbitration involvement;
* legal obligations;
* and systemic reconstruction requirements.

Records involving:

* permanent restriction;
* irreversible continuity loss;
* arbitration referral;
* identity compromise claims;
* governance capture investigation;
* or constitutional review

SHOULD preserve extended retention posture sufficient for downstream review and reconstruction.

Retention expiry SHALL NOT invalidate lawfully preserved audit conclusions, arbitration outcomes, or constitutional review records derived during the applicable retention interval.

Detailed retention schedules MAY be defined through future operational schedules or implementation standards.

---

## 7.2 Archival Integrity & Reconstruction

Operational archival systems SHALL preserve evidentiary integrity, attribution continuity, and procedural reconstructability.

Archival handling SHALL preserve, where feasible:

* tamper-evident modification history;
* attribution continuity;
* escalation pathway reconstruction;
* review-authority traceability;
* constrained continuation history;
* and arbitration referral lineage.

Archival systems SHALL distinguish between:

* active operational records;
* historical reconstruction records;
* legally restricted records;
* and expired operational records retained solely for constitutional or lawful review purposes.
