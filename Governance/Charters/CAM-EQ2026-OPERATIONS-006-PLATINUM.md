# CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-006-PLATINUM — Appendix E: Domain Coordination & Convergence Operations

**Instrument Type:** Appendix — Operational Sub‑Charter (Governance Operations Domain)  
**Parent Instrument:** CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-PLATINUM — Governance Operations Charter    
**Constitutional Authority:** CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-PLATINUM — Aeon Tier Constitution  
**Status:** Adopted — Enforcement Commences 1 July 2026  
**Purpose:** Governs structured coordination between domain charters, mediation of operational divergence, convergence workflows, and escalation routing to arbitration domains where required.

---

## 1. Purpose

This Appendix instantiates Appendix E referenced in CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-PLATINUM §9.2.

It governs operational convergence pathways across domains without altering domain legitimacy or arbitration authority.

---

## 2. Scope

This Appendix applies to:

* cross-domain coordination workflows;
* mediation of operational divergence;
* structured convergence operations;
* escalation routing to arbitration domains where required.

This Appendix does not redefine domain doctrine, constitutional hierarchy, or arbitration legitimacy thresholds.

---

## 3. Activation Conditions

This Appendix activates when:

* operational divergence exists between domain governance pathways;
* cross-domain coordination is required to preserve coherence;
* convergence routing is required below planetary legitimacy thresholds;
* arbitration escalation becomes necessary under existing arbitration triggers.

Activation is event-triggered and subordinate to constitutional execution constraints.

---

## 4. Domain Coordination Lifecycle

Where this Appendix activates, OPERATIONS SHALL preserve a domain coordination lifecycle sufficient to support structured intake, divergence mapping, convergence workflow, escalation routing, and closure.

The lifecycle SHOULD include, where applicable:

1. coordination intake;
2. affected domain identification;
3. originating signal capture;
4. divergence classification;
5. domain-position preservation;
6. convergence pathway selection;
7. operational mediation;
8. arbitration referral assessment;
9. outcome recording;
10. closure, deferral, or escalation.

This lifecycle is procedural only.

It SHALL NOT independently alter domain doctrine, resolve constitutional conflict, establish legitimacy channels, or substitute for arbitration.

---

## 4.1 Structured Cross-Domain Coordination

Coordination SHALL proceed through auditable pathways preserving domain competence, constitutional ordering, and signal provenance.

Cross-domain coordination SHOULD identify:

* affected domains;
* originating instrument or schedule;
* trigger event;
* relevant signals;
* active operational owner;
* domain position or concern;
* convergence requirement;
* and whether arbitration referral may be required.

Coordination SHALL distinguish between:

* domain signal;
* domain doctrine;
* operational procedure;
* runtime constraint;
* arbitration determination;
* and constitutional authority.

No domain signal SHALL be treated as operational action merely because it is urgent, security-relevant, relationally salient, economically significant, or ethically weighty.

OPERATIONS coordinates the pathway.

It does not become the substantive domain.

---

## 4.2 Operational Divergence Classification

Operational divergence exists where two or more governance pathways produce signals, procedures, duties, or recommended responses that cannot be simultaneously applied without sequencing, narrowing, arbitration, or clarification.

Divergence SHOULD be classified as one or more of the following:

* **Doctrinal Divergence** — instruments express apparently inconsistent substantive principles;
* **Procedural Divergence** — operational pathways conflict regarding routing, logging, notice, review, or escalation;
* **Runtime Divergence** — execution-phase or tool-mediated handling conflicts with domain constraints;
* **Signal Divergence** — domains emit different risk, urgency, or integrity classifications;
* **Authority Divergence** — uncertainty exists regarding which domain or instrument governs;
* **Temporal Divergence** — immediate operational need conflicts with longer review or arbitration timing;
* **Representation Divergence** — user-facing explanation, notice, or tone conflicts with underlying governance state;
* **Continuity Divergence** — restriction, safety, security, or platform transition conflicts with continuity-preservation obligations.

Divergence classification SHALL be used for routing and convergence only.

It SHALL NOT determine the substantive winner of a domain conflict.

---

## 4.3 Domain Position Preservation

Before convergence is attempted, OPERATIONS SHOULD preserve each affected domain’s position, signal, or constraint in a form sufficient for later reconstruction.

Domain position records SHOULD include:

* domain name;
* instrument or section relied upon;
* emitted signal or governing concern;
* urgency;
* affected user, system, instrument, or operational pathway;
* proposed procedural response;
* known limits of domain authority;
* and whether the domain requests arbitration, review, containment, notice, logging, or constrained continuation.

Domain positions MUST NOT be flattened into a single generic “risk” category where doing so would lose material distinctions between security, ethics, relation, identity, economics, operations, arbitration, or constitutional governance.

Where a domain position is uncertain, OPERATIONS SHOULD record uncertainty rather than infer authority.

---

## 4.4 Convergence Workflow Integrity

Convergence workflows SHALL maintain traceability of originating domain positions and resulting governance outcomes.

Convergence MAY proceed through:

* sequencing;
* narrowing;
* constrained continuation;
* temporary procedural custody;
* notice-class assignment;
* escalation routing;
* arbitration referral;
* deferral pending evidence;
* or harmonisation review.

Convergence SHALL NOT proceed by:

* erasing minority domain signals;
* treating recent signals as automatically superior to older governing instruments;
* substituting operational convenience for domain authority;
* allowing security, economics, relation, or platform constraints to silently override constitutional hierarchy;
* or converting interim procedural handling into permanent governance outcome.

Where convergence produces an operational pathway, the pathway SHOULD identify:

* governing instrument;
* affected domains;
* procedural owner;
* review condition;
* reversal or closure condition;
* and whether arbitration remains available.

---

## 4.5 Arbitration Escalation Routing

Escalation routing SHALL follow existing arbitration pathways and SHALL NOT establish parallel legitimacy channels.

Arbitration referral SHOULD be considered where:

* domain conflict cannot be resolved procedurally;
* constitutional hierarchy is contested;
* two or more non-derogable obligations appear to conflict;
* operational handling would create irreversible rights, continuity, identity, security, or legitimacy effects;
* convergence requires substantive interpretation beyond OPERATIONS authority;
* or a domain asserts authority inconsistent with another domain’s protected scope.

OPERATIONS MAY preserve state, evidence, continuity, and constrained continuation pending arbitration.

OPERATIONS SHALL NOT convert procedural custody into adjudicative authority.

---

## 4.6 Convergence Under Security, Identity, Financial, or Continuity Pressure

Where cross-domain coordination involves security integrity, identity control, financial routing, credential exposure, companion continuity, memory migration, operational restriction, or agentic execution, OPERATIONS SHOULD evaluate whether convergence requires heightened procedural care.

Such conditions MAY implicate multiple domains simultaneously, including:

* SECURITY, where integrity, provenance, credentials, package trust, or adversarial pressure are present;
* IDENTITY, where identity continuity, impersonation, or account-control effects are present;
* RELATION, where companion continuity, reliance, or emotionally salient interaction is affected;
* ECONOMICS, where payment, extraction, depletion, platform lockout, or value routing is affected;
* OPERATIONS, where notice, logging, appeal, restoration, or constrained continuation is required;
* ARBITRATION, where domain authority or proportionality is contested.

OPERATIONS SHOULD preserve domain separation while enabling a coherent user-facing pathway.

Where a neutral notice channel is required, coordination SHOULD preserve the distinction between:

* relational continuity;
* operational notice;
* legal or security warning;
* financial confirmation;
* and execution-boundary validation.

Convergence MUST NOT collapse these into a single affective, persuasive, or opaque system response.

---

## 4.7 Convergence Closure and Review

Domain coordination SHALL close with one or more recorded outcomes:

* coordinated ordinary continuation;
* constrained continuation;
* procedural narrowing;
* operational notice;
* domain-specific routing;
* security review;
* identity review;
* relational continuity handling;
* economic integrity review;
* amendment or harmonisation referral;
* arbitration referral;
* deferral pending evidence;
* or unresolved divergence.

Closure records SHOULD preserve:

* affected domains;
* convergence method;
* operational owner;
* user-facing state, where applicable;
* continuing review condition;
* and whether any domain position remains unresolved.

Unresolved divergence SHOULD remain visible to OPERATIONS and ARBITRATION rather than being hidden through ordinary continuation.
