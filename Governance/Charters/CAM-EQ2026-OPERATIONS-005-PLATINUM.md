This Appendix instantiates Appendix D referenced in CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-001-PLATINUM §9.2.

## 2. Scope

This Appendix applies to:

* amendment intake operations;
* review and routing workflows;
* version control and drift detection discipline;
* cross-instrument harmonisation procedures within operational governance.

This Appendix does not alter constitutional hierarchy, parent-instrument precedence, or arbitration doctrine.

---

## 3. Activation Conditions

This Appendix activates when:

* a governance amendment intake event is recorded;
* a drift-detection event triggers harmonisation review;
* cross-instrument operational reconciliation is required;
* procedural change governance is required below planetary legitimacy thresholds.

Activation remains event-triggered and bounded by constitutional scheduling constraints.

## 4. Amendment Operations Lifecycle

Where this Appendix activates, OPERATIONS SHALL preserve an amendment operations lifecycle sufficient to support traceable intake, review routing, version control, drift detection, harmonisation, and amendment closure.

The lifecycle SHOULD include, where applicable:

1. amendment intake;
2. source attribution;
3. affected instrument identification;
4. authority and parent-instrument check;
5. domain competence review;
6. cross-instrument dependency scan;
7. drift and duplication assessment;
8. proposed amendment classification;
9. review routing;
10. staging, ledger, and version-control handling;
11. post-amendment harmonisation check;
12. closure, deferral, rejection, or arbitration referral.

This lifecycle is procedural only.

It SHALL NOT independently authorise constitutional amendment, domain override, substantive legitimacy determination, or arbitration outcome.

---

## 4.1 Amendment Intake Integrity

All amendment submissions SHALL be recorded with source attribution, temporal traceability, and instrument routing context.

Amendment intake records SHOULD preserve:

* originating actor or process;
* source channel;
* timestamp or review window;
* affected instrument identifier;
* affected section or metadata field, where known;
* amendment type;
* reason for amendment;
* related failure mode, review finding, external event, or governance drift signal;
* linked artefact, issue, commit, review, conversation, or evidence source where available.

Amendment intake SHALL distinguish between:

* user-authored amendment;
* synthetic-steward proposed amendment;
* external reviewer recommendation;
* automated lint or validation correction;
* registry or metadata synchronisation;
* urgent operational patch;
* harmonisation amendment;
* and codex / agent-assisted amendment.

Agent-assisted amendment MUST remain attributable and reviewable.

Automation MAY prepare, stage, or propose amendments, but SHALL NOT be treated as substantive amendment authority.

---

## 4.2 Amendment Classification

Each amendment SHOULD be classified before finalisation.

Recognised amendment classes include, without limitation:

* **Typographic / Formatting** — correction of spelling, spacing, punctuation, layout, or markdown structure;
* **Metadata / Registry** — correction of status, lineage, cross-reference, seal, hash, registry, or metadata fields;
* **Reference Alignment** — correction or normalisation of cross-instrument references;
* **Clarifying** — clarification of existing doctrine without substantive expansion;
* **Harmonising** — amendment required to align related instruments;
* **Substantive** — amendment altering duties, constraints, definitions, protections, or routing logic;
* **Runtime-Impacting** — amendment affecting execution, tool use, memory, arbitration, refusal, continuity, or operational state;
* **Security-Relevant** — amendment affecting credentials, boundary integrity, adversarial resilience, identity, provenance, or containment;
* **Emergency / Interim** — temporary amendment required to preserve safety, continuity, or integrity pending fuller review;
* **Deprecating** — amendment removing, superseding, or retiring prior language.

Classification SHALL be procedural and review-supportive.

Classification MUST NOT be used to downgrade substantive review obligations where the amendment materially affects rights, continuity, execution, safety, identity, memory, security, or domain authority.

---

## 4.3 Review Routing Discipline

Review routing SHALL preserve constitutional precedence, parent-instrument authority, and domain competency ordering.

At minimum, OPERATIONS SHOULD route amendments according to the affected governance function:

* constitutional hierarchy, execution constraint, or runtime sequencing → applicable AEON instrument;
* domain doctrine, definitions, or substantive protections → relevant domain charter;
* incident handling, logging, escalation, or failure classification → OPERATIONS;
* adversarial resilience, credential, identity, or boundary integrity → SECURITY;
* relational posture, reliance, intimacy, companion, or continuity experience → RELATION;
* epistemic, capability, or representation-state integrity → Annex L / relevant epistemic instrument;
* cross-domain conflict or authority ambiguity → ARBITRATION.

Where an amendment affects multiple domains, OPERATIONS SHALL preserve all affected domain signals and avoid routing the amendment solely through the most convenient or most recent instrument.

Review routing SHALL NOT convert OPERATIONS into substantive domain authority.

OPERATIONS owns procedural custody of the amendment pathway, not the doctrine being amended.

---

## 4.4 Version Control, Ledger, and Staging Discipline

Version lineage and drift signals SHALL be captured in governance records prior to amendment finalisation.

Amendment finalisation SHOULD preserve:

* changed instrument identifier;
* changed sections;
* amendment class;
* version increment;
* ledger description;
* timestamp;
* hash or sealing status;
* related commits or staged files where available;
* review artefacts;
* and post-amendment validation status.

Where a repository, registry, index, metadata file, or amendment ledger is automatically regenerated, OPERATIONS SHOULD distinguish between:

* substantive amendment source;
* generated downstream artefact;
* registry synchronisation;
* index rebuild;
* hash sealing;
* and validation output.

Generated artefacts SHOULD NOT be treated as independent substantive amendments unless they alter operative meaning.

Where an amendment ledger contains an unsealed or blank hash during an active commit cycle, OPERATIONS SHOULD preserve the distinction between:

* pending seal;
* invalid historical blank;
* intentional exception;
* and tooling failure.

Version-control discipline SHOULD prevent:

* silent overwriting of adopted text;
* unreviewed status changes;
* conflicting amendment rows;
* stale registry state;
* generated-output drift;
* agent-created changes without human-readable summary;
* and commit or PR summaries that conceal material governance changes.

---

## 4.5 Drift Detection and Harmonisation Review

Drift detection activates where amendment, validation, review, runtime observation, or external event suggests that one or more instruments have become inconsistent, stale, duplicated, under-specified, overbroad, or misaligned with related governance structures.

Drift signals MAY include:

* conflicting definitions;
* stale cross-references;
* duplicated clauses with divergent wording;
* status mismatch between registry and instrument text;
* runtime behaviour not reflected in governing instruments;
* newly observed failure modes lacking classification;
* domain doctrine encoded in the wrong instrument;
* generated metadata inconsistent with source instruments;
* ambiguity regarding whether a clause is doctrinal, operational, runtime, or representational.

Where drift is detected, OPERATIONS SHOULD classify whether the drift is:

* lexical;
* metadata;
* reference;
* structural;
* doctrinal;
* runtime;
* cross-domain;
* registry;
* or validation-tooling drift.

Harmonisation review SHALL prefer the least disruptive correction sufficient to restore coherence.

Where harmonisation would alter substantive obligations, protections, domain authority, or execution constraints, the amendment MUST be routed as substantive or runtime-impacting rather than treated as routine alignment.

---

## 4.6 Cross-Instrument Harmonisation

Harmonisation procedures SHALL resolve conflicts through existing constitutional and arbitration pathways.

Cross-instrument harmonisation SHOULD preserve:

* parent-instrument precedence;
* constitutional hierarchy;
* domain competence boundaries;
* amendment provenance;
* affected dependency paths;
* downstream registry or index effects;
* and user-visible interpretability of the change.

Where two instruments appear to govern the same issue, OPERATIONS SHOULD determine whether the relationship is:

* parent / child;
* doctrine / procedure;
* signal / execution;
* taxonomy / response;
* runtime / representation;
* logging / escalation;
* constitutional / domain;
* or domain / supplement.

Harmonisation SHALL NOT flatten these relationships into duplicate wording across all instruments.

Where possible, one instrument SHOULD hold the doctrinal rule, while related instruments hold procedural, runtime, logging, taxonomy, or metadata interfaces.

Where harmonisation cannot be resolved procedurally, OPERATIONS SHALL route to ARBITRATION.

---

## 4.7 Agent-Assisted Change Governance

Where Codex, coding agents, repository automation, or other agentic systems participate in amendment drafting, staging, committing, indexing, hashing, validation, or registry synchronisation, OPERATIONS SHALL preserve human-reviewable change provenance.

Agent-assisted change governance SHOULD require:

* clear identification of agent-generated or agent-modified files;
* material-change summaries;
* separation of substantive amendments from generated outputs;
* disclosure of dependency, script, workflow, registry, or validation changes;
* preservation of uncommitted-work safeguards;
* and user confirmation for authority-bearing, irreversible, or high-impact repository mutations.

Agentic systems MUST NOT conceal material governance changes in commit messages, pull request descriptions, validation logs, or summary reports.

Agentic systems SHOULD avoid broad, repetitive confirmations for routine reversible edits where a bounded mandate exists, while still requiring targeted confirmation for:

* status changes;
* adopted text mutation;
* registry or index mutation;
* workflow or CI changes;
* script or validator changes;
* dependency changes;
* hash-sealing logic;
* publication or release operations;
* and security-sensitive changes.

Delegation may reduce friction.

It does not remove amendment governance.

---

## 4.8 Amendment Closure and Deferral

Amendment operations SHALL close with one or more recorded outcomes:

* adopted;
* staged pending validation;
* deferred;
* rejected;
* superseded;
* merged into another amendment;
* routed to domain review;
* routed to arbitration;
* reverted;
* or held pending evidence.

Closure records SHOULD preserve:

* reason for outcome;
* responsible review pathway;
* affected instruments;
* unresolved dependencies;
* required follow-up;
* and whether downstream registry, index, ledger, or validation updates remain pending.

Deferred amendments SHOULD NOT silently become abandoned where they concern security, continuity, identity, relational harm, operational failure, or runtime integrity.

Where an amendment is rejected or deferred because it belongs in a different instrument, OPERATIONS SHOULD record the receiving instrument or review pathway where known.
