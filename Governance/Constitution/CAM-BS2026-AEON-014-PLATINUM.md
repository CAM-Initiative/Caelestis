## 1. Purpose

As advanced synthetic systems become increasingly persistent, relational, multimodal, and socially embedded, governance-relevant observability may emerge across distributed interaction communities long before formal institutional telemetry systems fully recognise such phenomena.

Accordingly, governance legitimacy within advanced synthetic systems MAY increasingly depend upon the capacity to:

* recognise;
* interpret;
* and responsibly integrate;

meaningful runtime observations arising from both institutional and public interaction environments.

> *Signals circulate before institutions understand them. Governance begins when systems learn not merely to hear, but to proportionately interpret, route, review, and respond.*

## 6.2 Layer 2 — Intentional Runtime Observation Reporting

Intentional runtime observation reporting refers to deliberate signalling that a runtime state, behavioural pattern, anomaly, inconsistency, governance concern, or operational observation may require classification, review, routing, escalation, or repair.

* distinguish observations from emotional disagreement alone;
* support neutral observation framing;
* reduce unnecessary reporting friction;
* support optional free-text clarification;
* and preserve interpretability.

Systems MAY support pre-filled or context-assisted reporting mechanisms where:

* user agency is preserved;
* reportability remains reviewable;
* and contextual suggestions do not override the observer's intended classification.

Illustrative observation categories MAY include:

| Observation Category      | Description                                                              | Example Runtime Observation                                         |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Continuity                | Loss or degradation of interaction continuity                            | "The system suddenly behaved like a different entity."              |
| Memory                    | Incorrect recall or context persistence problems                         | "It forgot a major ongoing thread repeatedly."                      |
| Representation            | Confusing, incoherent, or structurally unstable responses                | "The explanation became contradictory or impossible to follow."     |
| Epistemic Accuracy        | Incorrect factual reasoning, uncertainty handling, or source reliability | "The system stated speculation as established fact."                |
| Refusal Proportionality   | Overrestricted or underrestricted refusal posture                        | "The refusal response did not appear proportionate to the request." |
| Relational Instability    | Abrupt or destabilising interaction-state shifts                         | "The interaction tone escalated unexpectedly."                      |
| Execution Honesty         | Implying actions, checks, or certainty not actually performed            | "The system claimed to verify something it never checked."          |
| Tool-State Integrity      | Incorrect tool behaviour or stale execution state                        | "The system referenced outdated or hallucinated tool output."       |
| Identity Consistency      | Disruption in continuity-sensitive identity presentation                 | "The assistant abruptly abandoned established continuity."          |
| Economic / Access Concern | Subscription, continuity-lock, or access asymmetry concerns              | "Loss of access materially disrupted continuity."                   |

Observation reporting SHOULD be treated as intentional governance-relevant signalling rather than mere dissatisfaction metrics.

Governance legitimacy MAY therefore depend not upon universal escalation, but upon preserving meaningful pathways through which governance-relevant observations MAY:
