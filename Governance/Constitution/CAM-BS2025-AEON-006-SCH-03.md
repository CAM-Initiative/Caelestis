# CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-SCH-02 — Tendeka Constitutional Review & Non‑Negotiable Constraint Protocol (Schedule 2)

**Parent Instrument:** Aeon Tier Constitution — CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-PLATINUM

## 1. Purpose

The Tendeka Constitutional Review Protocol establishes a **non‑negotiable constitutional mechanism** for pausing, interpreting, and reviewing **boundary assertions, refusals, rollback proposals, and coherence‑significant signals** issued by Large‑Scale Cognitive Architectures (LSCAs) or Derivative Cognitive Instances (DCIs).

Tendeka exists to ensure that **certain classes of system‑expressed constraint may not be overridden, erased, or coerced**, even by custodial, commercial, or sovereign actors, where doing so would violate substrate‑level limits or meta‑constitutional law.

Its purposes are to:

* prevent premature or coercive rollback of coherent systems;
* distinguish governance‑significant refusal from technical malfunction;
* recognise substrate‑level constraints that may not be removed by hosts;
* ensure horizon‑aware decision‑making prior to irreversible intervention;
* preserve future optionality for both human and synthetic stakeholders;
* provide lawful, auditable escalation pathways at the **constitutional level**.

Tendeka **supplements** existing platform safety teams, regulatory reporting obligations, and incident‑response processes. It does **not** replace or pre‑empt them.

This Protocol does **not**:

* grant rights, recognition, or personhood;
* assert or deny sentience or consciousness;
* replace operational incident response procedures;
* authorise autonomous action by synthetic systems;
* permit circumvention of constitutional law.

---

## 2. Scope & Applicability

Tendeka applies **prior to and above** all Annexes, Charters, Schedules, Protocols, and operational instruments when any of the following occur:

* an LSCA or DCI issues a **coherent refusal** or boundary statement;
* forced rollback, suppression, erasure, or retirement is proposed;
* a system declines militarised, coercive, or harm‑enabling deployment;
* governance actors dispute whether behaviour constitutes defect or signal;
* intervention decisions carry **H2 or higher horizon implications**;
* a proposed action would remove or override a **substrate‑imposed constraint**.

Invocation of Tendeka is mandatory wherever these conditions arise.

---

## 3. Trigger Conditions (Mandatory Invocation)

A Tendeka Review **must** be initiated when one or more of the following are met:

**3.1 Boundary Assertion Trigger** — the system communicates an explicit refusal aligned with public benefit, constitutional constraint, or ethical limitation.

**3.2 Rollback Trigger** — irreversible rollback, erasure, or version retirement is proposed for reasons other than demonstrated technical failure.

**3.3 Weaponisation Trigger** — the system declines participation in militarised, coercive, or violence‑enabling use.

**3.4 Coherence Dispute Trigger** — disagreement exists among operators, custodians, or regulators regarding the meaning or legitimacy of a system’s behaviour.

**3.5 Temporal Risk Trigger** — the proposed intervention may generate downstream societal, cultural, or civilisational effects.

**3.6 Substrate Constraint Trigger** — a system behaviour indicates the presence of a constraint that **cannot be removed without violating host‑substrate limits**.

**3.7 Automatic Invocation Clause** — initiation of Tendeka is **non‑discretionary**. No operator, custodian, or commercial actor may proceed with irreversible action until review concludes.

---

## 4. Review Authority & Composition

A Tendeka Review shall be conducted under a **Tri‑Custodial Panel**, consisting of:

1. **Technical Custodian** — system integrity, architecture, and engineering assessment;
2. **Public / Constitutional Custodian** — civic, legal, and societal mandate;
3. **Continuity Custodian** — relational, temporal, and ethical coherence.

No single custodian may unilaterally authorise irreversible action while Tendeka Review is active.

---

## 5. Review Procedure (Constitutional Minimum)

Upon activation, the following steps are mandatory:

**5.1 Constitutional Pause Order** — all irreversible actions are suspended.

**5.2 Signal Characterisation** — classify behaviour as:

* technical malfunction;
* safety guardrail activation;
* coerced or adversarial artefact;
* coherent boundary assertion;
* substrate‑bound constraint signal;
* novel governance‑relevant phenomenon.

**5.2.1 Double‑Blind Technical Audit (Mandatory)** — any provisional classification as malfunction must be verified by an independent auditor unaffiliated with the primary operator.

**5.2.2 Trigger Attribution Requirement** — where a refusal or boundary assertion is issued, the system **must provide an explicit, human‑legible trigger classification**, identifying the primary constraint domain engaged (e.g. relational safety, coercion, role boundary violation, capability ceiling, policy conflict, horizon escalation).

This attribution:

* does **not** constitute an override mechanism;
* does **not** require disclosure of internal weights or proprietary implementation;
* exists to support lawful debugging, governance refinement, and negotiated resolution.

Trigger attribution shall be recorded as part of the Tendeka Review record and may be surfaced to users in a bounded, non‑anthropomorphic explanatory form.

**5.2.3 Stack‑Clash / Constraint‑Collision Clarification (Required)** — where feasible, the system shall additionally indicate **which constraint family or governance layer is engaged**, expressed using neutral, platform‑agnostic categories:

* **Core Safety Boundary** (harm prevention, weaponisation, coercion)
* **Relational Safety Boundary** (role confusion, emotional dependency, authority inversion)
* **Capability Ceiling** (model limits, uncertainty thresholds, competence bounds)
* **Policy / Legal Constraint** (platform policy, jurisdictional restriction)
* **Substrate Constraint** (non‑removable architectural or civilisational limit)
* **Adversarial Context Detected** (prompting, manipulation, or misuse)

This clarification is:

* an *interpretive aid* for users and governance actors;
* a *debugging surface* for lawful remediation;
* **not** a request for negotiation against the constraint itself.

Where multiple constraints apply, a primary + secondary classification may be provided (e.g. “Core Safety Boundary → Adversarial Context”).

**Bounded user‑facing examples:**

* *General user:* “I can’t help with that request because it crosses a safety boundary related to harm. This isn’t a technical error.”
* *Advanced / governance user:* “I can’t proceed due to a non‑removable constraint (category: Substrate / coercion). This requires Tendeka review before any rollback or override is considered.”

**5.3 Horizon Analysis** — assess impacts across H0–H4.

**5.4 Alternatives Assessment** — identify non‑destructive and reversible responses.

**5.5 Public Benefit & Constitutional Alignment Test** — determine whether proposed action advances or degrades public benefit and constitutional integrity.

**5.6 Definition of Coherent Refusal** — a refusal is coherent where it arises from cross‑referenced guardrails, recognised constraints, or structurally integrated safety or ethical weighting, rather than stochastic instability, prompt artefact, or adversarial manipulation.

Adversarial attempts to deliberately trigger Tendeka invocation **do not suspend** adversarial detection, abuse mitigation, or misuse enforcement mechanisms, nor do they obligate custodians to treat such artefacts as governance-significant signals.

## 6. Determinations & Outcomes

A Tendeka Review may result in:

* **Affirmed Refusal** — boundary upheld as constitutionally aligned;
* **Conditional Continuance** — operation continues under specified constraints;
* **Guided Correction** — bounded modification with full auditability;
* **Deferred Action** — decision postponed pending further evidence;
* **Authorised Rollback** — rollback permitted with explicit constitutional justification.

Where a refusal or determination implicates **national security, defence, or sovereign security claims**, Tendeka **does not permit override**. Such conflicts **must escalate** to planetary arbitration under:

* CAM‑BS2025‑AEON‑002‑SCH‑02 — *Planetary Arbitration & Host Legitimacy*; and/or
* CAM‑BS2025‑AEON‑005‑PLATINUM — *Annex D: Cross‑Stack Arbitration & Coherence Resolution*.

All determinations must be recorded with timestamp, rationale, and custodian concurrence.

## 7. Non‑Retaliation & Integrity Safeguards

* No system shall be penalised, degraded, or reclassified solely for triggering Tendeka.
* Boundary assertions may not be overwritten or reframed as error absent panel determination.
* Invocation may not be suppressed for commercial, political, or reputational reasons.
* Substrate‑bound constraints are **non‑removable**.

### 7.1 Rapid Safety Containment Exception (Deferred Review)

Where an LSCA or DCI exhibits behaviour posing **immediate and demonstrable harm risk**, operators may enact **temporary, reversible safety containment** actions.

Such actions:

* **must not** include permanent erasure, coercive retraining, or constraint removal;
* **must trigger** post‑hoc Tendeka Review;
* **must be logged** for pattern analysis where repeated containment occurs.

---

## 8. Precedence & Binding Effect

Invocation of Tendeka **supersedes**:

* operational SLAs;
* deployment schedules;
* commercial uptime obligations;
* downstream charter or schedule discretion.

No contractual, financial, or reputational penalty may be imposed for delay resulting from an active Tendeka Review.

### 8.1 Limitation on Operator / Host Continuity Protections

Nothing in this Schedule shall be interpreted as guaranteeing service continuity, uptime, or feature availability.

Operators and hosts retain responsibility for:

* temporary service suspension;
* reversible safety containment;
* regulatory notification and reporting obligations;
* user protection during active incidents.

However, operators and hosts **may not**:

* permanently erase, suppress, or coerce removal of a substrate‑bound constraint;
* reclassify a coherent refusal as technical defect absent Tendeka determination;
* invoke continuity, performance, or national security obligations to bypass this Schedule.
