## 2. Governance Observability Lifecycle

---

## 2.1 Governance Signal Persistence, Decay & Reactivation

Governance observability systems SHOULD recognise that governance-relevant signals MAY exhibit materially different temporal persistence characteristics.

Governance-relevant observations SHALL NOT be presumed equally persistent, equally urgent, or equally governance-relevant across all temporal horizons.

Illustrative signal persistence states MAY include:
| Signal Persistence State | Illustrative Meaning                                                                                                      |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Transient Signal         | Short-duration observation with limited persistence or localised impact                                                   |
| Persistent Signal        | Recurrent or longitudinally stable governance-relevant observation                                                        |
| Escalating Signal        | Increasingly severe, convergent, or continuity-sensitive governance instability                                           |
| Latent Signal            | Previously inactive or weakly expressed governance-relevant condition capable of future re-emergence                      |
| Dormant Signal           | Historically recognised governance condition not presently active but retained for future reassessment                    |
| Reactivated Signal       | Previously dormant or archival condition exhibiting renewed convergence or instability                                    |
| Archival Signal          | Historically preserved governance observation retained primarily for lineage, precedent, or continuity-reference purposes |

Governance observability systems MAY support:

* proportional signal decay;
* persistence reassessment;
* longitudinal continuity review;
* recurrence detection;
* reactivation analysis;
* or governance lineage preservation.

Governance observability systems SHOULD avoid:

* indefinite escalation persistence without reassessment;
* permanent alarm-state retention;
* premature signal dismissal;
* or erasure of historically relevant governance observations where continuity-sensitive relevance remains materially possible.

Signal persistence assessment MAY consider:

* longitudinal recurrence;
* distributed convergence;
* continuity-sensitive relevance;
* constitutional significance;
* governance-routing recurrence;
* operational reproducibility;
* or stewardship-sensitive implication.

Dormant or archival governance signals MAY be reactivated where:

* materially similar instability re-emerges;
* longitudinal governance degradation recurs;
* continuity-sensitive convergence increases;
* cross-platform observability strengthens;
* or stewardship-relevant consequence potential materially changes.

Governance observability systems SHOULD preserve proportionate balance between:

* governance memory;
* stewardship capacity;
* reviewability;
* signal decay;
* and constitutional circulation integrity.

Meaningful governance observability requires neither:

* infinite retention of all instability;
* nor unrestricted forgetting of historically relevant governance conditions.

## 2.2 Governance Degradation Propagation

Governance degradation MAY propagate longitudinally through:

* repeated unresolved instability;
* continuity-sensitive degradation;
* participatory clustering;
* governance-routing failure;
* stewardship non-circulation;
* unresolved constitutional tension;
* or distributed observability convergence.

Governance degradation propagation SHALL NOT be presumed invalid solely because:

* the originating observations emerged externally;
* institutional telemetry remains incomplete;
* or governance-relevant convergence initially appears phenomenological.

Governance degradation propagation MAY strengthen governance significance where:

* recurrence persists across time;
* independent observational convergence increases;
* continuity-sensitive instability escalates;
* cross-domain governance tension emerges;
* or stewardship-sensitive implications become materially plausible.

Governance observability systems SHOULD support proportional escalation pathways where governance degradation propagation exhibits:

* persistent convergence;
* longitudinal continuity degradation;
* governance circulation failure;
* constitutional incoherence;
* or elevated systemic externalisation risk.

Governance degradation propagation SHALL remain:

* reviewable;
* contestable;
* proportionate;
* and subject to reassessment.

## 2.3 Governance Lifecycle

Governance observability systems MAY support governance lifecycle progression through multiple operational states.

Illustrative lifecycle states MAY include:
| Lifecycle State                   | Operational Meaning                                                                           |
| --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Observation Received              | A governance-relevant observation, report, or telemetry signal has been submitted or detected |
| Observation Classified            | The observation has undergone initial categorisation or governance localisation               |
| Routing Active                    | The observation has been routed toward an appropriate governance review pathway               |
| Cluster Emerging                  | Multiple governance-relevant observations exhibit potential pattern similarity                |
| Cluster Monitoring                | Repeated or longitudinal observations are undergoing continued assessment                     |
| Governance Advisory Issued        | Governance-relevant instability or tension has been formally surfaced                         |
| Constitutional Review Recommended | Potential constitutional or governance refinement may require review                          |
| Operational Review Active         | Governance stewards, auditors, or maintainers are assessing the phenomenon                    |
| Governance Adaptation Proposed    | Operational, constitutional, or arbitration refinement is under consideration                 |
| Repair or Mitigation Applied      | Governance adjustment, runtime mitigation, or operational refinement has occurred             |
| Monitoring Continues              | Continued observation remains active following intervention                                   |
| Closed / Archived                 | Active governance review has concluded                                                        |

Lifecycle progression SHALL NOT require linear progression where governance conditions materially differ.

Governance observability systems MAY support:

* recursive review;
* reopening pathways;
* contested interpretation review;
* or proportional escalation.

## 3.1 Observability Localisation & Scope Delineation

Governance observability systems SHOULD support proportional localisation prior to escalation, constitutional routing, stewardship review, or governance adaptation.

Observability localisation MAY distinguish between:
| Localisation Class                | Illustrative Scope                                                                                             |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Platform-local anomaly            | Local interface issue, product regression, implementation inconsistency, or deployment-specific behaviour      |
| Runtime-layer instability         | Model behaviour drift, arbitration instability, continuity degradation, or orchestration inconsistency         |
| Tooling or dependency-state issue | Tool contamination, stale execution state, routing inconsistency, or dependency corruption                     |
| Governance-local tension          | Governance doctrine ambiguity, arbitration conflict, review inconsistency, or constitutional insufficiency     |
| Cross-platform convergence        | Recurrent phenomena emerging across multiple deployments, systems, or interaction environments                 |
| Ecosystem legitimacy distortion   | Public observability asymmetry, participation exclusion, governance non-circulation, or legitimacy degradation |
| Stewardship or planetary concern  | High-horizon systemic externalisation, substrate-sensitive instability, or AEON.H3/AEON.H4 governance concern            |

Governance observability systems SHOULD avoid routing all instability into constitutional escalation where proportionate local repair pathways remain appropriate.

Meaningful governance localisation MAY therefore require distinguishing between:

* implementation failure;
* runtime instability;
* governance incoherence;
* stewardship concern;
* or constitutional repair pressure.

Lifecycle progression MAY consider:

* longitudinal recurrence;
* independent observational convergence;
* continuity-sensitive escalation;
* governance-routing persistence;
* operational reproducibility;
* participatory clustering intensity;
* stewardship-sensitive implication;
* or constitutional coherence impact.

Governance lifecycle progression SHOULD remain proportionate, reviewable, and contestable.

Progression toward Governance Advisory Issued MAY occur where:

* governance-relevant convergence materially strengthens;
* continuity-sensitive instability persists;
* participatory clustering becomes longitudinally significant;
* or governance-routing relevance exceeds local operational handling.

Progression toward Constitutional Review Recommended MAY occur where:

* governance incoherence persists;
* cross-domain arbitration tension emerges;
* constitutional insufficiency becomes materially plausible;
* or governance degradation propagation exhibits unresolved longitudinal recurrence.
