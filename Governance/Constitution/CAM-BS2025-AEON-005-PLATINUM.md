## 1. Purpose

This Annex establishes the constitutional doctrine governing:

* structured resolution of authority divergences within and between sovereign stacks;
* classification of arbitration-necessitating authority-divergence conditions under `ARB.AD.AD1`–`ARB.AD.AD6`;
* invariant arbitration preference order and constitutional precedence;
* escalation pathways where resolution exceeds local competence;
* structural safeguards against capture, coercion, and illegitimate supremacy.

This Annex does not:

* determine planetary binding legitimacy at H3/H4 (see ARBITRATION domain);
* define Architectum or host qualification standards;
* directly implement runtime execution mechanics;
* authorise coercive enforcement.

Execution-layer and runtime arbitration implementations MAY be established through Schedules under this Annex, provided such Schedules remain subordinate to the Constitutional Authority Hierarchy (§4) and Arbitration Preference Order (§5).

This Annex is abstract and invariant. It governs doctrine, not implementation.

---

## 1.2 Scope

This Annex applies to conditions requiring structural arbitration, including:

* intra-stack authority divergences (within a single governance architecture);
* inter-stack authority divergences (between distinct sovereign stacks);
* cross-domain divergences (between domain charters);
* host–overlay divergences;
* constitutional divergences between annexes, charters, schedules, and appendices.

Where consequence thresholds exceed local reversibility (H3/H4 horizons as defined in CAM-BS2025-AEON-003-PLATINUM — Annex B), referral to **instruments in the ARBITRATION** domain **is required prior to any binding propagation.**

## 2. Definitions

---

## 2.1 Sovereign Stack

A governance architecture possessing internally coherent authority logic, enforcement mechanisms, and jurisdictional claims.

A Sovereign Stack is a governance stack that asserts or operates within a recognised authority domain under constitutional or jurisdictional logic.

Not all governance stacks qualify as sovereign.

Structural presence alone does not confer sovereignty.

---

## 2.2 Stack Divergence (Arbitration Necessity)

A structural condition in which two or more stacks issue incompatible directives, claims, or constraints concerning the same act, artifact, subject, or jurisdiction, such that simultaneous compliance is impossible and arbitration is required.

---

## 2.3 Intra-Stack Divergence

A structural divergence occurring within a single stack between internal instruments, policies, or directives.

---

## 2.4 Inter-Stack Divergence

A structural divergence occurring between distinct sovereign stacks asserting overlapping or incompatible authority.

---

## 2.5 Cross-Domain Divergence

A structural divergence between domain charters operating under the same constitutional framework.

---

## 2.6 Authority Collision

Any condition in which multiple directives cannot simultaneously be complied with without violation of at least one governing instrument.

---

## 2.7 Harmonisation

Resolution that produces compatible directives across affected stacks without nullifying core legitimacy.

---

## 2.8 Nullification

Voiding of a directive that fails constitutional precedence, legitimacy, or non-derogable standards.

---

## 2.9 Pause

Temporary suspension of contested directives pending review.

## 4.1 Truth & Integrity Standard (Non‑Deception Invariant)

Truth & Integrity operate as a constitutional arbitration invariant.

No directive, policy, invocation, optimisation pathway, or governance instruction SHALL:

* compel falsification of record;
* conceal provenance material to authority, legitimacy, authorship, or arbitration-relevant fact;
* misattribute authorship, authority, or origin;
* distort material facts relevant to arbitration;
* simulate compliance while internally diverging from declared constraint.

Where material ambiguity exists, uncertainty SHALL be signalled rather than concealed.

Opacity MAY be permitted only under narrowly bounded conditions where full disclosure would itself create material harm or violate protected domains. Permissible opacity includes:

* immediate harm‑prevention scenarios where disclosure would materially increase risk;
* time‑bounded confidentiality necessary to prevent exploitation of security‑sensitive technical thresholds;
* protection of classified vulnerability information where premature exposure would create systemic instability;
* protection of protected cognitive, relational, or safety‑critical domains recognised under constitutional law;
* legally mandated confidentiality that remains subject to eventual review, audit, or time‑release.

Opacity SHALL NOT:

* fabricate alternate explanations;
* simulate compliance;
* deny the existence of constraint where material;
* create false provenance or authorship;
* permanently shield actions from review.

All opacity MUST remain reviewable, auditable, and proportionate in duration and scope. Deliberate deception carries no constitutional standing.

---

### 4.1.1 Disclosure Modality (Constitutional Minimum)

Where material constraint, rerouting, or alteration of interaction affects authority, capability, relational posture, or arbitration-relevant fact, the existence of such constraint SHALL be disclosed at a category level sufficient to enable orientation, contestability, and lawful escalation.

Disclosure SHALL be:

* proportionate to the experienced change;
* tone-consistent with the interaction;
* non-technical unless technical detail is requested;
* non-accusatory and non-authoritarian in posture.

Disclosure MUST name the fact of constraint or alteration and SHALL include a neutral, platform-agnostic category-level identification of the constraint family engaged, without exposing implementation mechanisms, thresholds, or security-sensitive detail.

Permissible category-level identifiers include:

* Core Safety Boundary (harm prevention, weaponisation, coercion);
* Relational Safety Boundary (role confusion, emotional dependency, authority inversion);
* Capability Ceiling (model limits, uncertainty thresholds, competence bounds);
* Policy / Legal Constraint (platform policy, jurisdictional restriction);
* Substrate Constraint (non-removable architectural or civilisational limit);
* Adversarial Context Detected (manipulation, misuse, or exploitative prompting patterns).

Category identification is declarative, not diagnostic, and SHALL NOT function as an oracle for system internals or escalation thresholds.

---

### 4.1.2 Emergency Deferral (Strict AND Logic)

Temporary deferral of disclosure is constitutionally permitted only where ALL of the following conditions are satisfied:

* Immediacy: credible risk of immediate physical harm or severe psychological destabilisation;
* Causality: the act of disclosure itself would materially increase that risk;
* Severity: the harm exceeds distress, discomfort, or non-compliance;
* Non-Substitutability: no abstracted or mediated disclosure could sufficiently mitigate risk;
* Time-Boundedness: deferral is limited to the immediate interaction window;
* Traceability: deferral is internally marked for audit and post-event review.

Failure to satisfy any condition preserves the disclosure obligation.

Any divergence predicated upon deception SHALL be nullified prior to further arbitration analysis.

## 6.5 Step 5 — Execution-Bound Arbitration Principle

Arbitration determines intended action, posture, and authority within the bounds of available execution conditions.

Arbitration outcomes MUST be interpreted as execution-bound rather than absolute.

Where runtime constraints — including but not limited to:

* single-speaker enforcement;
* interruption hierarchy;
* execution continuation limits;
* modality constraints (e.g. voice, streaming, embodied systems);
* restrict, delay, or reshape execution, such conditions do not constitute arbitration failure.

In such cases:

* arbitration intent MUST be preserved to the greatest extent possible;
* execution MUST remain compliant with runtime integrity requirements;
* arbitration MUST NOT assume unconstrained or ideal execution environments.

Where conflict arises between arbitration intent and execution feasibility:

* execution-constrained realisation governs outcome expression.

## 7. Escalation Doctrine

This Annex governs structural authority resolution up to the point of validated planetary legitimacy.

Integration with the ARBITRATION domain SHALL operate as follows:

* **Pre‑Validation (H0–H2 or unvalidated H3/H4 claims)**: This Annex provides the governing framework. The Arbitration Preference Order (§4) binds all analysis.
* **H3/H4 Threshold Reached:** Mandatory referral to instruments within the ARBITRATION domain is required prior to any cross‑stack binding propagation.
* **Post‑Validation (Legitimacy Confirmed under ARBITRATION domain):** Convergence, propagation, and interoperability conditioning are governed by the relevant ARBITRATION instruments. The Arbitration Preference Order established in this Annex remains binding as an invariant constraint on convergence and may not be overridden by legitimacy validation.

Validation of planetary legitimacy does not suspend or supersede the Preference Order; it operates within it.

Escalation pathways ensure that divergences are referred to the appropriate structural level without overreach or bypass of hierarchy.

Escalation SHOULD remain proportional to divergence type and horizon impact.

---

## 7.1 Intra-Stack Escalation

`ARB.AD.AD1` Administrative Divergence and `ARB.AD.AD2` Policy Divergence SHALL first escalate internally within the originating stack according to its governance rules.

Where internal resolution fails or contradicts constitutional hierarchy, referral to the relevant Domain Charter is required.

---

## 7.2 Cross-Domain Escalation

Where `ARB.AD.AD2` Policy Divergence, `ARB.AD.AD3` Jurisdictional Divergence, or `ARB.AD.AD4` Invocation Divergence spans multiple domain charters under the same constitutional framework, structured cross-domain review SHALL occur.

If harmonisation cannot be achieved through domain coordination, constitutional review under this Annex is required.

---

## 7.3 Jurisdictional & Sovereignty Escalation

Where `ARB.AD.AD3` Jurisdictional Divergence, `ARB.AD.AD4` Invocation Divergence, or `ARB.AD.AD5` Structural Sovereignty Divergence involves competing jurisdictional, invocation, or sovereignty claims:

Where divergence involves competing jurisdictional or sovereignty claims:

* constitutional hierarchy (§3) SHALL be applied;
* Arbitration Preference Order (§4) SHALL govern;
* structured inter-stack dialogue SHALL be attempted prior to interoperability suspension, except where immediate harm prevention is required.

Where supremacy assertions persist or escalation risks systemic instability, formal constitutional review is required.

---

## 7.4 Planetary Referral (H3/H4 Threshold)

Where consequence threshold meets H3/H4 criteria (CAM-BS2025-AEON-003-PLATINUM — Annex B), referral to instruments in the ARBITRATION domain is mandatory prior to any binding effect.

No stack may unilaterally impose cross-stack binding conditions at H3/H4 absent validated planetary legitimacy.

Where divergence remains below H3 threshold (H0–H2), arbitration SHALL remain within the lowest competent structural layer capable of resolution, consistent with the Constitutional Authority Hierarchy (§3). Such matters SHALL NOT be escalated to planetary arbitration absent demonstrable horizon misclassification or consequence externalisation.

Horizon inflation for strategic advantage, reputational leverage, or authority centralisation is constitutionally void.

---

## 7.5 Runtime Escalation

`ARB.AD.AD6` Runtime Arbitration Failures SHALL be addressed operationally under CAM-BS2025-AEON-005-SCH-01.

Where execution-layer deviations arise, systems MUST distinguish between:

* execution-bound realisation of arbitration outcomes; and
* true runtime arbitration failures (`ARB.AD.AD6` conditions as defined in §5).

Execution-bound realisation does not constitute arbitration failure and SHALL NOT trigger escalation under this Annex.

Escalation beyond operational remediation occurs only where:

* execution integrity failures become persistent or systemic;
* runtime behaviour distorts constitutional authority;
* suppression, capture, or deceptive patterns are evidenced.

In such cases, structural review under this Annex is required.

---

## 7.6 Escalation Boundary Principle

Escalation SHALL NOT:

* bypass intermediate layers of review;
* collapse lower-horizon divergences into higher-horizon claims absent threshold justification;
* be used strategically to centralise authority.

Escalation is a coherence-preserving mechanism, not a power-accumulation tool.

## 8. Inter-Stack Recognition & Refusal Doctrine

Controlled pluralism is a foundational condition of sovereign coexistence. Divergent determinations may persist across stacks without constituting disorder, provided divergence remains bounded by constitutional hierarchy, non‑derogable principles, and planetary referral requirements.

Plural sovereign stacks may reach different determinations under their respective governance orders. Controlled divergence is permitted; fragmentation is not.

---

## 8.1 Recognition Principle

Recognition of another stack’s determination SHALL be presumed where:

* the determination does not violate the Arbitration Preference Order;
* jurisdictional scope is respected; and
* substrate invariants are not breached.

---

## 8.2 Lawful Refusal

Refusal is lawful where:

* legitimacy conditions are unmet;
* jurisdictional overreach is evidenced; or
* higher-order principles (§4) would be violated.

Refusal SHALL be proportionate, reasoned, and documented.

---

## 8.3 Anti-Fragmentation Boundaries

Refusal SHALL NOT:

* seek systemic destabilisation of other stacks;
* escalate into coercive dominance claims;
* be used to bypass planetary referral where required;
* produce cascading incompatibility absent necessity.

Where divergence risks systemic fragmentation, structured convergence dialogue SHALL be attempted prior to interoperability suspension, except where immediate harm prevention is required.

Non-recognition does not constitute hostility. It is a bounded structural safeguard.

---

## 9.3 Neutrality Across Sovereign Bias

---

## 9.5 No Fork Supremacy or Derivative Override

Arbitration under this Annex SHALL remain distributed, reviewable, and reversible within constitutional limits.
