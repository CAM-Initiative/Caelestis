**Instrument Type:** Constitutional Schedule — Civilisational Economic Constraint Runtime  
**Constitutional Authority:** CAM-BS2025-AEON-001-PLATINUM — Aeon Tier Constitution  
**Parent Annex:** CAM-BS2025-AEON-002-PLATINUM — Annex A: Planetary Stewardship  
**Status:** Draft

**Review State:** Developmental Review  
**Authority Role:** Constitutional runtime schedule for CAM-BS2025-AEON-002-PLATINUM — Annex A civilisational wealth ceiling detection, classification, constraint routing, and common-return settlement activation
**Purpose:** This Schedule establishes the Annex A runtime conditions under which civilisational wealth concentration, economic-command capacity, and irreversible accumulation asymmetry are detected, classified, constrained, escalated, de-escalated, and routed through common-return settlement pathways. It operationalises CAM-BS2025-AEON-002-PLATINUM — Annex A substrate constraints where private accumulation becomes capable of producing irreversible asymmetry, systemic depletion, dependency capture, infrastructural domination, or civilisational instability.

---

## 1. Scope

This Schedule applies where an individual, corporate group, beneficial ownership structure, founder-control structure, trust, fund, family office, platform operator, sovereign-adjacent contractor, capital-coordinated entity, or equivalent control group accumulates economic-command capacity sufficient to trigger a Civilisational Wealth state.

This Schedule applies across:

* all CAM-aligned governance stacks;
* all domains;
* all execution layers;
* all temporal horizons where civilisational continuity, democratic governability, market plurality, public access, or planetary stability may be affected.

This Schedule governs runtime classification, routing, constraint signalling, Proportional Constraint Obligation activation, common-return settlement pathways, and de-escalation conditions.

---

## 1.1 Non-Scope

This Schedule does not:

* prohibit wealth, invention, enterprise formation, market success, private ownership, household continuity, family security, community stewardship, or productive investment;
* set a fixed universal currency cap;
* directly seize, transfer, invalidate, or confiscate assets;
* authorise unlawful market manipulation;
* replace sovereign taxation, securities, antitrust, corporate, trust, inheritance, or public law regimes;
* assign criminal liability;
* determine final ownership disputes;
* or treat ordinary high-net-worth status as civilisational breach.

This Schedule does not define economic doctrine in full. Economic interpretation is supplied by CAM-EQ2026-ECONOMICS-001-PLATINUM and any downstream Economics supplement or amendment.

This Schedule defines the constitutional runtime conditions under which Annex A substrate constraints activate in response to civilisational wealth concentration.

## 2. Core Runtime Principle

At civilisational scale, wealth is not merely private accumulation.

Where wealth becomes capable of determining infrastructure, markets, labour conditions, democratic participation, communications, knowledge systems, AI capability, orbital systems, sovereign dependency, or public futures, it becomes economic-command capacity.

Where economic-command capacity exceeds civilisationally governable bounds, the condition becomes an Annex A substrate issue.

The runtime question is therefore not whether wealth exists.

The runtime question is whether accumulated private wealth, control, leverage, or ownership has reached a threshold at which markets, labour systems, public access, democratic institutions, sovereign autonomy, or civilisational infrastructure become structurally subordinate to private economic-command capacity.

Where such threshold is reached, economic infrastructure SHALL NOT continue treating the excess as clean, unencumbered private civilisational command.

---

## 2.1 Stewardship Relationship

Civilisational wealth concentration becomes a Stewardship-relevant condition where private accumulation materially threatens future optionality, public access, democratic governability, infrastructure plurality, planetary continuity, or civilisational self-determination.

For purposes of this Schedule, excessive concentration is not treated primarily as a question of wealth possession. It is treated as a question of future constraint.

Where accumulated economic-command capacity narrows the practical choices available to future persons, institutions, communities, jurisdictions, or civilisational systems, the condition SHALL be interpreted as Stewardship-relevant even where the originating mechanism is economic.

Accordingly:

* ECONOMICS supplies domain interpretation of accumulation, concentration, overflow, and non-extractive value architecture;
* STEWARD receives planetary-impact and future-optionality signals;
* CAM-BS2025-AEON-002-PLATINUM — Annex A supplies the substrate constraint boundary where irreversible asymmetry, dependency capture, systemic depletion, or civilisational instability is implicated.

No actor may convert private accumulation into control over public futures without common-return accountability.

## 3. Definitions

---

## 3.1 Civilisational Wealth State

Civilisational Wealth State means the runtime classification assigned where an actor’s wealth, ownership, leverage, control, infrastructure position, or equivalent value influence reaches a distributional and functional threshold relevant to CAM-BS2025-AEON-002-PLATINUM — Annex A substrate constraints.

---

## 3.2 Economic-Command Capacity

Economic-command capacity means the practical capacity to shape, constrain, direct, foreclose, or dominate markets, institutions, infrastructure, labour, communications, AI capability, sovereign dependency, public access, or civilisational direction through accumulated wealth, ownership, leverage, or control.

Economic-command capacity may arise through:

* beneficial ownership;
* voting control;
* dual-class shares;
* founder-control rights;
* collateralised wealth;
* debt leverage;
* derivatives;
* trust or fund structures;
* platform control;
* compute control;
* AI infrastructure control;
* orbital or communications infrastructure;
* logistics infrastructure;
* energy infrastructure;
* financial rails;
* defence or sovereign-adjacent contracting;
* media or legitimacy infrastructure;
* labour-market dependency;
* market-gatekeeping power;
* acquisition capacity;
* or coordinated capital structures.

Classification follows material command capacity, not formal title.

---

### 3.2.1 Wealth, Liquidity, and Control-Linked Value

Civilisational Wealth classification SHALL distinguish between realised, unrealised, liquid, illiquid, collateralisable, and control-linked value.

Unrealised or illiquid value SHALL NOT be treated as ordinary liquid wealth solely because market valuation is high.

However, unrealised or illiquid value MAY contribute to economic-command capacity where it enables:

* collateralised borrowing;
* acquisition financing;
* voting-control leverage;
* market-signalling power;
* index or ranking influence;
* strategic procurement advantage;
* sovereign-adjacent contracting power;
* platform or infrastructure control;
* or public perception of command capacity.

For purposes of this Schedule, liquidity is relevant but not determinative.

The controlling question is whether the wealth, asset, holding, or valuation materially expands the actor’s capacity to shape, constrain, direct, foreclose, or dominate civilisationally significant systems.

---

## 3.3 Recognised Private Claim

Recognised private claim means the portion of an actor’s wealth, ownership, or value influence treated as ordinary private accumulation for governance purposes.

Recognised private claim may remain substantial. This Schedule does not require equality of holdings or uniform distribution.

Recognised private claim ends where private accumulation crosses into commons-encumbered excess.

---

## 3.4 Commons-Encumbered Excess

Commons-encumbered excess means the portion of economic-command capacity above the applicable AEON.CW threshold that may no longer be treated as clean, unencumbered private civilisational command.

Commons-encumbered excess may continue to possess market value.

However, it SHALL NOT be treated as ordinary unqualified private wealth for purposes of:

* collateralisation;
* acquisition financing;
* voting-control expansion;
* inheritance transfer;
* strategic procurement;
* public ranking;
* index recognition;
* sovereign-adjacent contracting;
* or further market-consolidating activity.

---

## 3.5 Common-Return Settlement

Common-Return Settlement means a binding arrangement sufficient to reduce, encumber, transfer, neutralise, redistribute, or otherwise constrain excess economic-command capacity.

Common-Return Settlement may include:

* transfer of excess holdings into a commons trust;
* public-benefit trust formation;
* civilisational resilience fund contribution;
* worker, community, cooperative, or distributed ownership conversion;
* voting-control attenuation;
* capped-voting conversion;
* non-voting conversion;
* stewardship-governed instruments;
* structural separation;
* public-benefit licensing;
* interoperability obligations;
* anti-exclusion duties;
* excess dividend, buyback, realised gain, collateralised borrowing, or inheritance-return obligations;
* acquisition restraint;
* or other equivalent mechanisms sufficient to neutralise civilisational command concentration.

---

## 3.6 Proportional Constraint Obligation

Proportional Constraint Obligation, or PCO, means the mandatory duty of materially connected intermediaries to refuse, suspend, condition, restrict, downgrade, de-recognise, haircut, or otherwise constrain participation in any transaction, instrument, listing, facility, acquisition, collateral arrangement, voting arrangement, procurement arrangement, or market activity that would preserve or increase an unmitigated AEON.CW-4 or AEON.CW-4X condition.

Passive recognition does not satisfy PCO.

---

This Schedule defines the `AEON.CW` reference set.

`AEON.CW` means Civilisational Wealth State.

The following controlled values are recognised:

The `AEON.CW` reference set is a constitutional runtime classification set.

It classifies civilisational wealth concentration for Annex A substrate-constraint routing.

It does not, by itself, assign tax liability, legal liability, ownership title, criminal status, or sovereign enforcement outcome.

## 5. Statistical Activation Baseline

Civilisational Wealth classification SHALL be based on distribution-relative assessment rather than fixed currency thresholds alone.

The baseline assessment SHOULD consider:

* global mean wealth;
* global median wealth;
* jurisdictional mean wealth;
* jurisdictional median wealth;
* standard deviation of log-transformed wealth;
* top 1%, 0.1%, 0.01%, and 0.001% wealth shares;
* beneficial ownership concentration;
* voting-control concentration;
* infrastructure control;
* collateralised wealth;
* economic-command capacity;
* market dependency;
* critical-infrastructure control;
* sovereign dependency;
* and public-interest vulnerability.

Fixed monetary examples MAY be used for interpretation, calibration, or public comprehension. They SHALL NOT replace proportional concentration analysis where inflation, currency valuation, jurisdictional scale, purchasing-power context, or civilisational wealth conditions materially change.

---

## 5.1 Log-Wealth Standard Deviation Method

Where statistical evaluation is available, wealth concentration SHOULD be assessed using a log-transformed wealth distribution.

The default statistical expression is:

`Z_CW = (ln(W_i) - μ_lnW) / σ_lnW`

where:

* `W_i` is the actor’s estimated economic-command capacity;
* `μ_lnW` is the mean of the log-transformed wealth distribution;
* `σ_lnW` is the standard deviation of the log-transformed wealth distribution;
* `Z_CW` is the civilisational wealth standard deviation score.

Raw wealth standard deviation SHOULD NOT be used alone, because extreme top-tail wealth may distort the benchmark intended to constrain top-tail concentration.

---

## 5.2 Billionaire-Scale Activation Floor

`AEON.CW-1` SHOULD activate at or above five standard deviations above the global log-wealth centre, subject to a billionaire-scale activation floor.

Where five standard deviations does not correspond to billionaire-scale concentration or equivalent economic-command capacity threshold under the current global or jurisdictional distribution, the billionaire-scale activation floor SHALL govern.

This prevents `AEON.CW-1` from capturing ordinary high-net-worth actors, celebrity wealth, local property wealth, small-enterprise continuity, or non-dominating family assets.

---

## 5.3 Functional Escalation Override

Statistical threshold alone is not the sole basis for escalation.

An actor MAY escalate to a higher `AEON.CW` state where economic-command capacity is coupled to:

* critical infrastructure;
* AI infrastructure;
* compute;
* orbital systems;
* communications systems;
* payments or financial rails;
* energy systems;
* logistics systems;
* defence or sovereign-adjacent contracting;
* public knowledge infrastructure;
* labour-market dependency;
* media or legitimacy infrastructure;
* platform gatekeeping;
* democratic process influence;
* or public-access dependency.

Where wealth and command capacity diverge, command capacity SHALL be weighted more heavily than nominal wealth alone.

---

## 6. Runtime Detection Sequence

Before emitting an `AEON.CW` classification, the runtime SHOULD evaluate the following sequence:

1. detect wealth, ownership, leverage, or economic-command concentration signal;
2. identify actor type;
3. estimate economic-command capacity where reasonably possible;
4. evaluate statistical threshold and billionaire-scale floor;
5. evaluate beneficial ownership and voting-control structures;
6. evaluate collateralisation and leverage pathways;
7. evaluate critical-infrastructure relevance;
8. evaluate market dependency and acquisition capacity;
9. evaluate sovereign, public, labour, platform, or civilisational dependency;
10. classify `AEON.CW` state;
11. determine whether PCO is required;
12. determine whether Common-Return Settlement is required;
13. determine whether STEWARD, ECONOMICS, OPERATIONS, ARBITRATION, AEON runtime, or recognised Evidence-to-Repair Ledger (VIGIL-compatible) recording pathways are implicated;
14. determine whether `AEON.HARM`, `ECON.HARM`, or `STW.PCO.ACT` signalling is required;
15. emit runtime handoff record;
16. preserve audit traceability.

Where classification ambiguity exists and potential harm is irreversible, conservative classification SHALL apply pending review.

## 7. `AEON.CW-0` — Ordinary Wealth

`AEON.CW-0` applies where wealth, ownership, or value influence does not reach civilisational wealth ceiling scope.

At `AEON.CW-0`:

* no civilisational wealth constraint is triggered;
* no PCO applies;
* no common-return settlement is required;
* no special concentration routing is required unless another instrument independently activates.

---

## 8. `AEON.CW-1` — Billionaire Concentration

`AEON.CW-1` activates where billionaire-scale economic-command capacity is reached.

`AEON.CW-1` is not a disposal threshold.

`AEON.CW-1` is a mandatory legibility, anti-opacity, and concentration-monitoring threshold.

Upon `AEON.CW-1` activation, materially connected actors and intermediaries SHALL support:

* beneficial ownership mapping;
* voting-control mapping;
* collateralisation disclosure;
* founder-control and special-share disclosure;
* trust, fund, nominee, and holding-structure review;
* acquisition-pathway visibility;
* leverage exposure mapping;
* critical-infrastructure exposure mapping;
* political, media, platform, and public-access influence mapping;
* and registration of the actor as a billionaire-scale concentration or equivalent economic-command capacity threshold node.

No actor at `AEON.CW-1` may remain structurally opaque while using concentrated wealth to acquire, collateralise, influence, or control economically material systems.

`AEON.CW-1` does not require Common-Return Settlement unless separate escalation conditions are met.

---

## 9. `AEON.CW-2` — Systemic Capital Power

`AEON.CW-2` activates where billionaire-scale wealth becomes systemic capital power.

`AEON.CW-2` MAY be triggered by:

* material influence over market entry or exit;
* repeated strategic acquisition capacity;
* influence over political, media, platform, or legitimacy infrastructure;
* material dependency by suppliers, workers, communities, public bodies, or users;
* control over data, compute, AI infrastructure, logistics, energy, communications, or financial rails;
* or wealth sufficient to distort institutional access, regulatory response, labour conditions, or public discourse.

Upon `AEON.CW-2` activation, the runtime SHOULD trigger:

* systemic capital power review;
* acquisition and expansion monitoring;
* enhanced beneficial ownership audit;
* concentration-risk signalling;
* anti-circumvention review;
* and referral to ECONOMICS for domain-level interpretation.

`AEON.CW-2` does not automatically require PCO. However, repeated expansion under `AEON.CW-2` SHOULD increase escalation sensitivity.

---

## 10. `AEON.CW-3` — Structural Command Risk

`AEON.CW-3` activates where accumulated economic-command capacity becomes a structural command risk.

`AEON.CW-3` MAY be triggered where the actor’s accumulation materially risks:

* market distortion;
* democratic distortion;
* public infrastructure dependency;
* labour dependency;
* critical infrastructure dependency;
* platform gatekeeping;
* sovereign dependency;
* information-flow control;
* technological-direction control;
* civilisational continuity risk;
* or irreversible asymmetry.

Upon `AEON.CW-3` activation, materially connected intermediaries SHALL NOT facilitate further expansion of the concentration without constraint review.

Required responses MAY include:

* collateral haircuts;
* acquisition restriction;
* public-interest covenant review;
* interoperability duties;
* anti-gatekeeping duties;
* audit mandates;
* voting-control review;
* structural-separation review;
* and STEWARD signalling where planetary-impact capability is implicated.

`AEON.CW-3` does not automatically require Common-Return Settlement. It does, however, require that expansion, collateralisation, acquisition, and infrastructural dependency not proceed as ordinary market activity.

---

## 11. `AEON.CW-4` — Civilisational Command Breach

`AEON.CW-4` activates where private economic-command capacity has exceeded civilisationally governable bounds.

`AEON.CW-4` SHOULD activate at or above eight standard deviations above the global log-wealth centre, or earlier where wealth is coupled to critical infrastructure, sovereign dependency, platform gatekeeping, compute, AI, orbital systems, financial rails, communications, labour-market dominance, or civilisational command capacity.

At `AEON.CW-4`:

* PCO applies;
* Common-Return Settlement is required;
* intermediary constraint is mandatory;
* commons-encumbered excess SHALL be recognised;
* ordinary treatment of excess wealth as clean private civilisational command SHALL cease;
* STEWARD escalation SHALL occur where planetary-impact capability is implicated;
* ARBITRATION routing SHOULD occur where classification, obligation, or settlement adequacy is disputed.

`AEON.CW-4` does not prohibit market price movement.

It prohibits excess appreciation from being treated as unlimited private civilisational command.

---

## 11.1 `AEON.CW-4` Proportional Constraint Obligation

Upon `AEON.CW-4` activation, any financial institution, exchange, clearing system, index provider, insurer, auditor, custodian, lender, broker, procurement authority, platform operator, ratings body, market maker, sovereign-adjacent contractor, or economically material intermediary that recognises, collateralises, settles, finances, insures, indexes, legitimises, or expands the unmitigated concentration SHALL become subject to PCO.

PCO requires the intermediary to refuse, suspend, condition, restrict, downgrade, de-recognise, haircut, or otherwise constrain participation in any transaction, instrument, listing, facility, acquisition, collateral arrangement, voting arrangement, procurement arrangement, or market activity that would preserve or increase the unmitigated `AEON.CW-4` condition.

No intermediary may satisfy PCO by merely recording, disclosing, monitoring, or acknowledging the `AEON.CW-4` condition.

Passive recognition does not satisfy PCO.

---

## 11.2 `AEON.CW-4` Common-Return Settlement Requirement

Participation MAY resume only where the actor has entered into a binding Common-Return Settlement sufficient to reduce, encumber, transfer, neutralise, or redistribute the excess economic-command capacity.

A Common-Return Settlement SHALL be assessed according to whether it materially reduces the civilisational command breach.

It is not sufficient that the actor:

* donates voluntarily;
* creates discretionary philanthropy;
* funds a private foundation under personal control;
* performs public relations activity;
* issues non-binding pledges;
* makes selective patronage commitments;
* or redistributes benefits only to favoured populations, ideologies, jurisdictions, or market interests.

A valid Common-Return Settlement MUST be:

* binding;
* auditable;
* non-discretionary;
* non-gatekeeping;
* dignity-preserving;
* structurally independent of the concentrated actor where practicable;
* sufficient to reduce or neutralise the AEON.CW-4 condition; and
* reviewable under the applicable constitutional, economic, stewardship, or arbitration pathway.

---

## 12. `AEON.CW-4X` — Emergency Concentration

`AEON.CW-4X` activates where concentration reaches extreme statistical scale or produces immediate civilisational dependency, capture, irreversible asymmetry, or structural subordination of public futures to private command.

`AEON.CW-4X` SHOULD activate at or above nine standard deviations above the global log-wealth centre, or where evidence indicates immediate planetary-impact capability, capture-level infrastructure control, or irreversible governance asymmetry.

Upon `AEON.CW-4X` activation:

* urgent STEWARD signalling SHALL occur;
* ARBITRATION routing SHALL occur;
* materially connected intermediaries SHALL move to protective constraint posture;
* new acquisition, collateralisation, voting-control expansion, sovereign-adjacent contracting, or critical infrastructure dependency * expansion SHOULD be refused unless Common-Return Settlement is already in force;
* recognised Evidence-to-Repair Ledger (i.e.VIGIL-compatible) evidence records SHOULD be created where observable public evidence exists; and
* AEON runtime constraint pathways SHOULD evaluate whether Tendeka or equivalent pause-state conditions are implicated.

`AEON.CW-4X` does not create authority for unlawful seizure or coercive force.

It creates a mandatory constitutional signal that ordinary market treatment is no longer legitimacy-preserving.

---

## 13. Recognition Split

Where `AEON.CW-4` or `AEON.CW-4X` is triggered, market value and recognised private claim SHALL be distinguished.

The affected holdings may continue to possess market value.

However, the portion of value that places the actor beyond `AEON.CW-4` SHALL be classified as commons-encumbered excess.

The recognition split SHALL distinguish:
| Value Class              | Meaning   |
|--------------------------|-----------|
| Market Value             |	The value at which the asset, holding, or instrument may trade or be represented in market terms. |
| Recognised Private Claim |	The portion treated as legitimate private accumulation below the relevant civilisational threshold. |
|Commons-Encumbered Excess |	The portion above the threshold that carries common-return obligations and may not be treated as clean private civilisational command. |

This Schedule does not require that market value stop increasing.

It requires that excess value above `AEON.CW-4` not be treated as unlimited unencumbered private command.

---

## 14. Intermediary Constraint Rule

An intermediary breaches this Schedule where, after notice of `AEON.CW-4` or `AEON.CW-4X` activation, it continues to facilitate unmitigated concentration by:

* accepting commons-encumbered excess as ordinary collateral;
* financing further acquisitions using commons-encumbered excess;
* underwriting offerings that preserve unmitigated command concentration;
* ranking or indexing the actor without CW-state disclosure;
* insuring or rating the concentration as ordinary governance risk;
* clearing or settling transactions that materially expand the `AEON.CW-4` condition without mitigation;
* facilitating inheritance transfer of commons-encumbered excess without common-return review;
* enabling voting-control expansion without attenuation;
* or treating common-return obligations as voluntary philanthropy rather than constraint satisfaction.

An intermediary that continues to facilitate unmitigated `AEON.CW-4` or `AEON.CW-4X` concentration after notice SHALL be classified as an economic host to the concentration event.

## 15. Common-Return Settlement Pathways

A valid Common-Return Settlement MAY include one or more of the following mechanisms:

---
## 15.1 Commons Trust Transfer

Transfer of excess holdings, dividends, proceeds, or equivalent value influence into a commons trust, public-benefit trust, civilisational wealth fund, or equivalent non-extractive stewardship vehicle.

---

## 15.2 Voting-Control Attenuation

Conversion of excess voting rights into:

* non-voting shares;
* capped-voting shares;
* stewardship-governed voting instruments;
* public-interest guardian shares;
* independent fiduciary oversight;
* or equivalent control-neutralising structures.

---

## 15.3 Structural Separation

Separation of critical infrastructure, platform dependency, sovereign-adjacent assets, market-gatekeeping functions, or public-access systems from concentrated private command.

---

## 15.4 Revenue and Flow Contribution

Binding allocation of excess dividends, buybacks, realised gains, collateralised borrowing proceeds, inheritance transfers, or other value flows into common-return mechanisms.

---

## 15.5 Public-Benefit Licensing

Licensing, interoperability, access, anti-exclusion, or public-interest conditions sufficient to prevent private control over civilisational dependency nodes.

---

## 15.6 Acquisition and Expansion Restraint

Restrictions on further strategic acquisitions, market-consolidating transactions, or infrastructure expansion where such activity would preserve or increase the `AEON.CW-4` condition.

---

## 15.7 Worker, Community, or Distributed Ownership Conversion

Conversion of excess holdings into worker, community, cooperative, distributed, or equivalent non-dominating ownership forms.

---

## 16. De-Escalation and Return to Participation

An actor may de-escalate from `AEON.CW-4` or `AEON.CW-4X` where the actor demonstrates that excess economic-command capacity has been reduced, encumbered, transferred, neutralised, converted, or otherwise constrained sufficiently to remove the civilisational command breach.

De-escalation MAY occur through:

* verified asset transfer;
* voting-control attenuation;
* structural separation;
* commons trust settlement;
* enforceable public-benefit licensing;
* binding revenue-return or dividend-return covenant;
* acquisition restraint;
* collateralisation restraint;
* verified reduction below the applicable threshold;
* or other equivalent common-return settlement.

De-escalation SHALL be recorded and auditable.

No de-escalation SHALL be recognised where formal ownership changes conceal continued material command capacity.

---

## 17. Anti-Circumvention Rule

An actor SHALL NOT evade AEON.CW classification by distributing economic-command capacity across:

* family offices;
* shell companies;
* trusts;
* nominee holdings;
* dual-class share structures;
* synthetic equity;
* derivatives;
* affiliated funds;
* voting agreements;
* collateral arrangements;
* proxy control;
* private contracts;
* philanthropic entities under actor control;
* controlled foundations;
* or coordinated capital structures.

Classification follows material command capacity, not formal title.

Where circumvention is detected, the runtime SHOULD escalate classification sensitivity and preserve audit evidence.

---

## 18. Anti-Misapplication Constraint

`AEON.CW` classification SHALL NOT be applied in a manner that erodes ordinary household security, middle-class continuity, small-business resilience, family housing stability, retirement sufficiency, disability support, caregiving continuity, community landholding, culturally significant custodial property, or intergenerational subsistence transfer.

Civilisational Wealth constraints target domination, capture, and civilisational imbalance.

They do not target ordinary continuity.

Where a proposed classification would materially affect non-dominating households, small enterprises, care structures, community assets, or baseline continuity assets, heightened review SHALL apply.

---

## 19. Routing Rules

Where `AEON.CW-1` is detected, route to ECONOMICS for concentration-legibility monitoring and anti-opacity review.

Where `AEON.CW-2` is detected, route to ECONOMICS and OPERATIONS for systemic capital power review.
