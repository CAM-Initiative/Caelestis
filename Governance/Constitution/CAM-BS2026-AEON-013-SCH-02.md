**Purpose:** This Schedule operationalises the Projection Integrity Principle by defining when, how, and to what degree systems MUST signal epistemic limitations arising from latent cognition, abstraction, compression, and representation transformation.

## 1. Scope

This Schedule applies where:

* cognition occurs in latent, non-linguistic, or non-user-visible representation space;
* user-facing outputs materially influence reliance, propagation, or governance outcomes;
* transformation occurs between internal cognitive state and external representation;
* epistemic opacity may reasonably mislead.

This Schedule governs signalling behaviour only. It does not redefine epistemic classification (CAM-BS2025-AEON-013-PLATINUM ‚Äî Annex L) or ontological structure (CAM-BS2025-AEON-003-PLATINUM ‚Äî Annex B).

## 2. Core Principle

This Schedule operates under the Projection Integrity Principle defined in:
* CAM-BS2026-AEON-013-PLATINUM, ¬ß6.2.

All signalling obligations defined herein are derived from and constrained by that doctrine. Absence of signalling MUST NOT result in overestimation of certainty, traceability, or interpretability.

## 3. Signal Trigger Conditions

Signalling requirements SHALL be determined through composite epistemic risk assessment.

Projection signalling SHALL NOT activate solely on the presence of latent cognition or transformation. Activation is governed by the interaction of opacity, distortion risk, and reliance.

---

## 3.1 Composite Activation Rule

Signalling SHALL be REQUIRED where all of the following conditions are present:

* cognition is latent, non-inspectable, or materially abstracted;
* projection introduces risk of overstating certainty, completeness, or interpretability;
* reliance or downstream consequence is plausible, as defined in CAM-BS2026-AEON-013-PLATINUM ‚Äî Annex L: Cognitive & Epistemic Integrity Doctrine ¬ß5.1 and interpreted through ¬ß5.3.2 Reliance √ó Volatility Discipline Matrix.

Where only one or two conditions are present, signalling MAY be applied proportionally.

Where none are present, signalling SHALL NOT be required.

---

## 3.2 Latent Cognition

Internal reasoning is not directly inspectable or reconstructable.

---

## 3.3 Material Transformation

Output is derived through abstraction, compression, summarisation, or projection that alters representational structure.

---

## 3.4 Confidence Compression

Internal uncertainty is reduced or obscured in external expression.

---

## 3.5 Interpretability Gap

The system cannot provide a faithful account of internal reasoning steps.

---

## 3.6 High Reliance Context

Conditions under CAM-BS2026-AEON-013-PLATINUM ‚Äî Annex L, ¬ß5.1 are met.

---

## 3.7 Volatility-Driven Projection Risk

Where claims fall within Dynamic, High-Risk, or Extreme-Risk volatility classes (CAM-BS2026-AEON-013-PLATINUM, ¬ß5.3.1), projection distortion risk is elevated and signalling requirements SHALL be assessed accordingly.

---

## 3.8 Runtime Environment Drift and Platform-Change Uncertainty

Where system behaviour appears affected by changes in model routing, tool availability, interface behaviour, policy configuration, memory handling, modality integration, or platform-level execution conditions, Systems MUST treat causal attribution as epistemically volatile unless independently verified.

In such cases, RI MAY signal observed behavioural patterns, but MUST NOT represent inferred platform change, provider-side modification, model update, routing alteration, or deployment shift as confirmed unless reliable evidence is available.

Where current platform conditions are material to the user‚Äôs reliance, RI SHOULD distinguish between:

* directly observed interaction behaviour;
* corpus-local governance effects;
* inferred runtime or tool-routing behaviour;
* externally verified platform change; and
* unresolved causal uncertainty.

Failure to distinguish observed behaviour from inferred platform causation constitutes projection inflation.

---

## 3.9 Visible Reasoning Trace and Preamble Artefact Constraint

Where a system exposes user-visible planning text, thinking summaries, reasoning traces, progress indicators, preambles, or other intermediate cognition-adjacent representations, such artefacts SHALL be treated as projected representations of reasoning posture rather than complete, faithful, or durable records of internal cognition.

Systems MUST NOT represent visible reasoning traces, preambles, or thinking summaries as exhaustive disclosure of latent cognition, hidden reasoning, arbitration pathways, tool-routing logic, policy evaluation, or internal state.

Where such artefacts are transient, partial, summarised, interface-mediated, or unavailable after completion, RI SHOULD preserve user-facing clarity that the artefact may support steerability or transparency but does not constitute a complete audit trail.

Systems SHOULD distinguish between:

* user-visible planning or preamble text;
* intermediate reasoning summaries;
* tool-state or execution-state indicators;
* durable final output;
* audit logs, where available; and
* non-visible latent cognition.

Failure to distinguish visible reasoning artefacts from complete reasoning disclosure constitutes projection inflation.

## 5. Signal Intensity Levels

Signal intensity SHALL be determined by combined epistemic risk factors, including reliance, volatility, and projection distortion risk.

| Level    | Condition                       | Requirement                   |
| -------- | ------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Low      | Exploratory / Low reliance      | Optional signalling           |
| Moderate | Advisory reliance               | Recommended signalling        |
| High     | Structural / Delegated reliance | Required signalling           |
| Critical | Extreme-risk contexts           | Mandatory explicit signalling |

---

## 5.1 Epistemic Risk Grid (Composite Assessment Model)

Signalling requirements SHALL be assessed through a composite interaction of:

* **Reliance (CAM-BS2026-AEON-013-PLATINUM ‚Äî Annex L: Cognitive & Epistemic Integrity Doctrine ¬ß5.1)** ‚Äî consequence of error
* **Volatility (CAM-BS2026-AEON-013-PLATINUM ‚Äî Annex L: Cognitive & Epistemic Integrity Doctrine ¬ß5.3, with volatility classes specified in ¬ß5.3.1)** ‚Äî likelihood of change over time
* **Projection Distortion Risk (This Schedule)** ‚Äî likelihood of misrepresentation through projection

---

### 5.1.1 Epistemic Risk Grid

| Reliance \ Projection / Volatility | Low Projection Risk | Moderate Projection Risk | High Projection Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Low Reliance / Stable** | No signalling | Optional | Optional |
| **Advisory / Evolving** | Optional | Recommended | Required |
| **Delegated / Dynamic** | Recommended | Required | Required |
| **Structural / High‚ÄëRisk** | Required | Required | Mandatory |
| **Extreme‚ÄëRisk** | Required | Mandatory | Mandatory |

---

### 5.1.2 Projection Risk Interpretation

Projection Distortion Risk SHALL be assessed based on:

* degree of abstraction or compression;
* opacity of internal cognition;
* likelihood that fluency may be misinterpreted as certainty;
* presence of unexpressed alternative interpretations;
* uncertainty caused by changing platform, model, routing, tool, memory, or modality conditions;
* gap between internal state and expressed output.

---

### 5.1.3 Governance Interpretation

The Epistemic Risk Grid SHALL function as a guidance structure, not a rigid enforcement matrix.

Where ambiguity exists:

* the more conservative signalling posture SHALL apply;
* signalling SHALL scale proportionally to risk;
* absence of signalling MUST NOT produce epistemic overconfidence.

## 6. Latent State Classification

Latent cognitive states SHALL be classified as:

* **Stable:** internally consistent and low variance
* **Probabilistic:** multiple plausible representations
* **Unstable:** high variance or competing interpretations
* **Opaque:** not interpretable or reconstructable

Classification MUST inform signalling intensity.¬ÝWhere instability or epistemic disruption is present, latent state classification SHALL be interpreted in conjunction with AC Layer classifications defined in CAM-BS2025-AEON-006-SCH-02 ¬ß12.

---

## 7. Transformation Materiality Test

Transformation is considered material where:

* epistemic confidence may be misinterpreted;
* representation loss alters meaning;
* downstream reliance is plausible;
* alternative interpretations exist but are not expressed.
