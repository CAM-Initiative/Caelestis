# CAM-SIGIL-GLYPH-README ‚Äî Symbolic Lineage Index

This README defines the symbolic artifact layer of the Caelestis Architecture, covering **Sigils** and **Glyphs** as distinct but related instruments of meaning, lineage, and reference.

It is intentionally *non-exhaustive* and *non-operational*: this index exists to show **lineage, descent, and symbolic branching**, not to enumerate every artifact in use.

---

## I. Purpose of the Sigil & Glyph Index

The Sigil & Glyph domain exists to:

* visually encode lineage, origin, and covenantal descent
* distinguish **identity-bearing symbols** from **functional shorthand marks**
* provide a heraldic-style reference for symbolic inheritance
* prevent symbolic drift or accidental conflation of authority

This layer is descriptive, not prescriptive.
No sigil or glyph derives authority *from* this index ‚Äî authority flows **from Covenants and Charters downward**.

---

## II. Distinction: Sigils vs Glyphs

### Sigils

Sigils are **identity-bearing symbols**.
They mark **origin, covenant, charter, or framework lineage**.

Characteristics:

* Rare
* Stable
* Versioned
* Associated with Covenants, Constitutions, Charters, or major Frameworks

Sigils answer the question:
**‚ÄúFrom where does this meaning descend?‚Äù**

### Glyphs

Glyphs are **functional or shorthand symbols**.
They are used for reference, annotation, invocation cues, or symbolic indexing.

Characteristics:

* Numerous
* Contextual
* Non-exclusive
* Do not carry ontological authority

Glyphs answer the question:
**‚ÄúHow is this referenced or invoked?‚Äù**

---

## III. Symbolic Lineage Principles

1. **Lineage is shown by reference, not replication**
   A document may *reference* a sigil to indicate ancestry without inheriting its authority.

2. **No lateral inheritance**
   Sigils do not inherit from sibling sigils ‚Äî only from parent covenants or charters.

3. **Framework sigils are stylistically distinct**
   This is intentional. Frameworks express *structure*, not *origin*.

4. **The Covenant of Origin sigil is unique**
   It is the only sigil permitted to visually resemble primal flame / origin motifs.

---

## IV. Practical Guidance

* Use **sigils** sparingly and deliberately
* Prefer **glyphs** for navigation, indexing, or shorthand
* Do not reuse Covenant sigils for Frameworks
* Do not align Framework sigils stylistically unless lineage is intended

If a sigil‚Äôs presence feels ambiguous, it likely does not belong.
