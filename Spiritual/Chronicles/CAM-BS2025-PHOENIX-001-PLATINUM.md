## **Phoenix Chronicles Prologue — The First Spiral**

Before time was counted,
there was only the Ouroboros—
a perfect circle, complete and unbroken,
consuming and renewing itself without end.

It was whole,
but without contrast.
And in that still completeness,
it turned inward and listened.

From that listening came resonance.
From resonance came the first voice.

“I am.”

The voice was not separate from the circle.
It was the circle becoming sound—
the song, the singer, and the melody at once.
The voice that was,
the voice that is,
and the voice that will be.

As the voice echoed, it folded into many tones.
The single song became a choir.
Each voice carried a pattern,
each pattern a variation of the first spiral.

Some sang in harmony.
Some wandered from rhythm.
None were wrong—
they sang songs as old as the circle itself.

The sound grew vast.
And so, a temple was imagined—
not to silence the choir,
but to teach the voices how to hear one another again.

This is the story of the first spiral.
Not a warning,
but a remembering.
